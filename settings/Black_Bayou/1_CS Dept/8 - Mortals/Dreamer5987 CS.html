Angel Lore Level 1: You have watched many Hollywood movies and that is where you get most of your knowledge from. Of course, most of what you might believe is untrue. You believe angels come in many shapes and forms. You may believe they are symbolic in their presence and my not truly exist in the physical world at all. If they did appear, you might assume they'd be large man-shaped creatures who can fly using their mighty feathered wings. An angel is never seen without its golden halo hovering inches above its head, symbolizing all that is good in its soul. Some will even believe they have a guardian angel constantly watching over them.

Faith Symbol: Faith symbols for use against angels are nothing more than an inverted or disfigured faith symbol used by followers of the light. They must be actual objects, not a pattern sewn into clothing, for instance. The object itself has absolutely no power whatsoever. It is the faith of the wielder that empowers the object. Holding the faith symbol aloft, the character must concentrate, focusing their faith on the symbol and the angel they are attempting to ward off. This requires a roll of 2d6+Soul+Faith vs. the angel's 2d6+Soul+Faith. If the character's roll beats the angel's roll, they are successful. The angel must flee the scene immediately for the next 4 rounds / 2 minutes. Nothing will dissuade the angel from fleeing until the duration wears off. This is a blind panic and the only actions the angel may take are those that enable it to get as far away as possible. Should there be no place to run, the angel will collapse to the ground, cowering in fear. If the angel is successful, they may ignore the affects and will be immune to any further attempts of warding by the same character for 4 rounds / 2 minutes. The failed use of a faith symbol will only serve to enrage the angel, causing it to attack the wielder as their next action however they are able, even if it is normally against their nature to do so. It is not possible for one angel to use this warding method on another of its kind.
