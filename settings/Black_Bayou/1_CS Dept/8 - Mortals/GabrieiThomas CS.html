Background

Gabriel was born in 1972 in New Orleans. However, he grew up in France and lived there most of his life. He was raised by his mother Monique, his father having died when he was 2 years old. His father had been a vampire slayer like his father before him, and thus the mantle was to be passed on to Gabriel. But being that Gabriel's father died when he was so young, there was not alot of training that could be done. Gabriel's mother, however, had been able to teach him the basics about protection against vampires, and the different methods of destroying them, as Gabriel's father had taught her in order to protect her. Being a notorious slayer, Gabriel's father had constantly been hunted by vampires, and he knew that Monique would be in constant danger from these vampires. Ironically, Monique survived, but it was Gabriel's father that was eventually taken down by a gang of the undead.
Gabriel wanted to be a vampire slayer like his father, but he was more interesting in writing. He did very well in school, and was given a scholarship to a school in America, where he majored in English Literature. In his senior year, a short story he had written was published in a collection, and Gabriel was paid $1000 for his story. He became serious about becoming a writer at this point, and managed to land a deal with a moderately large publisher. Slaying became second priority, though he never forgot what he'd learned.
When he finished college he wrote his first book. It made the best seller list for 10 weeks, and landed him an even better deal with his publisher. He decided he wanted to write a gothic, occult-oriented novel. The interest in the subject, of course, came from what his mother had taught him. And though he understood the basic mechanics of vampires, he had never really experienced them firsthand. He went to his mother, asking her where he could go to experience the occult better. She asked him to take his mind off of it and not bother going to such a dangerous place, but she she saw she could not shake him, she told him about Isle Voletta, but did not mention that it was the town in which his father had been killed. And so he went.
