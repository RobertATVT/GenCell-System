Background


Chaya Venkatesan was born a Brahman Tamil in Madras, India on 23 August 1978. She moved with her family to San Francisco in time to start high school, and then she attended Berkeley, her parents not wanting her to stray too far from the nest and into the culture of the West. But after graduating with a BA in Psychology (not the BS that her parents wanted) and coming home to skip a year before applying to graduate school, her parents decided that her future would best be served by getting married. They did not want her to end up like her elder sister and marry a white non-Hindu (Heaven forbid!). To their dismay, the Western taint had already seeped into her good Tamil brain. They found her room empty the next morning.

She has always been a strange child, making weird observations as a young girl (and as a young woman) and growing up with a joyfully detached demeanor. She is the essence of quiet until she bursts out into an uncontrolled fit of laughter. She cries at the beauty of beaded dew on a mangled leaf, her mouth splits with a wide grin at the thought of mango ice cream. She creates chaos in the kitchen, but manages to cook well. She gets moody with the sky or the moon, and falls asleep at the drop of the hat. She is Chaya, hear her yawn!
