Background

His father was a pilot. His mother was a nurse. The pair met in a hospital in Laos, where his father was flying for a US-backed transport operation. The result of this union (though it was short) came in the form of a squalling baby, delivered in a hangar as his father was preparing to take off for a delivery mission. His father did return for the mission, landing the battered DC-10 moments before an engine blew.

His Mother, however, wasn't exactly so lucky. She left Caleb with his father when he was 2, never bothering to contact the pair again. And so, Caleb grew up an Air-brat, travelling with his father from nation to nation, whereever the government decided to send them. He had learned to fly a small plane by his 12th birthday, Helicopters by his 16th, and at 18 became a member of the USMC. They taught him how to fly in military zones, extracting soldiers. One year after the training was complete, he was dishoorably discharged.

The truth behind the matter was that after his discharge, he became a "volunteer" for a government backed air corps. Without any official ties to the military, Caleb and his comrades could extract everything from strike teams to spies to diplomats out of hostile nations. Stationed in the Florida Keys, his major assignment was to get these people out of latin nations unfriendly to the US. At least until, at the age of 33, he was shot down over Cuba. After a moment spent in a Cuban prison, he was released..but without government recognition or aircraft, he couldn't get off the island. After his extraction a year later, he officially retired, recieving in the severance package an old Cesna.

Well..that summed it mainly up. With his career non-existent, he moved to Voletta, his father's hometown. Through conniving favors, he managed to get the Cesna stored in an old hangar outside of town, flying it sparsely (no money for upkeep or fuel).
