Background


Background:
Giacomo was born to a poor Italian family. His father was a baker, but unfortunately not a very good one and thus the family was pitched into continual struggle. Giacomo, being the middle child of 10 was starved for attention, but was met only by the harsh teachings of his Roman Catholic mother when he tried to excell. Because of his starvation at times and lack of family, Giacomo soon drew within himself.
By his teens years, Giacomo was an extraordinarily rebellious individual. This rebellion, however, was not only confined to his parent's control over him, but also towards God, Himself. As a result, Giacomo readily immersed himself in all forms of vice. He became an avid drug user and drunk and at the age of 18, his parents tossed him to the streets. They had 9 other children to fill the gap, and they were determined not to be undermined by an upstart teenager. So, Giacomo gladly left and resumed his life on the street.
In order to fund this life, Giacomo sold the only thing he had left, his body. He became a prostitue and quite well known. He entertained street urchins and dignitaries alike. All in exchange for money to buy his true love, cocaine. These years as a shiftless, rootless, person hardened Giacomo and transformed what little innocence he possessed into a cold calculating demeaner.
It was this demeaner which attracted the attention of a very pale man as he strode down the avenue which Giacomo was "working". The man was intrigued by the hardset chin and the cold look in Giacomo eyes. He fell in love at first sight, but watched from afar, not knowing Giacomo's occupation.
Instead, this man revealed his presence in short bursts to Giacomo and tried to seduce the youth. However, the caterization of Giacomo's soul had left him impervious to the man's advances. This defeat drove the man's passions to even furter extremes and finally over the edge. As a result, the man cornered Giacomo one night and revealed his true vampiric nature to struggling youth.
This nature enthralled Giacomo and he begged the man to share it with him. However, this time it was the man's turn to be elusive and he denied Giacomo this gift, but left him with his name, that of Gabriel. The idea of immortality consumed Giacomo and he fell into a vast yearning everytime Gabriel was near. Giacomo offered his blood and Gabriel took it, but never enough to cause the transformation. For several months, Giacomo lay trapped between the desperation of Gabriel denying him immortality, and the ecstacy of being fed upon. These extremes further hardened Giacomo, until he was completely devoid of emotion, save the ambition to live forever.
Eventually, Gabriel acquiesced and took completed the ritual of turning Giacomo into a creature of the night, and that is how Giacomo was received into darkness; an arduous struggle with a disappointingly undramatic climax.
However, Giacomo's story does not end there. That is only the beginning. The rest of Giacomo's tale takes place in the 1970's in the city of Rome after Gabriel and Giacomo have built a family and a shipping empire. On the tenth anniversary of his reception into darkness, Giacomo perpetrates the ulitmate betrayal by destroying his sire.
With Gabriel dead, Giacomo returns to his family in order to ascend to the position of family head. However, his brothers and sisters do not respect or trust him nearly enough and subsequently to do instate him as either the Family Head, or the CEO of Fairfax Shipping.
Giacomo, who had anticipated such a move by his family, again delved into his heart of darkness and pulled out another horrifying betrayal. He paid several expert hunters who lived in Rome and gave them the whereabouts of where his family resided during the day. As a result, these hunters completely wiped out the Fairfax family in a single day, leaving Giacomo the only one with claim to the shipping empire.
But, without his family to dominate or their adoration, Giacomo grew tired of Rome. He tired of the history and the Church and even the business. So...he severed all ties with anyone in Italy, had his mortal family murdered to the last one, and sold his corporation.
With the millions he made off of the sale, Giacomo simply wandered the world. During the eighties, he wandered the major cities of Asia and America, and in about 1994 in New York he tried to establish a family, but his attempt only met with tragedy as his chyldren were destroyed very quickly. So, he left the Big Apple and looked for a suitable place to build an empire and a family.

And in January of 1998, he found Voletta....
He lived in Voletta from 1/98 until 5/99. He sired a number of childer, but only three were alive by the time he left and he had discovered a long lost vampiric relative. In 6/99 he was contacted by his aunt to hunt down an errant childe. He killed this childe in New Orleans and started to dabble in infernalism until his childhood friend cast out the vampirism within him. In Janurary 2000 he returned to Voletta.
