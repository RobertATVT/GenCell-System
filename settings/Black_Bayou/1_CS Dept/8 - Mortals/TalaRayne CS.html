Physical Description


Tala has long hair, reaching to the bottom of her lower back. She has eye glasses, and always keeps part of her hair up in a pony tail, and lets the rest hang loose. She has a wolf tattooed right where her hair ends, on her lower back. Each of her ears are pierced four times, three post-earings and one ring at the upper part of her ears. She only wears dark clothes, baggy pants , usually tight fitting tops, and black thick soled tennis shoes

Background

Born on a full moon in Florida, to a single mother, Tala never knew a father figure. Her mother was an alcoholic, but she loved and cherished her daughter. She became an alcoholic when her husband vanished one day, no note.. no nothing. Tala didn't care, she just wanted her mother to be alright.
She and her mother were poor, her aunt helped out a little by giving them a small amount of money so Tala could go to school, and her mother could afford food and clothing.
Tala was always quiet, dreary, and "far".. as in never hanging around kids her own age or talking to them. She always had an "i don't care" attitude towards life and all of it's aspects. She only had one friend, in her whole life. Her name was Jaymie. Jaymie was just like Tala, they both were artists and were so much alike.
Jaymie's parents beat her though, and she was always depressed. One night, since they lived next door, Tala heard screaming and glass breaking. When she ran into her friend's house, she found her parents drunk and stoned, passed out on the floor.. and... Jaymie... hanging from the staircase. She killed herself.
Tala was never the same, she was now 22 and still living with her mom. Her mother got in a fight with Jaymie's step-father, and he being drunk ( as always ) killed her.
Her aunt gave her money, Tala packed, and she left. Driving.. that's all that she was going to do. Just drive until she got far enough away from her life. At last, she came to a town, not knowing whether or not she was still in Florida. She didn't care. And then she saw an old worn sign.. :

" Welcome to Isle Voletta, Louisiana "
