Masteries


Brink of Death - 1 Soul to use. (##BrinkOfDeath##)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: NA / Continuous; takes 0 rounds to activate.
Effect/Description: Ever notice how, just when the hero is going down, they make an amazing come back and strikes a blow for truth, justice, and the American way? How about the bad guy that always comes back for one final scare? The ability to return from the brink of death is possessed by some Mortals. Haidee -1 Soul each use. (##Haidee##) (Nitram Tahalshia, SN Martichi)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: NA / 4 rounds or 2 minutes; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: Haidee was the only woman ever truly loved by Don Juan. Imagine the greatest of lovers only truly opening his heart to one woman. Such is the power of love. A force sometimes as strong as the tides. Sometimes as steady as a mountain. Based upon this connection that some Mortals have for those they love deeply, they sometimes are able to sense danger almost on a psychic level, to their loved one. The sensing takes the form of a vision, that seems to flash across the lover's eyes. These visions occur at anytime the loved one feels an extreme emotion. Hate, fear, happiness, and lust are only examples. They will know exactly what is happening to their loved one, though they will not know any details except for a 30 second flash of seeing through the eyes of the loved one. Also, should someone attack the object of the Mortal's Haidee, their love will be channeled into a blind rage. If the lover focuses their soul on the rage, they gain +4 to all attack rolls for the remainder of the scene. This bonus is only applicable after a physical attack causing damage to the Beloved, which is witnessed by the Mortal visually or via their 30 second vision. The loved one must be stated during character creation, or role-played over a month's time in the game. Should either loved one die, the Haidee is lost and may never be purchased by either character again.



Physical Description


She is usually seen wearing a tank top and some faded jeans, though occassionally dresses semi formally just for the heck of it. She never forgets to bring her tiny black purse with her wherever she goes. Her hair, which is shoulder length, is most of the times, worn down, unless it is extremely hot and humid. The first impression that people get when they see her is a natural inquisitiveness with a tad of playfulness in the background. However, when she becomes angry, they may wish they never saw her. Juliet often cannot control her temper.


Background



Background: Juliet Michelle Meyers was bought up by both of her parents in the upper class neighborhoods of Manhatten. She was fortunate in always having her parents there for her whenever she was in trouble, or needed help. Her parents, who were Shakespeare buffs, named all of their children after characters in some of their favorite plays he had written. Since she was a little girl, Juliet had always been a little extroverted. She had two sides of her personality: one was her constant search for excitement and the other was a want to become more knowledgable. These seem like completely opposite traits, but that was Juliet. Another thing that she has had since her childhood was her disgust for snobby and/or arrogant people. Nothing got on her nerves more than some people. Her childhood was, for the mostpart, was a happy one, though she was a victim of the "middle child" syndrome. She had an older brother, Anthony, who did excellent in his studies, and a little sister, Olivia, who was always the baby, the one who got most of the attention. It was not as severe as most cases so she did not stress about it too much. Other than that, she had an almost perfect childhood. At age 6 a frightening thing happened to Juliet, causing a reverence towards her brother. SHe had been throwing a beach ball around with her other cousins close to the pool, when the ball fell into the water. Juliet thought nothing of it and reached as far as she could to get a hold of the ball. Unfortunately, she fell in and what was worse, she didn't know how to swim. She started panicking, kicking with her feet, and trying to breathe air instead of water. She soon fell weak and went under. By this time, her cousins had run into the house and told everyone what happened and the first person to rush out was her brother Anthony. He immediately jumped into the pool and pulled Juliet out of the water. Luckily, nothing detrimental happened. From then on, she strived as best as she could, to be just like him. And so, like her brother, she also did well in her studies, academically and athletically, being in track and the swim team, earning trophies in both, while maintaining a 3.8 GPA. While in high school, another formidable thing happened to her: her father had developed prostate cancer. Once Juliet found out, she was totally devastated. For weeks, she would just stay in her room and cry. Her grades started to drop dramatically from high A's to low C's. All her family was sick with worry. Fortunately, after chemotherapy and radiation, the cancer was defeated. Juliet determined that it must have been the work of her amulet that her brother had given her. From then on, she hardly ever took it off. She enjoyed her company with all her family, not taking any of them for granted after her father's full recovery. Juliet attended UCLA, majoring in psychology, where she met up with her soon to be best friend, Gena Kilcher. They had a really good time together and were roomates all through college. Suddenly, in the middle of her Junior year, her older brother Anthony tragically died in Isle Voletta, being stabbed to death by some unknown assailent. Juliet was crushed because she had always looked up to her brother and now he had mysteriously been killed. She had to find out why. So then, Juliet went to Voletta to find out about Anthony's death. However, no one had ever even heard of him, and the case was still unsolved. Juliet tried and tried, but to no avail. Soon, she gave up, tired and alone, and stayed put, partly because her brother had died there and partly because she had grown a little accustomed to the strangeness of the whole place.
