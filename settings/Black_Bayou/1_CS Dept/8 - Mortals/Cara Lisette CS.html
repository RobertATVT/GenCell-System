Background

Twenty-two years ago, Harriet and Michael Cendrice had a second child. Well, Harriet had the child, if you want to be precise, but anyway. They lived in a small suburb, just outside of L.A., a fairly average family. Not perfect, but normal, if you can define normal. But their second kid was extraordinarily beautiful, and didn't even take after them. They all had black hair, with blue eyes and fairly pale skin. Kinda weird people. Welsh.

Their daughters, Rachel and Cara, were as different as night and day. Rachel was withdrawn, moody, prone to depression, and rebellious.. She was fairly average in looks, too. A whiny chick with a guitar. But Cara.. Cara was outgoing, friendly, beautiful, and she played the flute in band.

Yes, Rachel hated Cara.

Mostly because of looks, though. Rachel wanted *so* much to be extraordinary. Special. She was special, because she had an amazing flair for poetry, but that's something else. She also hated their parents, because, while they certainly gave Rachel an adequate amount of love and care, she *knew* she could never amount to what Cara was. Their golden daughter. I'm not trying to say that they favored Cara, but it was difficult to praise them equally because Cara did so much more to be praised for.

December 25. 1993. Rachel shot herself through the mouth. Cara was heartbroken. She really loved her sister, she did, and she knew it was because of her. Her parents were sad, but they tried not to show it, not wanting to worry Cara.

Cara's grades dropped dramatically, she became depressed and withdrawn. She stopped eating. Her looks were beginning to disappear. Mrs. Cendrice was trying to help her by getting her internet. Bad idea. She was on it constantly.

Then Cara just took off. Left. Seventeenth birthday. She became a waitress in Wisconsin, at some diner. Earned a living. Got fired. But, she was perky again, happy and nice and innocent and all that stuff. So she decided to make a new beginning.

Here she is, folks, ready or not. Cara is Isle Voletta's new burst of sunshine.
