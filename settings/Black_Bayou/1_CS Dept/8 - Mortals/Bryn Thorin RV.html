Background

::You hear these words delicately wrapped in sweet Southern accent::
I was born second daughter to the Monroe's of Savannah, Georgia. Marie and Gregory, to be exact, as if you didn't already know. Yes, I'm talking about THE Gregory Monroe, President and CEO of THE Southern Bank. That's my Daddy. :::with almost a mocking tone in her voice::: I'm the baby of the family :::She rolls her eyes at that and takes a seat on the couch offering some tea like a "good" hostess... programmed for every social function/occassion:::

Well, I must admit I've never had an interview quite like this.... :: She peers at the person sitting directly across from her with the ANT nametag:: I simply want to visit my sister, Mist. :::She pulls out an envelope from her pocket with return address marked, Voletta, LA::: This is the letter I received from Mist inviting me. I've never heard of a place that interviews visitors.. ::nervous chuckle:: but then again, my sister never would pick a common place to hang out.. always living on the edge. Anyway, you want to know about lil' ol' me, right?

Well, like I said, I'm the youngest. Momma and Daddy's "Pride and Joy" they call me. Daddy even called me PJ for a short time when I was little. Well, MY childhood was pretty much a wall to wall social calendar event. I had ballet, piano, gymnastics, cheerleading, formals, balls, finishing school.. name a day of the week and I had SOMETHING grooming me with Southern graces. Except Sundays, that was reserved for church. My faith in the Great Creator is what gets me through every day, knowing He's there by my side, supporting me. How unlike my father... oh he's by my side, he's just... well.. let me continue on with my upbringing and perhaps you'll understand.

Daddy sent me to the University of Georgia in hopes of me getting my "MRS." degree. I believe HE chose that school for me because it wasn't too far from home. Within hours he could reach me and remind me of my place in life, as his "obedient" daughter, Bryn. Signing me up for classes, choosing my friends, and boyfriends, and denying the *undesirables* access to my life. I only wished for him to have a litle faith in MY choices. Praise God for good friends who understood that when Daddy was in town, they were to remain scarce, or face the wrath of my father. ::Sigh:: Oh, don't get me wrong.. I love my Daddy, but he just has this way of thinking that he knows what is best for my life, and I do declare that it's just not true! ::Her voice hushes as in fear of someone hearing:: HE was the one that originally made the "match" between Junior Beauregard and myself. ::Cringes at the sound of the name:: He was the most boring, unimaginative, self-centered, pretentious human being I have ever met. The one thing he had going for him, his unbridled passion for his career, was what ultimately led to Daddy's condemnation. (Oh, how I wished he showed passion for someone... er.. something else.) Junior had plans of his own and rejected Daddy's "generous" job offer with his company. Needless to say, Junior was no longer acceptable in Daddy's eyes because he was uncontrollable. :::Suddenly her mind flashes on a picture of her sister Mist:: I would PRAY at night for the Lord to taketh this cup away from me :::giggling:: ...meaning Junior... and apparently He understood. ::smiling:: Since the honeymoon is paid for... my parents thought this would be an opportunity for me to "get away from it all" and find myself, ::chuckles:: or a rich count on the French Riviera. ::Cautiously looks at door:: And that's where THEY think I'm going to be. However, my friend Cecily was gracious enough to take the tickets and will be mailing Momma and Daddy best wishes from me from time to time. But *I* have other plans.... ::She says with a twinkle in her eye::

My sister, Mist left the household at the age of 18. Naturally, Mist was cut off from the family fortune, and Daddy would KILL me if he knew I had kept in contact much less helped finance her way through college on my allowance. One of the few things in my life I cherish as being "mine". The relationship between my sister and I. ::Smiles sweetly:: I love her. She is the one person who has never TOLD me what to do.. but asked me. Between you and me.. I think something happened 11 years ago when Mist left... something that no one dares speak about... even Mist.. who wouldn't pass up dishing dirt on Daddy in a minute. ::Reminiscing:: I remember, one night... when I was about to leave for college... I awoke to hear my momma crying.. She was talking to Daddy... and saying something about Mist... I arrived too late at their door to make it out clearly... between the tears... and then Daddy... he... :::Bryn shudders at the sound of the slap then shakes herself as if out of a deep dream::: Daddy... he ... must have been helping her out of a fit... Momma gets those.. from her drinking.. that must have been it :::She says in a soft whisper.. more to convince herself than anyone else::: I need to find out what happened. What broke my perfect family. Now's my chance to confront her, without being under Daddy's "watchful" eye.

:::Looks at letter again as if drawing strength and says with intensity::: I have been in the "Monroe" stronghold for so long that I feel I have been totally unmarked and been unremarkable. It's time for me to experience life and put some color on this blank canvas of mine. Voletta is somehow part of my Destiny... I feel it in my soul. I'm being beckoned... and for the first time in my life... I'm going to take the road less traveled.. the road to the unknown.. and confront those demons head on. Demons.. ::startled by her choice of words::: I wonder what made that come out of my mouth?

:::The ANT smiles a sly grin:::

::She looks and says:: Well, do I pass? Do I get to see my sister? I've heard it's all up to you....
