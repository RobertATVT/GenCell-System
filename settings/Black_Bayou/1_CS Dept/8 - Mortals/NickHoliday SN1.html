 Background

Dominic "Nick" Holiday, was born on Nov. 8th, 1973, in San Francisco, CA. He grew up in an apartment over his parents' deli with his older brother, Mitch, and his younger sister, Gwen. Space was cramped, and money was usually pretty tight, and these two factors motivated Nick to spend a lot of his time on the streets of San Francisco. He has always been charismatic and easy-natured, and he made friends easily. Had he lived in a better neighborhood, he probably would have made much nicer friends, but such was not the case. Mitch, on the other hand, spent most of his time working in the store with their parents, Bill and Janice. Mitch didn't terribly approve of Nick's carefree lifestyle and shady circle of friends, and ooften lectured Nick on responsibility, and about Dad's failing health, and how Nick should stop hanging out with hoods and start helping support the family. And that's when Nick decided to go into business. At the age of fifteen, he became an errand boy for some of the lower level crime figures in S.F. Because people felt so naturally easy with Nick, he started to learn a lot of somewhat confidential information, which also turned out to be quite profitable as well. over the next four years, he started bringing home a fair amount of money from his odd jobs, with Mitch being the only one who suspected the money's origin. Mitch knew about Nick's illegal pastime and he despised it. Because if Nick spent more time at the store, he might have known that some of the same people he was dealing with were behind a couple of gents charging "protection" fees from his parents.
When Nick did learn of this, he told his big brother not to worry, it was just a little misunderstanding that he'd take care of. Nick voiced a complaint to his primary employer, one Eddie Gennaro, of the squeeze on his family. Eddie knew it to be the work of one of his rivals, the DeGassi's, and performed a little "counter-protection" and things were back to normal again. For a little while.
Within the next year and a half, Eddie Gennaro was killed. And the DeGassi's went about reclaiming all of their old territory with a vengeance. Remembering the fuss that went down over a certain deli owned by a certain Irish family, they felt that it was a slight that could not go unchallenged.
Bill Holiday died at 3:17 a.m. of a bullet to the chest.
Mitch told Nick that he thought it was time for him to leave. His mother never knew about his roundabout involvement in his father's death, and Mitch said he'd never tell her or their little sister, if Nick just left.
Since then Nick has made a living as a middleman, a fence, and a smuggler. A damn good one, at that. But he's never gotten involved in the deeper "business" matters. He claims no allegiance, and works for the highest bidder. But he maintains his neutrality. Since he's moved out, Mitch has run the store, taking care of mama and Gwen and eventually getting married. Nick keeps in tough with his mother, sending regular checks back home to take care of her medical bills. As far as she knows, Nick's in the import/export business.
Nick has most recently been living in Texas, and has been running his business between Texas and Miami. He's passed through Isle Voletta more than once along the trip, and has taken a shine to it, thinking it a nice place to lay low between jobs, and also close to New Orleans, a nice major port city.
