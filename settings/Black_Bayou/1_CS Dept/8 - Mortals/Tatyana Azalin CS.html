Background


Tatyana Azalin is one of five children born to Anastasia and Donald Azalin in New Orleans, Louisana. Born into a banking family, Tatyana found herself drawn more to the psyche of people. Being the only daughter of the family, her mother, born of gypsy stock and brought over from Romania by her father, imparted the gypsy knowledge upon her. She took the lessons to heart and all the lore that she was told, over and over again was committed to memory. She was the only one of the children who learned her mother's native tounge. This helped to fuel her thirst for knowledge.
Tatyana was a deligent and hard worker and her academic prowess awarded her a scholorship to Yale University. She choses psychology as her major and with her fascination of people, specialized in criminal profiling, after attending a lecture by one John Douglas. His insight into the criminal mind unleashed what she felt deep within her and yet hadn't been able to put her finger on that feeling. Needing to know more, she kept pressing Douglas with questions until he invited her to Quantico, to take a couple of classes he and Ressler taught agents. She snapped up the invitation.
Not one to have had any type of law enforcement background, she proved to be an adpt pupil, and with some strings pulled, she continued on at Quantico in the Investigative Support Unit of the FBI as an honory agent. Her two years of study helped her to become a well-respected criminal profiler, and she doesn't hesitate to turn to the Support Unit of the FBI when she finds herself unable to figure out a signature or other details to help identify the UNSUB. Having read the work on the Red-Headed Reaper by Rafe LeMon, she made her way to Voletta, to hope to gain some more, and first-hand knowledge about this serial killer.
