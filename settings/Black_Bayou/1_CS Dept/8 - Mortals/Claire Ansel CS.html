Background:
This Peach, born and raised in Georgia, had a special ability from a young age. Open and able to speak with the angels above, Claire sought their guidance during very trying times in her life. Not only in tune with herself and the world around her, Claire was able to tune herself into special people in her life. Family, a few friends, and a loved one. Only a few could truly understand her.
The youngest of five children, Claire knew at an early age that she wanted to be a doctor. Her father was a family physician, having a practice in the small town in which they resided; she knew she wanted to work with children. And so she studied medicine at Johns Hopkins University in Maryland, like her father did so many years prior.
It had taken her many years of frustration to get her to college. A learning disability, dyslexia, went undetected and kept her from functioning at her potential. Guidance from her angels gave her the strength to become an honor's student and then attend the prestigious university and medical school.
At the age of 16, Claire was involved in an automobile accident, in which her friend - the driver- was killed. This devastated Claire, leaving her to question everything and pushing the angels and her beliefs aside. It was after meeting Aisa Grigori that her belief and faith were restored. A small gift, an angel pin, was left after their meeting and from that point on, Claire knew they were destined to meet again. And they did.
Beyond college and medical school, Claire returned home and established a pediatric practice with her father's help. Due to demand in the small town, Claire also served as a midwife. Her first gift to herself was a blue 1967 Ford Mustang Convertible.
Her entrance into Voletta did not come without loss. The death of her paternal grandfather, a great loss to everyone, especially Claire, is the reason for her journey. It was no surprise that his small home and land in Voletta were left to her; their special bond was something always questioned, but he was one person that understood her.
