Luck - 0 Soul to use. (##LuckRe-roll##)
System/Duration: NA / Instant; takes 0 rounds to activate.
Effect/Description: What is it about mortals that seems to always get them out of trouble at just the right moment? It may not be used to re-roll the same dice roll more than once, and the user must take whatever the second roll is, regardless of the result. The mortal can only enact this mastery twice per night.

The Gift - 0 Soul to use. (no room command needed)
System/Duration: NA / NA
Effect/Description: Everyone has that little something they just seem naturally gifted at. Whether it be juggling, mathematics, or knife throwing, it's there, and it's just waiting to be found. The player may gain 1 skill at level 3 for no cost, except Lore. However, they may not use other points to build upon the skill at generation, only later with updates. This skill must have an * by its name to denote which skill is the Gift. Should you purchase an Occupational Training type that grants a non-combat skill bonus that you have chosen for your Gift, the bonus levels add to the ones granted by the Gift. You may only use a skill from the non-combat skill list as your Gift if an occupation gives you additional levels in it during character generation. (Note, Evasion, Reflexes and Sure Shot are classified as Combat Skills). If you ever change breeds, you do not lose the levels of the gift unless they interfere with another rule. For example, if you have Chi it is automatically replaced with the fighting style of the new breed at half its current level. The levels are still halved as normal, even if it was the Gift.

Background


Background: Sanity Carter was born to a family of 7 other children. Her father and mother worked a ranch in Dallas, Texas until a disease killed half the cattle. The financial and emotional strain sent her mother packing. 3 of the children followed, Sanity stayed behind to wrangle the other siblings, strangely living up to her name by being the voice of reason in the crisis. As her father slipped deeper into a steady depression, she worked to get her remaining brother and sisters off to college. As soon as they were gone, she set off down the road to maker her fortune.
Sadly, as many find out, the life of a traveler is a lonely one. Sanity spends most of her time seeing the world and seeking out parties and friends. She knows someone in every town, and makes it a point to think of herself as a sort of missionary--floating on the tides of fate to do the good work of the Powers That Be.
On her way to New Orleans from the St. Patty's Day celebration in Savannah, GA, she was dropped off by a trucker who'd become tired of her endless chatter. Faced with the Voletta Bar for the first time, Sanity quickly realized the bleak tendancies of the town, and through one bad turn after another, has decided maybe she's supposed to stay for awhile.
Just until the wind calls again...
