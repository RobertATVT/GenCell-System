Background



Born into a family of old Southern money, Antoine had it made from birth- his acceptances to college, law school, and an elite fraternity were already assured, legacies of his father and grandfather. And, what's more, Antoine knew he had it made, a fact that he didn't hesitate to revel in and lord over the help. Almost from the time he could understand what words like "money", "wealth", and "privledge" meant, he's gotten what he wanted, when he wanted it. This made him, in short, a spoiled little boy that's grown into a spoiled man. He has committed various petty crimes, from petty theft to drunken bar fights, but he's never been to prison- or, indeed, to lock-up- because his family's lawyer keeps him out of trouble as a matter of course. In fact, the law firm's number is on the speed dialer of the cell phone Daddy gave him- right after the bank.

Only two things mar this idyllic lifestyle. The first is that he can't access his trust, which has an estimted value of several million dollars, and the second is his now dead grandmother.

The key to the trust lies in the special conditions placed upon it- if he doesn't meet them, by spending at least four months of his 21st year in Isla Violetta, then he can't get his money.

And his grandmother, whom Antoine hated, is the one that set the trust up.

His grandmother, Marie, was a spiteful old witch- literally. When Antoine was born, she used her magic and foresaw two things: one, that he would be a "spiteful, viperous, shallow little dilettante," and two, that somewhere, a spark of something... special... burned within him. And so, when she set up the trust, she made his time on the island mandatory, believing that the power there would call to him and awaken him- hopefully to become someone- or something- better than what he was.

Years have passed, and Antoine is now 21. His college courses are a joke, his grades already bought and paid for- and so, using spring break as an excuse, he's made his way to the Island, for the purpose of satisfying his grandmother's wishes and thereby gaining the entirety of his trust when he comes of age.
