Background


A month early, James Tallbear was born to a single Shoshone mother from the Wind River Reservation in the Landers hospital in central Wyoming. It had been a long, traumatic labor for his mother, without attendance from either family or the father. Her family had not been told of her pregnancy when she left. The father had not been Shoshone, but a French Canadian tourist with amber eyes like a wolf. She did not immediately return to the Wind River Reservation, but attempted to live in the capital city of Cheyenne for a year with her secret child. It was a hand-to-mouth sort of existence; she had been nineteen in 1972, with no skills to speak of. In 1973, though, she gave up and returned to her home on the reservation. But she could not take the child. When asked, after all, she had said she was baby-sitting someone else's child.

James was given up for adoption and still young enough to be snatched up quickly, mixed heritage aside. Jackson and Krisha Wolfe doted on their new son. It had been his sterility and encroaching age that prompted the adoption, but Jackson was an enlightened sort. Together his new parents decided to change his name, feeling that it would make him feel more welcome as he grew older. Josiah's life, honestly, was little touched by hardship after his adoption. At least, until his birth mother contacted his adopted parents. She wanted her son to know about his people, or at least her people. The only things she remembered about his father after all this time was his eyes, amber and wolfish. He had inherited those eyes. He was sixteen when he met his mother, against his real wishes. He was humoring his parents, honestly. They agreed that he should know of his heritage.

She called him James. It was not a pleasant day.

He did not agree to see her again, though she did try. Communication was filtered through his parents, after that first meeting, however. He continued on with the interests that had developed through school. He had decided at the tender age of fourteen to be an artist. His birth mother, Michelle Tallbear, tried once more to meet with him in person before he left Wyoming for college in Houston's Art Institute. She hoped to persuade him to come to the reservation, after his studies, and depict modern tribal life. He was nineteen then and something cold had grown in him through her continued intervention in his life. Josiah told her in no uncertain terms that she had given him up, and had no right to his attention or affection after that.

That last meeting stayed a source of disagreement with his parents while he was away, but they did not press. They saw the hurt it caused their son. He maintained little to no working knowledge of his mother's people. He preferred the culture he had been raised with. He returned to Wyoming to make his name, and take care of his now-ailing parents. He did become a well known nature and abstract artist. Late in the December of 1998, began the end of his time in Wyoming. His mother died in her sleep; simple and painless. Josiah watched grief kill his father over the next year, though medical reports listed heart failure.

Michelle appeared in that wreckage to offer him comfort, another family. He shut the door in her face and began making plans to move. Isle Voletta was selected with the dart board method, and a house was bought. Then, without word to anyone, he made a path south.
