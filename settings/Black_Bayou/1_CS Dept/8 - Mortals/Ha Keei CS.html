Background:

Born to an American family in the carribean he grew up in a villa with semi wealth. His father was a mayor of a small town that overlooked the beatiful Gulf of Mexico. Though his father seemed to be a lawful man to the public he had a darker side, that of a drug smuggler. Because of this Ha Keel grew up learning how to take care of "business" so to speak. From an early age he showed signs of being a marksman and was able to pick targets off at a large distance.
Upon reaching the age of 20 he left his fathers house to inspect holdings in Florida. While riding in a small plane the DEA made a raid and brought the plane down for inspection. Upon finding out he was the son of a wanted drug lord they took him into custody and to a prison in southern Alabama. There he spent eight years in a federal prison. One night two men came to his cell and opened it, telling him to come with them. Doing so he soon found out that the men were his fathers doing and that he was now a free man. Heading swiftly for an air field he boarded another plane and was soon on his way back to his fathers home.
While flying over Lousiana the plane was again found by the DEA and when they refused to land the plane it was shot down. He barely escaped with his life and took refuge in the swamp, determined to get to Texas then back home. That is where he stumbled onto Voletta. Having had money given to him after being busted from the prison he swiftly armed himself with his two favorite weapons and decided to ply his trade in this city for awhile as it had cought his interest. Being a sharp person with keen eyes he noticed much odd behavior from certain people and places within the city. His courosity perked he now works as an assassin for hire within the city. Because of his high profile he must still dodge the law, or eliminate it if possible. His first day there he was attacked by a strange wolf beast which he soon figured to be a werewolf. Because of this he now hunts them with more ferocity then any other group, though he never loses site that the real money is still making hits on humans.
