I do my best now to describe what I see on her face--tell her just what I think. Foolish perhaps, but the ever easy conversationalist, I am not. She frowns. A Repeated Story



"I don't care if you believe me. I know how [ ]ing crazy I sound. When I was a little girl, my dad used to scare me with all the routine monster stories. You know, dragons, ghosts, werewolves..vampires. The same tall tales I'm sure you were told when you were young. Of course, as we all get older we learn that things like that don't really exist. The tooth fairy isn't really the one putting quarters under your pillow. Santa Claus isn't really the one leaving presents under the tree. As we grow up we accept the reality of the world around us. So did I, so did you."
Another menthol cigarette is lit and the diminutive, female form in front of you leans back casually.
"So when I tell you that Vampires really do exist, I expect you to laugh at me, call me a loon. You see, people don't want to hear that the world that they have come to accept is a farce. And you know why? Because that would mean that they would have to question everything else. Is there life after death? Do things like angels and demons really exist? Hell, does your husband really just go out for a drink with his buddies every other night?"
A rough chuckle is issued, along with a toke from the cigarette, thick smoke blown into your face.
"So, do you think I am rude yet? Lacking in manners? Believe me, I have been called some cruel [ ]. To tell you the truth, I am surprised you have remained her as long as you have. I mean, come on, just who am I to sit here and practically tell you that your husband is a cheating bastard, or even worse that Vampires exist?
The woman leans forward, half smoked cig perching on the swell of pouty lips.
"I suppose I can't say for sure that your husband is unfaithful, hell, what's less, I don't care. But I can tell you one thing with all certainty. Vampires are not just the scary stories that we have been told in countless movies. That's right, look at me like I am bull [ ]ing you. I'm not honey. They roam the night. They hunt us."
The accumulating ash is tapped into a nearby ashtray and the woman leans back yet again, drawing a tiny knee up and folding her leg neatly under her. A calm sigh is issued.
"Do you want the whole story? I mean my insanity had to have originated from somewhere right? Hell, I don't keep secrets. As far as I am concerned if you have tolerated my [ ]ing ramblings this far, you should get the complete, uncropped tale. Yes? Alright then."
Another long drag, bringing the cigarette to death on her cinnamon lips. And then it is mashed into the ashtray. She stares at you, as if trying to think of the best way to start. And when she does start, finally after a sip from the squat coffee cup at her right, her voice is low, somber.
"I grew up like every one else, you know, 'normal' family--dad, mom, brother, cat, dog--the whole kit and kaboodle. Hell, I think we even had a station wagon for Christ's sake. I finished high school, though my grades left a little to be desired. I started college, yeah, tried the whole dorm thing, but that wore off fast. The girls were prissy, [ ]y sluts, and the guys, well, they all liked the prissy, [ ]y sluts. Nope, wasn't my thing. So, I came home and worked at a cute little café called 'Sylvia's'."
The woman pauses, toothpick digits tapping lightly over the polished table top.
"I met Saul, that same year. I won't get into the mushy love [ ], but I will say that I hated him at first. I've always been partial to blondes. He was a cocky brunette, and smart ass to boot. But as love goes, I found I had no control over what developed between us. I fell head over heels. And that SOB talked me into marrying him."
The story is halted and the woman issues a gravely chuckle, fumbles around for another cigarette, then decides against it and tosses it onto the table in front of you with a feminine huff.
"I never smoke when I tell this story."
Her hands are folded as if needing to prevent them from lurching toward the discarded menthol.
"Anyway, I think I was 23 then, or just turned. We commenced with married life, and had a great time of it. We fit perfectly, though it surprised me to no end. He could aggravate me like nothing else, then turn around and make me cry with his tenderness. I was completely baffled by the hold the bastard had on me."
She laughs again as a hand rises to brush at her lips, eyes darting away and then back to you.
"We loved each other fiercely. But I suppose the fates enjoy being cruel. Who knows. We had decided on a vacation. It had been a year since we were married, and we both needed a little getaway. There was a resort in Florida we were interested in and so made plans to travel there. We arrived and spent several days filled with laughing, drinking and wonderous sex. Then on the third day, or night rather, Saul was taken from me."
The woman uncurls from her position, waving a perky waitress over as she requests more coffee. The cup is filled and she drinks deeply, clinking the mug down in front of her. She regards you with a frown, which seems to fit her doll-like face perfectly.
"I don't expect you to believe this part. Hell, I barely believe it, and as I was saying earlier, it's just easier not to believe it. Saul was killed by vampires. I was attacked as well, and barely survived. Don't look so stunned, honey, you knew it was coming didn't you? [ ], I pretty much set you up for it. Yes, he was killed by the bastards. I watched the whole thing, screaming like a banshee, and struggling to get to him, but the one on me, had other plans--namely this."
She leans toward you, nearly knocking the steaming coffee over, and moving the collar of her jacket. A large scar graces the flesh of her neck. Not bite marks, rather tear marks, as if her throat was ripped to shreds and peiced carefully back together. She draws back and crosses her arms, as if that is the only proof you will ever need.
"Do you believe me now?"
