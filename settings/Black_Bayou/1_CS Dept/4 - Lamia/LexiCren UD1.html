Background
((Approved by WestWd 7-10-99))


In the winter of 1977, Alexandra Erin Crenshaw was born in Chicago, IL, to Walter and Denise Crenshaw. Denise stayed at home with Lexi while Walter built and managed a formidable publishing empire of half a dozen popular travel magazines. Lexi was raised and shuffled in the three homes--and single Aspen cabin--the family owned.

Being raised in a rather privileged environment, Lexi was allowed to taste test numerous hobbies and interests, but it wasn't until her teenage years that she discovered a talent all her own and her true passion...tennis. Once bitten by the bug, she immersed herself in the sport and by the end of her high school career had been vaulted consistently into the top seats of state and national junior competitions. There was much debate over whether to turn pro or enter college, but in Lexi's mind there was no question...tennis was her life.

The summer she turned pro, however, was a major turning point in her life. Being an only child and consummate Daddy's girl, Lexi often spent time bouncing from office to office with her father. She met a young photography intern by the name of Jakob Munro who was fresh from the farm in Montana. The chemistry between the pampered little city girl and the rough & tumble cowboy was ignited instantly and flared like wildfire through the summer. Figuring this was a teenage fling, her parents indulged the nonstop attention they gave one another once she finished practicing and he finished laying out pictures and material. When Jakob left, however, Lexi's parents were heartily dismayed to find that Lexi pined after him daily and even allowed her game to suffer. She went from earning the #18 seat after the Virginia Slims tournament to falling to an appalling #120 after she and Jakob were separated.

That, however, was only a slight disappointment in the face of what happened next.

During the Thanksgiving Holidays, Lexi was due to fly to Montana to meet Jakob's family for the first time. As she was packing the night before, she began feeling dizzy and hot, and she collapsed before she could finish the first suitcase---the victim of a rare form of viral meningitis which ravaged her body and left her immune system severly weakened, her brain slightly damaged, and her hearing non-existent.

Jakob flew to her side immediately and remained with her and her family throughout the winter. His presence and encouragement retrieved her from the brink of severe depression and steadied her through the transition of learning a whole new language and way to communicate--sign language. Her parents were thankful for the inspiration he was to their daughter, rewarding him with a generous position with one of the magazines to keep him close to Lexi.

Eventually Lexi returned to tennis with an increased fervor and passion for the sport. Without the sound of the crowd to distract her, her focus was even more intense than before, and she was sure to match--if not exceed--her previous levels of performance. With her weakened immune system, however, it was impossible for her to maintain the levels of endurance she needed to compete. By the third tournament, she almost had to be carried from the court. Not once, but twice, she had been forced to leave the sport she so loved. Not once, but twice, Jakob served to give her cause to go on. He had become her purpose in life.

Now, almost 2 years after her retirement, her father has decided to try to wean Lexi from her tremendous reliance on Jakob by giving him assignments and responsibilities that require travel. The assignments started off small and infrequent but have become more regular with time.

Usually circumstance has prevented Lexi from joining Jakob. Mother has suddenly needed her at home, or she has been asked to make appearances for various health and medical organizations. But Lexi had never been to Louisiana before--had never even heard of Isle Voletta, so when Father asked Jakob to help oversee a small, southern magazine he was bailing out of trouble by buying it out and resurrecting it, Lexi couldn't help but tagalong, much to Daddy's chagrin.
