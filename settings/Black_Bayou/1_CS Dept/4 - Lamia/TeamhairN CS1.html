Background

Teamhair NicIomhair (Pronounced as TE-a-var N'giver) was born in a small town in Ireland, on July 6th, 1975. Her family were well-known traveling singers, and were rumored to have descended from an ancient Bard. She was taught both English and Gaelic, and could sing in both languages by the time she was 4.
At age 7, Teamhair's grandfather Seumas MacIomhair, a famous Irish fiddler, began teaching her his craft. Over the next 7 years, Teamhair learned every fiddler's trick Suemas knew, and could learn a new song after hearing it twice. Also during this time, Suemas had other family members teach young Teamhair the pennywhistle and the basics of the harp.
When Teamhair turned 14, Suemas started taken her on the road with him for the summer holidays. They traveled all over Ireland, from one festival to the next. Spending 9 months in school, and 3 months on the road taught Teamhair a lot about the music business, including handling money, getting gigs, and also how to handle herself in the middle of a drunken brawl. As a graduation present, Seumas added Teamhair's name to the posters and marquees where they performed, and turned over more and more of the show to her.
Suemas took sick when Teamhair was 20. That was when Suemas told her the family secret: the MacIomhairs were the last of the Great Bards. All the things he'd been trying to teach her was so she could search out the Lost Songs of Mastery: the Song of Joy, the Song of Sadness, and the Song of Slumber. With these Songs, she could take her place as a Full Bard, something which had not been seen in generations. He also warned her that there were dangers in the world, unseen by most. The stories of Shapeshifters were true. He gave her what knowledge he could of these creatures, and encouraged her to find out more, for her own protection. Suemas was dead within the month.
That year Teamhair toured as normal, because Suemas would have expected it, but there was new emotion and energy in her music. A record company scout was at on of her shows, and after much convincing Teamhair went in and recorded an album. It went gold in Europe on her 21st birthday.
Now: Teamhair has just finished her first US tour on her second album. The last stop was in New Orleans, and she's looking for a place to relax before going back into the studio. A friend of her US manager suggests she borrow a small fishing bungalow he owns in a little place an hour outside of New Orleans. A small town called Ilse Voletta. While she'd prefer to return to Ireland, it was getting a little too busy for her liking.
