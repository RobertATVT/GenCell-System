Healing - 0 Soul to use. (no room command needed)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Effect/Description: Angels have an incredible natural healing ability. Any wound caused to the angel or its host body will heal at the rate of 1 Body per hour the character rests. If the character takes any strenuous action, the healing is disrupted and the hour starts over. A Room Host must be notified when you claim your Body point back. Wounds created by faith water cannot be healed with this mastery. Wounds from fire heal at the rate of 1 Body per day and only when the character sleeps. If the character is woken up before the day is over, the healing process is disrupted and they do not regain their point (which means they cannot role-play until the damage is healed). The damage from an attack that removes a limb heals at the normal rate, but the player must role-play the limb slowly growing back. (Length of time for the limb to regenerate is determined by the Forum Director.)

The Gift of Life - 0 Soul to use. (##GiftLife ##)
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 18 / Instant; takes 2 rounds to activate.
Effect/Description: The angel may transfer their Body points to any willing (or stunned / unconscious) individual through touch. This transfer is almost instantaneous in normal RP, but during combat, no more than 2 Body points can be transferred per round, requiring an re-roll for every additional round. Transferring Body points to a stunned or unconscious individual without permission is perfectly acceptable. This same mastery also has a number of minor effects, causing flowers to bloom spontaneously, or making a starving person feel as though they've just eaten their full. In the end, a Host or GM or ST has the final say in what can and cannot be performed with this power.

Volange form, Aida Falak (Azrael) :


Almost wholly dissimilar to the mortal seeming, the angel's flesh has a gilded, almost internal incandescence of bronze, dark golden hair tumbling free about slender shoulders, gray-green eyes gleaming as if lit from within. The external evidence of the body form's gender is reduced, the slim figure assuming an andrognyous quality, clothed sparingly in a loose drapes of shimmery, intangible light that might resemble white silk, hanging from one shoulder to shroud the chest at an angle, and again about the hips to brush the upper thighs. Bare feet and hands are obscured by the pale brilliance of the aura, the angel's compassion in its function as a death-herald of the Light stilling the lovely features, a pair of ivory-feathered and gold-tipped wings folded neat at its back, the pinions dripping down with the aura's glow.

Physical Description


Aida is 23 years of age, of average height (5'6") and slender build (115 lbs) with a spare, almost boyish frame. Her skin is a medium olive tone, her hair pitch-dark and faintly highlighted with mahogany, her eyes an eerily consistent pale green. She's reasonably attractive of feature, though not classically so, evidently Arabic of heritage, with a long, slim face (and an aquiline nose to match) and juttingly hollowed cheekbones, thinnish lips. Said features contribute to a rather severe look when matched with back-swept hair and the silver wire-rims she dons in the classroom, but in casual company she prefers the jeans-and-tee-shirts uniform.

Background


Aida Sabirah Falak (Arabic, meaning in part, "reward" and "star" for a surname...had to have some kind of a tie-in to "Stella"), native of New York, NY, born and raised, the youngest daughter of second-generation Lebanese-American parents. Sort of a drifter by nature, she graduated from NYU with a B.A. in English literature and intended to be a teacher, but after a couple years working in New York public high schools didn't find teaching as rewarding a path as she'd hoped, and gave her resignation with the end of the winter break. Her father is an oil executive, rather well-off to say the least, and highly displeased with her choice to quit teaching, as he was never thrilled that she was teaching at public school (besides staunchly refusing to be wed to any of the men of his choice) in the first place. The family is not particularly devout, religiously (Sunni) but Aida's personal faith is unshakable, and she knows that the Light is leading her on the correct path, and will have her do as she is fated. A half-hearted job search in other regions of the country leads her to an available position at Voletta's public high school, and much to the increasing displeasure of her parents, she decides she is going to pursue that position. After begging and pleading for a loan from them and adding that to her meagre savings, she trucks down to Louisiana to attempt to start a new life. Azrael has, of course, been subtly directing this sequence of events since recently being embraced by the mortal Aida- the Light would have its angel of death at the side of Voletta's archangel, to aid in the goals and pursuits of the angelic flock in Voletta.
