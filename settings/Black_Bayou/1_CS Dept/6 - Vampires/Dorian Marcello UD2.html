Effect/Description: A vampire that is attuned to its own inner nature is able to assume its sister form at will. While in bat form, they have +2 Quickness and -4 Body (a minimum of 1 remaining). A vampire in bat form effectively cannot see. It uses active sonar to move around. Background


Evviva il coltello! - Cry To Heaven, Anne Rice


Late in the biting cold of a New York Christmas in 1980, a small boy approximately two or three was found hiding for warm in the dumpster outside of a police station. The almost delicate child could give no last name or home address. No reports of a missing child matched the boy who gave his name as 'Dory', either. Despite the best efforts of the state, his parents could not be located. The name Dorian was assigned to him as a best-guess on his original first name with the appellation Smith. The child ended up as so many others have, fallen between the cracks, and shipped from one foster home to another until he was seven.

It was then Stephen Marcello decided the time was right to expand the family. The youngest son of a corporate entity, he had long since given up on marrying -- they only saw his money. But he did want a child. Someone to teach his favorite 'hobby' to, someone to sing with, someone to watch take flight -- as his father had done with him. Stephen's money -- for once not cursed -- and the family reputation led to the quick adoption of Dorian Smith - now Marcello.

Stephen suffered from a well hidden set of delusions. He was obsessed with music and thieving. It was Stephen's obsession with music -- particularly the now mythical castrati -- drove him and the still delicately built boy eight year old into hours long lessons, leaving the private tutor for other days as often as not. It drove him to resurrect the castrati -- if only in one child. The Marcello fortune paid for the operation -- labeled a possible gang initiation attack on police records -- that protected Dorian's voice from the 'ravages' of puberty. The music lessons were pushed harder.

At age fourteen, the 'hobby' lessons began. Excursions to various cities or seedier areas of New York. Burglary sat as well with the lean adolescent as had the intensive music lessons. A careful grace fell into place about his movements, concern over his differences from normal boys vanished. He was special under the eye of his father, Stephen Marcello. That is all he needed.

Stephen retired from the field when diagnosed with too-far-gone cancer two years ago. He continued to monitor his adopted child's heists and musical practices until his death a year and a half later. For six months, Dorian Marcello was lost. His always available yet never pressing father would no longer be there to see him through. But Dorian had been raised to be strong.

Six months later saw Dorian on the road to a shabby house in a backwater town. Somewhere new, somewhere to start on his own.
