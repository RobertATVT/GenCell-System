3) Summary (what you want to happen)
 The entrance is simple. Double-doors; wooden and a little worse for wear, framed by solid oak posts and a plan exterior. Once the handles are grasped, you step into this small paradise.
To the left, there is a polished bar. The nicks and scratches have been painsakingly filled in and polished - in the dim light, they are barely noticable. Besides, no one is here to study these sort of things, are they? Barstools (hijacked from a horribly named place called 'Al's') line the enter row. Backless, with no ledges to rest your feet on. If you want to be comfortable, it will have to be at one of the twenty or so tables scattered around the floor.
The stage is a creation thought of, and built, by Trinity herself. Designed for easy access by the dancers, but quelling the idea of interaction by the men sure to visit. Smooth wooden panels make up the floor of the curvy stage. Not polished so heavily that a dancer might slip; but covered with a unique laquer that is gritty in nature. The edge of this winding stage is trimmed with a small, spiked fence, about four inches tall. The tips are blunted; so as not to cause serious harm, and the material it is made of glitters menacingly; a reminder that only the girls are allowed on stage. There are three places where this fence is not placed. The middle entrance of the stage (dead center of the room; the stage extending from there to the back wall, where there is a curtained entrance) and the two side steps. One snug against the left wall (steps leading right into the bartenders area); the other against the right wall (steps ending next to a small hall that holds two doors - one to the office, the other to a same sex bathroom.)
The bar consists only of domestic and imported beers. Two bartenders are budgeted for - and two bouncers. Under the bar, in three different places, are red buttons, designed to be used if and when the police are needed. When pressed, a computer located in the office will dial 911 and issue a plea for help giving all information to the operator.
Behind the bar, there is a large mirror, behind which there is a pane of bulletproof glass, allowing those in the small dressing room there to watch the action of the main club. From this small room, there is a narrow winding hallway, leading into the 'green room' (originally planned to be a stockroom). In the green room, there is a cabinet, readied with several bottles of Mace to be used if a customer gets.. overly rowdy.
In the office, a plain, wooden desk is set about five feet from the back wall. On it, there is a computer (internet ready), a phone and an answering machine. Two plants spice up the room a little; and it is in here that the only clear window is located; the others placed throughout the building having been covered with dark shades.
