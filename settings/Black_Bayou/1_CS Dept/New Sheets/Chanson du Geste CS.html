Special Abilities



Locust - 0 XP - 0 Soul (##TDF##)
System/Duration: NA/NA; takes 1 round to shapeshift
Effect/Description: A demon is capable of forcing its true form upon its mortal host body. A demon that assumes the locust form will rip free from its host body, physically. Their skin will crack and split, falling off of the demon in a macabre display. A typical demon in its locust form stands about 9 feet tall. Its skin is a deep reddish tint with long, stringy black hair. The body is covered in a hard carapace that helps protect it from physical harm. Its black lips curl up from the sickly-yellowed fangs as its eyeless sockets stare on. Black, leathery wings sprout from its back, enabling it to fly at a speed of 25 mph. Once the demon releases its locust form, the beast will shimmer slightly as the host's flesh slowly begins to reappear over the creature's features and it returns to its moral form. Locust form grants the demon +3 body and gives it the ability to fly (as described above).

(Just a quick note about shape changing (all forms). While a demon is in another form, if its current Body falls to 0, the demon will immediately change back to mortal form minus the damage it has already taken, leaving a minimum of 1 Body. It should also be noted that materiel objects do not shapeshift with the character. Any and all clothes and objects the character was holding will need to be transported by some other means.)

Liars Ears - 0 XP - 0 Soul (##LiarsEars##)
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Mind vs TN 22 / 10 minutes; takes 1 round to activate
Effect/Description: A demon, being a part of the lord of lies himself, is capable of picking out any attempts of deception directed at it. This is effective against anyone who attempts to lie to the demon. They must immediately IM the demon's player with the truth behind their lie.

Healing - 0 Soul to use. (no room command needed)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Effect/Description: Demons have an incredible natural healing ability. Any wound caused to the demon or its host body will heal at the rate of 1 Body per hour the character rests. If the character takes any strenuous action, the healing is disrupted and the hour starts over. A Room Host must be notified when you claim your Body point back. Wounds created by faith water cannot be healed with this mastery. Wounds from fire heal at the rate of 1 Body per day and only when the character sleeps. If the character is woken up before the day is over, the healing process is disrupted and they do not regain their point (which means they cannot role-play until the damage is healed). The damage from an attack that removes a limb heals at the normal rate, but the player must role-play the limb slowly growing back. (Length of time for the limb to regenerate is determined by the Forum Director.)

Mindspeak - 0 Soul to use. (>>::target of Mindspeak:: message<<)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Effect/Description: By concentrating on a chosen target, the demon is capable of speaking directly to the creature's mind. Mortals are unable to return the thoughts. The target must be within view of the demon attempting to communicate in this fashion. Mindspeak can only be used to speak to one individual at a time.

Masteries



Soul Mask - 1 Soul to use. (##SoulMask ##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 20 / Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: This is the ability to hide a demon breed from those with the ability to Scrye such things. Whether it is a lamia's spell, a werewolf's scent or even an angel's true sight. The demon chooses the breed it wants to be detected as.

Fetish - 1 Soul to use. (##Fetish ##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs 2d6+Will / 4 rounds (combat) or Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: In visual sight of the victim, the demon may cause their hidden desires to manifest to the point that they act upon them. The priest with a long-hidden penchant for fish net might find himself unable to resist wearing it to 'mass'. The recovered alcoholic may suddenly fall off the wagon, drinking himself into a stupor. The victim's Will modifier is at -2 for the duration.

Illusions - 1 Soul to use. (##Illusions ##)
Generation Cost: 5 per level / Experience Cost: 25 per level (maximum level: 5)
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs victim's 2d6+Will / Scene; takes 2 rounds to activate.
Effect/Description: Each level purchased grants the demon the ability to create an illusion with an additional sense. Level 1 is sound, 2 is sight, 3 is smell, 4 is taste, 5 is touch. A demon can make use of any of the senses he has for a single illusion, i.e., a demon with level 2 could make a fire that could be heard and seen, while one with level three could make a fire that could be heard and seen, as well as smell the wood burning. At level 5, actual attacks can be made. This is done using the demon's stats and skills, though a Will roll is still made to see if the illusion is created in the first place and believed. Damage for the skill used is halved, (rounded down) and done to the mind, though the target will see their own blood etc. Unless dispelled by the demon, mindspeak wont break through to someone being effected by an Illusion, however, Hypnotic Suggestion would cancel out the effects. A round of concentration is required to form the Illusion. On the next round, the demon sends the Illusion out to be heard, seen, smelled, tasted, or touched. Many Illusions can be created with this mastery, however, a new roll and another Soul point spent would be in order. *Note: If the Illusions effect individuals, then they totally believe in it. Meaning the Illusions has the ability to affect them physically. For instance, if a demon creates a deafening, high pitched sound, it would hamper or possibly damage the hearing of its victim. If the demon created a bright flash of light, the victim may be temporarily blinded. Once the Illusion is over, the victim regains their Mind points, as the effect is over.


Physical Description


Chanson (or 'Chance', as she prefers to be called) is 5'3", 105#, slim and wiry, with the figure of a china doll. A headful of short, curly sandy-blonde ringlets frame a creamy face; sharp, frighteningly intelligent violet-blue eyes peer out from behind round-framed, wire-rimmed glasses. A pert, cute nose sits above rosebud lips and a tiny, foxy chin; high cheekbones frame the eyes. She dresses casually at most times--jeans, polo shirts, a worn jeans jacket with many pockets. Three things she has with her at all times are a high-quality 35mm camera on a strap around her neck with several lenses stashed in her pockets, a microcassette tape recorder with spare tapes, and a notepad and several pens. She wears little jewelry, save for a plain silver chain around her neck that carries her college class ring.

 In True Demon Form, Chance--who has merged with the demon Carnivean--looks far, far different. Carnivean, formerly an angel of the Order known as Dominions, now Fallen, is one of the Pancubi, similar to Incubi (male) and Succubi (female), save that it can be both, depending on what it wishes. (In other words, the pancubi have sexual organs of both genders, though only one gender is shown at a time). In myth and legendry, the pancubi were able to present themselves as female for Male mortals, have sex with them, and collect the male mortal's sperm within them, then change to a Male form and use that sperm--now tainted by Hell's Abyssal energies--to father a half-demon child on a mortal woman. Thus, the legend of Merlin, spawned by a demon father on a mortal woman, and many other tales of demon-spawned heroes and villains in legends and myths. Carnivean is incapable of fathering children in this way nowdays, and Chance of bearing them, but the ability remains.
In either gender, Carnivean's skin is a deep scarlet, like a sunset sky, with black, retractable claws like a cat's on her fingers. Descending from her spine is a black-furred tail, also like a cat's, but prehensile. Carnivean's feet are taloned and black-scaled, like a dragon's, the scarlet skin shading to black at the feet and tail and base of the wings. Carnivean's yellow eyes are slitted like a cat's. The curling horns of a ram, black and glossy, surmount Carnivean's head, and it bears two sets of wings atop its back that are those of the angel it once was, one thickly black-feathered, the other leathery, tattered, and black as midnight. Finally, a lush, thick mane of raven hair falls from the crown of Carnivean's head to mid-calf, the tendrils seeming to billow softly, as if blown by a gentle breeze...or as if they were alive. Carnivean is a sexual demon, excruciatingly so, and every move is taken with grace so exquisite it is nearly painful to watch, seeming to radiate an aura of Hellish allure with every step it takes. In TDF, in either gender, Carnivean/Chance is exceptionally beautiful, but the demon's features are not Chance's, and when viewing her in TDF, she is not recognizable as Chanson du Geste.

Background


Originally from Isle Voletta, Chanson graduated from one of the finest colleges in the nation, at the top of her class, an English major with a talent for writing hardcore news. Then--so some said--she threw away a promising career as a reporter to write for the scandal sheets. She thrived on gossip, rumors, idle chit-chat, and whispered innuendos--the dirtier, the better. Her stories sold more copies of a tabloid in an issue than most 'decent' newspapers sold in a year.
After five years, Chanson was working for the National Enquirer for over $75,000 a year--and quit when, in her words, 'the rag started getting too respectable'. Disgusted with such a turn, she decided to come back to Voletta to see what had happened in town while she was gone. She returned to find her family broken apart--her parents divorced, her brother in jail. More amused by the scandals in her own family than upset, she decided to stay around for a while...to see if she can find a story that will bring her back to the work she loves.
