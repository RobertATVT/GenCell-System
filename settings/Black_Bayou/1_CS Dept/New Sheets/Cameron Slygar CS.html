~BACKGROUND~


Cameron Slygar was born in Charleston, South Carolina, on March 12th, 1966. His father, Robert C. Slygar was not around much in the first six years of Cameron's life. Being a naval aviator, his father was deployed to Vietnam early and pulled multiple tours of duty there, remaining until the withdrawl in October 1972. These early memories of his father in uniform would in fact guide Cameron for years to come. He quickly became involved with the Naval Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps. at Fort Dorchester High School, where he graduated with honors in the top five percent of his class. He carried on to the next logical step, attending college at Furman University in Greenville where he graduated in 1988 with a BA in Criminology. Cmdr. Slygar also holds a degree in Government and Public Administration from George Washington University in Washington, DC.

After his commission in April of 1989, his first assignment was with the Naval Investigative Service (NIS) in Yokosuka, Japan, where he served as a counterintelligence analyst and command administrative officer.

While attending the Defense Intelligence College in 1990, Cameron became an investigations specialist. After serving in the Pentagon as an analyst and supervisor of International Terrorism Investigations Detail, Cmdr Slygar was assigned as Force Intelligence Officer for Commander, Middle East Force Staff embarked on the USS La Salle homeported in the Persian Gulf.

After a brief tour in Washington on the CNO's intelligence staff in the Pentagon, Cameron was sent back to sea in 1992 as Fleet Intelligence Officer (N-2) for Commander Sixth Fleet aboard USS Blue Ridge, Cmdr Slygar spent a year deployed to the Persian Gulf before rotating back to the States where he spent a year at the National Defense University's Industrial College of the Armed Forces and earning an MPA from George Washington University, Cmdr Slygar served for one year as Executive Assistant to the Director of Naval Intelligence.

Leaving the USN, Cameron returned home to Charleston for a few months before deciding to get out of the hussle of the big city. He took a vacation during Mardi Gras and ended of discovering Isle Voletta where he decided to settle down and seek employment with the local law enforcement.

Cameron's law enforcement career has seen many turns since his arrival in Voletta. Being hired on as a deputy almost immediately, he was then drafted by the US Marshal's service for a stint with them. Upon the Marshal's withdrawl from Voletta due to the high casuality rate of it's people, he returned to the SD and was named Sheriff when the acting sheriff at that time stepped down. He held that position for a respectable period of time before deciding that Voletta had been tamed as much as it could and moving on to be the Chief of Police in nearby Bristle, Louisiana where he purchased a home. The desk job proved too boring for him, so once again he returned to Voletta as a deputy. His latest job change is to the position he desired all along, but not under the circumstances he had hoped for. With the death of Andrew Trask as SAC, the FBI came looking to replace him and increase the agency's presence on the Isle. Cameron and his wife Catherine both stepped up to the challange, and when it was all said and done, were the newest Special Agents for the FBI.
