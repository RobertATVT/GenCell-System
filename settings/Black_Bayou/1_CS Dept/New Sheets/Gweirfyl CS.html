Physical Description


Too many smokers around town seem to pick up a cigarette without any of the usual adverse affects of years of smoking. How do they do it? Well, the miracle has been found. They must be transferring their problems onto the slightly stooped shoulders of our girl, Denise. Spun on the wings of gossamer smoke, she hardly noticed the rasping of her voice, the sickly cough, and the yellowed stain of nicotine on her fingertips until it was too late, and she was solidly deposited in the realm of the constant smoker. Otherwise, she is what might be called pretty, though her charm lies in her breathiness, the soft rasp of a voice and the thin wisp of her frame. She knows her beauty's strength lies in that very thinness of her body, and so clothes herself in thin-knitted sweaters and ragged cutoffs. Too often she hid wave-blue eyes behind the curtain of her puddle-brown hair, but now that it is cut into a ragamuffin's mop to match her oddly styled clothes, she no longer has that problem, learning instead to meet her challenges head on, like she should have in the first place.

She has two tattoos, each on the inside of her ankle: a thistle on her left, a rose on her right.


Background


Darkness wreathed the house where the girls slept, but they were not afraid of the dark. Instead, the stench of liquor coyly wrapped itself around their dreams, holding them in a constant half-sleep, ready to defend themselves against the drink's power. Their father was always the instrument, a large man chosen as a weapon for his weakness for the erasure of pain and a horrible temper that none could control. The sweet woman he married was soon transformed by the drink as well -- she became useless to defend, even in the face of her daughter's peril.
She remained so even when the dam broke, and one of Denise's "infractions" finally turned her father's full potential as an abuser towards her. One hit was harder than the others, and hit just the right spot, and suddenly Denise was still, looking lifeless on the floor. Her sister -- her twin, her partner in running away from the problem that was her father -- guarded her until the paramedics came, the one who defended her sister to the end.
Rose, the twin, continued to think that Denise was dead -- that was what she was told, and that is what she had to believe. However, her parents knew that Denise had been taken to a foster home, then bounced around a few other places before she reached eighteen and suddenly.. disappeared.
She did not really disappear, certainly, merely faded from sight, traveling the country and investigating other cultures and countries outside the borders of America. As she went, love affairs bloomed and wilted, friendships started and just as suddenly stopped. The only thing that remained true was her determination to find her sister, wherever she had planted herself in the world.
Denise finally found her twin in Isle Voletta, Louisiana, and found the home she'd been searching for. Now she has the semblance of a life she'd only dreamed of having, and a twin to share it with. Perhaps Denise has finally found a place in which she can win.
