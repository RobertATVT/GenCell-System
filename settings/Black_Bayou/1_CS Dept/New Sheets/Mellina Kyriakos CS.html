Physical Description


Rich waves of hair the color of black-cherry wine fall in luxurious silken ringlets past slim shoulders the color of slightly-aged ivory. Laughing, darkly mischievous eyes the same color as ripe olives, a green so dark they are almost black, twinkle beneath a thick fringe of sable lashes. The curvaceous form would shame Aphrodite herself, and Mellina often goes clad in simple shepherd's clothing--tunics and smocks, sandals, drawstring pants stained with paint or grass; the slender limbs are tautly muscular after years of martial-arts training, the smooth skin unmarked save for a single scar midway across her right upper arm where a practice sword struck too hard during a bout when she was 16, leaving its mark in her flesh when the bamboo bokken split and splintered, gouging open her fair flesh. Mellina disdains the use of makeup, needing none to grace her exotic, sensual features, and the only jewelry she wears is her mother's wedding ring, a simple silver band, and that on a thin silver chain around her swanlike throat.


Background


Mellina Ariadne Kyriakos loved her mother with an undying fervor comparable to that of the Amazons for their Queen Hippolyta. Born of Greek immigrant parents, her father died shortly after Mellina was born in a fire on the oil tanker on which he worked as an engineer. Mellina's mother Danae took it upon herself to raise Mellina alone, without male assistance, a task for which she was eminently qualified. Danae Kyriakos, nee Aristedes, had been a silver medal winner at the 1968 Olympics, taking second place in Judo for Greece. She brought her daughter up with love but with firm discipline, teaching her the philosophy of the martial arts, then sending her to Japan for training in a more refined and pure form than she had learned herself--not Judo, but Chi. The training helped to forge Mellina into a modern-day Amazon--no ugly, brutal, gruff warrior, but a light-hearted, agile, playful, and sensual woman who could take care of herself.
Mellina is an intensely private person who does not make friends easily, but treasures the ones she has. She is of only average intelligence, but determined studies have enabled her, aside from her martial-arts training and artistic pursuits, to have earned a degree in Art History. She has also picked up several languages other than English and her parents' Greek: Japanese, during her studies in Osaka, Tokyo, and Kyoto; Spanish, Latin, and German. She is an outspoken, self-confident woman who does not hesitate to speak her mind or stand up for what she believes is right, despite what it may cost her.
