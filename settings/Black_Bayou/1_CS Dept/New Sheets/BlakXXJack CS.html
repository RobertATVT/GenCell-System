Physical Description

Jack has short, dark brown hair and striking gray-green eyes. Of a thin stature and above-average height, he's not very physically intimidating. His looks and charm, however, are usually enough to help him avoid trouble, and so Jack has never had to develop much muscle-wise.


Background

Jacob Dominic Barikson was born October 27, 1975. He grew up and spent most of his life in New York City, the son of Maria and John Barikson, who were both heavily involved with drug smuggling and dealing. When Jack was 11, both of his parents were shot. Their bodies were left in the family's house, and Jack found them when he came home after school. He was taken into an orphanage, but never really settled in, and when Jack was 13, he ran away from the orphanage, living on the streets. His sharp mind made up for his lack of physical ability, and after years of his parents' sheltering him from criminal life, he walked down their road. By the time Jack was 20, he was a savvy ringleader who ran his gang through sheer guile. At 21, he was killed, or so his enemies thought.
In one of the many suburban areas of the city, Jack was run down by a rival gang leader's lackeys, hit full-on by a car he never saw any of, save the glow of the headlights. He was left for dead, his legs and lower back so thoroughly devastated by the crash that walking would have been impossible. Jack regained consciousness, and through force of will, did the impossible. Crawling and walking in alteration, he made it to his personal physician, who arranged for him to get out of New York by plane. His legs healed over a course of months, and when they were healed completely, he began to wander the nation, moving away from his life of crime and making up for the end of his education by studying everything he could in his meandering.
After a year of these wanderings, Jack Barikson arrived in Isle Voletta, Louisiana. Soon after his arrival, he was contacted by demonic forces who offered him power in exchange for servitude, and he eagerly accepted. Gradually, his focus of attaining power for himself changed to a strange religious fervor, in which he saw his duty as preparing the world for hell, by making people as perverse as possible; his logic was that if someone could become as inhumanly corrupt as possible, hell would be paradise to them. His memory of a year and a half of demonic servitude is shoddy; he knows that at the end of it, his possessor ascended back to heaven, regaining its status as an angel. Jack was offered the chance to follow in its ascent and declined, wanting to continue his mission, his psyche firmly directed to service in hell's ranks, even if he was no longer among them.
The next six months were taken up in finding himself again, spent trying to gather the shambles of his life after the newly ascendant angel reworked Jack's memory "for his own good." Many of the memories of his wife Fiona were taken away, as well as other residents of Isle Voletta; all of his memory of his powers as a demon, and of hell, were taken, and now Jack has only the most basic idea of what he was. Now that Jack feels he has the pieces put together, he's returned to Voletta to put his life back together, with a new light on his life: he serves hell, but no demon. He's a free agent of sorts, even if he's a weak mortal once more. Now he can make his moves with full freedom.
