Background


He was born in Louisianna. More specifically, he was born in New Orleans. His father was a Marine and his mother was a SeaBee. The military family lived happily, except that young Will was growing up to be a problem. Despite the fact that the family normally attended the non-denominational church on the base, Will was without guidance or remorse for almost all of his actions. It wasn't exactly easy to be a military teen either. When he hit 16 in 1962, his father more or less shipped him off to his grandmother's.

Even this failed to help the troubled youth. He became a well known trouble-maker in small Tennessee community. He spent more time in lock-up in those two years tghan in school...but somehow he graduated.

By this time, his father had made a name for himself in AirCav. And there was no choice....Will enlisted. With prodding and beatings from his Full Colonel father, he made 2nd Lt. and was assigned to the 31st...shortly before America became involved in the Vietnam War. It was shortly before he shipped off that he met and married the woman that would be his future wife.

His record during the war was exemplary. He even managed to make Captain. However, his recorded land kills came not from enemies, but rather from small rice-paddy villages. He was a maniacal pilot, willing to shoot anything and everything that moved in front of his twin guns. His crew soon became known as "Hell's Fire", a quote used by the regiment chaplan when describing the destination of these 4 men.

It all came back to him.

The Chopper was shot down by a small Vietcong force over a village one day. The bad luck was that they had just torn up the small village, and when he was fished out of the paddy, they attacked. Over 50 of the people he had just shot at beat the flight crew mercilessly. One managed to crudely carve the letters on his flight patch into his bicep. Another unsuccessfully slit his wrists. By the time the NVA got there, two of his three other flight members were dead at the hands of the villagers. His machine-gunner, an enlisted man, was dragged off to a prison. Will was taken to a seperate camp.

He was released shortly afterwards, when the NVA found out his father was now heading up many of the AirCav Regiments in the area. It was part of a deal cut. He was brought quietly home, and nursed back to health. Not by his wife, but rather by a local minister. His wife had left him, filing for divorce the same day he set foot on American soil.

So the natural solution was to turn to the bottle. At this point, it was 1974, and he was approaching 30. The images flooded back to him, the looks on the faces that he gunned down as he made low passes. Until one night when he made a suicide attempt. In a dingy Los Angeles hotel room, he slit one of his wrists. He later managed to drive himself to the ER. And was referred to a help group. The help group was located in a church.

He went for one group..and one sermon...and at the age of 35, he went to the seminary.

And at the age of 50, he found himself at the Voletta Southern Baptist Church. Assigned by the Southern Baptist Convention, he's spent the last four years raising membership. Now it was time to try his luck...
