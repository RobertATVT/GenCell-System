Background


Born on New Year's Day, 1970 to Charles and Delores MacKenzie, of the New Hampshire MacKenzies, she was the middle of 5 children. Her other siblings, all brothers (Charles Jr., Phillip, Henry and Louis in order by age) were raised to go into various political/banking careers. Boys were essential to Charles MacKenzie continuing the family dynasty of banking and politics in the New England arena. Girls were decorative and useful only if good marriages could be made for them. Patricia was shuttled off to boarding school early on and later to the better colleges, Radcliffe, Vassar and eventually graduated with a law degree from Harvard. After graduation, she shunned her parents attempts at matchmaking her with the elitists and preferred the company of taxi drivers and bartenders... basically for the stories they could tell her... they had intrinsic entertainment value.

Upon graduation from law school, she came into her inheritance as set up by her grandmother and decided to set up a practice in a small town so that she could actually learn her trade by doing. If she screwed up, it would be by her own mistakes, and not those of large firms that wanted to work her 20 hours a day while they took 90% of the money she earned for them.

She moved to Belize, Maine where she made countless mistakes but learned a great deal about a lot of things... lawyering being one of them. It was in Belize that she discovered a woman that showed her how to earn millions of dollars tax free annually. She represented the woman in a number of lawsuits, winning a few, losing a few, and learning a lot.

After a while of this, she decided to try the woman's trade... and discovered she was quite good at it. And wouldn't Daddy just be surprised to find out just how good his little girl could be when she really applied herself. Deciding that the winters of New England were just too much for a person of her new stock and trade, she moved to a sleepy little town in the bayou area of Louisiana where she could set up a network similar to what her friend in Belize, Maine ran... and with Patricia's drive, she would only settle for being the best. And when you're an expensive call girl, it's a definite plus to be the best.
