Description:
The brown bear lives atop her head, leaping in fine tendrils over a peach forehead and galavanting across elf-like ears. Eyes much too dark to be blue glare into the night, surrounded by heavenly darkness. Lips, tainted a nectar of light peach(brown if the eye chooses so), curve in a most sensual way. Is this the fox that she claims to be? The fox that can bare teeth if need be. The red fox that speeds through forests at lightning speed and gallops over obstacles as if they were rocks? Yes, this is her. The legendary fox, ready for battle. What looks say, they are not. The cute n' fuzzy fox may cause the next body rolling into the morgue.


BG:

An adopted child, she never knew who her parents were. Left at the Maybury Hospital for Children when only the age of three months old. They were Jason and Iveta de Massard, which went on to Cloe's name of Massard. Her name was attached to the anklet found about her ankle. Cloe Rose de Massard. French that is. Sister Margarette of the local church(St. Peter's) found her upon the doorstep twenty seven years ago, crying out for the love she needed, and picked the bundle up. From then on, she was under the Sister's care.
Kept at the convent, one would expect Cloe to live a pure life, but the sisters taught her more than she bargained for. More than she needed to know. On December 25, 1986, when Cloe was 16, the Father of their convent was found murdered. He had been "stabbed" twice in the neck, and all the blood has been drained. However, there was none left on the pavement, and he was excessively pale. Cloe had read about this, about beings called "vampires", about the myths, and about the facts. She had had her Cross(fashioning Jesus dying on the cross) blessed the day after by Deacon Fitzgerald. The sisters were mournful for years after this loss, and tried to tell Cloe that it was not vampires, though she insisted. She began to read. Every book she could lay her hands on, she read of these monsters. The Roman Catholic religion was guiding her to Christ, yet she was stone to their teachings. She did not believe in half of the rituals, or sacrifices. She believed in her relationship with God, and the knowledge that evil was more abundant than good. The cross never leaves her neck, nor does the vial of holy water(Silly myth). She gave up the garlic at the age of nine. Now she is on a journey, having left the convent behind(though they had begged her to stay), to find these creatures, to speak with them, and to avenge the Father's death.
