Background


Roman Slavotski lead his small family from one gaming palace to another for nineteen years. His wife didn't make it the whole way, dying when their only son was barely nine years old. Zadiel, when he thinks of her, remembers only a tiny, sad doll that went through the motions of motherhood, religion and marriage. She left only her name to her son in the end. Her husband never kept the promise to raise him in her faith. Roman taught his son everything, keeping them on the road, and away from authorities that would take the frail boy from his father to put him through school. As a result, when Zadiel left his father's company nearly two years ago, he only had the reading, writing and math skills to handle daily life. He strangled the roving desire long enough to learn more, landing a GED. Then it was on the road, losing contact with his father mostly until a year ago. He was tracked down by police to claim what was left of his father's belongings, after Roman was shot for cheating in a private game. He turned from his western trek to head down into Georgia, claiming the possessions. He arranged for the burial with money won in a similar game, without the cheating. Then he was on the road again, trying to part with the last fading memories of childhood. His wandering path lead him into Voletta, with little money left to spare.
