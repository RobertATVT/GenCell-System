Empathy - 0 Soul to use. (##Empathy ##)

Effect/Description: Psychics have learned to read the facial expressions and body language of others. These insightful individuals can occasionally pinpoint someone else's emotional state just by observing them for a few seconds. The victim must IM the mortal, describing their emotional state. Angry, aroused, excited, and distraught are only examples.

Meditation - 0 Soul to use. (##Meditation##)
System/Duration: NA / see below; takes 15 minutes to activate.
Effect/Description: In order to center themselves and replenish their mental energies after utilizing the power of the mind, a psychic needs to shut themselves off from the world around them and meditate. By sitting alone and relaxing, concentrating on a candle flame, or some sort of talisman while chanting, the psychic can slowly restore their soul. They must meditate in the main room. If they are interrupted (legitimately) for even a moment, the meditation must begin anew. 1 Soul Point may be regained each quarter-hour, going no higher than the original level.

Mind Shield - 0 Soul to use. (##MindShield##)
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 18 / Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: It is difficult for a Psychic to keep their wits about them, and even more difficult when outside forces try to penetrate their mind. By blocking everything out, they can prevent anyone from manipulating their mind, thus adding +3 to their defensive Will rolls. Because the psychic is so mentally adept, no concentration is necessary to keep this mastery active. It may only be used once per Scene, but psychics can use any other mastery they own or act as they wish while this one is active.

Physical Description


Grayson has the body of a swimmer, streamlined and fit. He has been swimming since he could walk, and it shows. He is just over six feet tall with short-cropped, dark blond hair and pale blue eyes. He has high cheekbones, a strong jawline, and features that have a sort of clarity (for lack of a better word). His skin is fair and burns easily.


Background


Grayson Hardwick was born in Southern California on August 28, 1976 - the Feastday of Saint Augustine of Hippo. His birthday is appropriate to the Feastday of the patron saint of brewers as his life rather closely mirrors it. He comes from a Catholic family and he has a keen grasp of theology but his life does not reflect its spiritual heritage. He was in the local parish boys choir, and though he dropped out to join a local gang, his love of music has not changed. He was involved only in small-time local gangs in his youth, but his parents rectified that when they found a handgun in his room. He was intelligent enough to earn acceptance to Tulane University where he majored in English and concentrated in creative writing. He spent much of his time drunk (more even than the average student at Tulane), high, and engaged in any number of unsavory activities.

He also had an interest in mythology and did his senior thesis on vampiric literature. He went to the Folklore Institute at the University of Indiana in Bloomington in order to do research and found a dusty tome in their Special Collections Department that recounted the history of certain warrior-monks who hunted vampires and witches and some of their methods of harming the Undead. He filched it.

Now graduated, Grayson wants to be a writer, but he acknowledges that he has not experienced enough of life to write anything worthwhile or interesting. From his forays into the darker quarters of New Orleans he has heard rumors about Isle Voletta, and some of his research has led him there as well. He has come to live a life worthy of writing about.
