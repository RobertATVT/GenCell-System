Lore

Name Level

1) Vampire Lore Level 3

When a vampire uses a mental mastery with you as the target within line of sight from you, roll 2d6+Mind+3 vs. a TN: 20. If you succeed, you recognize the ability and its effect, giving you a one time +5 to the resistance roll (if any) for that mastery. (masteries requiring the vampire use Will, Mind or Soul rolls only; cannot be used while in direct combat).

Wooden Stakes: To kill a vampire, they must first be brought down to the negative total of their body statistic. A body of 8 would have to be brought down to -8, for instance. Once this is accomplished, it becomes possible to get around the vampire's natural healing abilities. Vampires happen to be susceptible to a wooden stake that penetrates the heart. The stake must be a piece of wood no smaller than a foot in length and an inch in diameter. Once the appropriate body number is achieved, the attacker must place the point of the stake over the vampire's heart. A full round of uninterrupted concentration is necessary as they summon their faith, empowering the stake. This is when a roll of 2d6+Soul+Faith vs TN 26 must be rolled successfully. A ST or GM must be present. If the roll is successful, the stake must be driven through the vampire's heart on the round following the roll. The empowerment does not last any longer than that. No character may attempt to empower a killing weapon more than once an hour. As the stake passes through the vampire's heart, the faith the character used to empower it is released, spreading throughout the vampire's body. This causes the body to burn internally and on the following round, the flames will erupt around the body. Seconds later, nothing will remain of the body as the ashes are extremely thin, disintegrating with any kind of contact. Since a vampire's body has to be in the negative when they are staked, they are obviously considered prone. No attack roll is necessary.

 2) Werewolf Lore Level 2

Lore Level 2: You have done an incredible amount of reading on the subject of werewolves. Having read everything from the local booksellers', to the more arcane legends, you believe to know the truth. Most of this information is, of course, false. You believe that any type of silver can kill a werewolf, once it enters the bloodstream. You also know that werewolves can shape shift into the form of a wolf. You still believe, however, that the werewolves' change into the monstrous form is related only to the full moon. You also realize merely touching silver has no effect on them. Having studied werewolves for so long, you've begun to pick up on their more subtle abilities. When a werewolf uses a mental mastery with you as the target within line of sight from you, roll 2d6+Mind+2 vs. a TN: 20. If you succeed, you recognize the ability and its effect, giving you a one time +3 to the resistance roll (if any) for that mastery. (masteries requiring the werewolf use Will, Mind or Soul rolls only; cannot be used while in direct combat).

Bane Water: Bane water is little more than water used to boil the wolfsbane herb that has been empowered with the character's faith. It takes 10 minutes to empower enough water for 1 attack. The water must have been used to boil wolfsbane and posted in the ST's Lair message board area each day it is used. The empowering with faith must be role-played in the main room for the entire 10 minutes for it to work. The character must put the bane water in whatever item they intend to attack the werewolf with prior to the empowering. These objects can be any small item that can hold water. Balloons, vials, squirt guns, etc. Only 3 attacks worth of bane water may be empowered at one time. If the character attempts to make more than 3 attacks worth, the first amount looses its ability, etc. The empowered water may only be used by the individual that created it. The roll to empower the water is 2d6+Soul+Faith vs TN 22. This roll must be witnessed and approved my either a room host, ST or GM. Attacks made against the werewolf are made as normal, depending on the method being used to hold the water. For instance, balloons and vials are thrown while squirt guns use the small arms skill. If an attack is successful, the werewolf suffers 1 point of damage that cannot be healed supernaturally. The werewolf must heal the damage as any normal mortal would. That would mean each point of damage would take 3 days. Major Medical is the only medical skill that will help the werewolf heal faster at the maximum rate of x2.

Background


Shannon Fortner was born and raised in Louisville, KY on April 18, 1968. She
graduated high school in Louisville and won a scholarship to Julliard majoring in
drama. With a taste for musical comedy, she began auditioning at various
Village theaters and landed several small to mid-range parts. The critics told her
that while her acting was acceptable, her voice was exceptional. While she
didn't really believe the critics (because she wanted to be an actress) she started
accepting jobs as a singer in several small nightclubs.

The visibility eventually paid off and she began getting small parts on Broadway -
Hair, Anything Goes, Company, and a short stint in Cats were among her credits.
She moved in with her boyfriend, Antonio (Tony) Dubocelli a locally well known,
and moderately successful songwriter and began performing his material at clubs
such as the Blue Note.

After living together for 8 months, Tony and Shannon were going to be married.
Their families were very much against the marriage... the Dubocelli's did not want
Tony to marry an actress... "Wives should be wives and stay home and raise the
babies!", Tony's mother screamed. Shannon's parents still preferred to think of
her occupation as a hobby and feared a marriage to a songwriter would keep her
in New York and away from them. Tragedy intervened when Tony was killed in a
car wreck 2 weeks before the marriage was scheduled to take place.

Shannon was devastated and could not bear staying on in NYC without her Tony.
She disposed of the apartment in New York and flew back to KY. But there was
no consolation in KY for her. Her parents felt Tony's death was a blessing and
did not try to hide their pleasure. Furthermore, all of Shannon's friends had
moved or were heavily tied up with their own lives.

Finally she remembered Tony telling her of a small city in Louisiana he had
discovered in his travels before they had met. It had been a source of great
inspiration for Tony and Shannon decided that this little spot might possibly
inspire her too. So she packed her bags again and set out for Isle Voletta,
Louisiana.
