Physical Description

Ashkelon, or Ash, as he prefers to be called, is tall and lithe, moving with a feline grace. He wears the color black almost uniformly--mostly silk and leather--with the very rare touch of either scarlet or white (scarves, etc.). His hair falls to just past his shoulders, mostly straight, though the ends tend to curl around his neck; it tangles easily in the wind, giving him a deliciously rumpled look. Shoes are usually Doc Martins; he wears no visible jewelry save for a single silver stud in his right earlobe. His nails are painted black, and he smokes clove cigarettes and vanilla bidis. He could be considered to look goth, except that he seems to project an aura of being above that. His skin is pale, and he has no scars or tattoos as distinguishing marks.


Background


 In these jaded times, Ashkelon Sain could be considered a throwback to the days of the Renaissance Man, centuries ago. A dabbler, his formal education is not quite complete, and stretches over numerous different fields--medicine, botany, law, literature, art, cooking, psychology. But his interests lay specifically in two fields; medicine--surgery, to be precise--and criminal justice.
Not at all unusual, since Ash is a serial killer.
Ashkelon Sain was born to a wealthy family in Seattle--stomping grounds to the likes of Ted Bundy, the Green River Killer, and Kenneth Bianchi (of the Hillside Strangler duo) for a time--and lived there throughout his childhood. A charming, intelligent child, Ash was good at impressing his peers and adults with his sharp mind, quick wit, and good looks; however, once the original, surface impression had faded, it became obvious that deeper down, he was quiet and somewhat insecure, though masking it well. He had no close friends, and no long-term romantic relationships in his teens and early twenties. His parents were worried about a number of problems that had manifested themselves as far back as his toddler years, including bed-wetting and setting small fires; hidden from them by Ash was also catching, torturing, mutilating, and killing small animals from the age of six.
His first killing took place while he was still in high school. Cruising the strip one night in the car his father had given him for his 16th birthday, a black 1960 Corvette, he picked up a girl on a street corner for some cheap sex, thinking her a prostitute. She turned out to be a runaway instead, and after having sex in an abandoned and condemned house in one of the poorer neighborhoods in Seattle, the same urges he had felt when torturing the animals overtook him. He strangled her, then cut up the body and dumped the pieces down an old well in the house's basement. The body wasn't discovered for over three years, by which time he had gone on to more of the same.
Ashkelon has attended various colleges, earning most of a medical degree; he lacks just 6 credits to be ready to practice. Most of his knowledge is put to use for his true passion, murder; he has become quite proficient at seducing women, drugging them with his large collection of stolen drugs, then carving them apart like a master surgeon. His interests in criminology and forensic pathology have given him valuable information on how to make certain that he leaves nothing on the bodies to be traced back to him--fibers, body fluids, fingerprints, etc.--and indeed, most of the time the bodies are completely destroyed. He carves certain portions of the muscles--the 'meat'--to eat, and the rest is ground up in meat grinders, incinerated, or fed to the gators.
A true wanderer, Ash has moved from Seattle to San Diego, and from there to Chicago, then New Orleans--as well as many small towns in between--before finally ending up in Isle Voletta. Like the aforementioned Ted Bundy, he is charming and intelligent, and has left a trail of bodies in his wake: 11 in Seattle, 7 in San Diego, 9 in Chicago, 6 in New Orleans--and now two already in Voletta, making him one of the more prolific killers of modern times, at a total of 35 and counting. His preferred victims are women--though he will, of course, kill anyone unlucky enough to catch his eye.
