Background


 There's an old legend surrounding the old Venetian House of Cortano, a legend that has thus far flourished barely under three-hundred years. It has to do with what is now known throughout modern-day Venice as myth and what the ancient Venetian State proclaimed as divine. Whether the former, latter, or a combination of both, it is solid fact that despite the efforts of history the walls of the house of Cortano may have cracked a time or two over the centuries, but they have never crumbled.
The whispering winds of Italy say that it all began in the year of our Lord 1715 with the entrance of the family's first daughter for generations into the world. By that time the name Cortano had been in the Golden Book for ages, ancestors bearing the name from the very beginning of Venice's rise to supremacy hundreds of years before. And so it was merely a matter of the sole daughter, to be called Moira, coming of an age to be entered into the most influential convent until she was of a marriageable age. While under the care of the nunnery, Moira birthed a prodigious talent for history and a devotion to the Christian God. The years swept by; the Venetian State continued to decline; and in an effort to gain back the Cortano wealth, Moira was married off to one Giambattista Antonio San'Camiel, whose family was well known for its fortune if not for its high nobility. And as fortuna smiled, the joining of Tonio and Moira turned out to be prosperous both publicly and privately; for unlike many arranged marriages, Moira discovered in Tonio her true love as he did in her.
In 1734, three years proceeding the marriage of Moira and Tonio and the year of Moira's nineteenth birthday, Fortune faltered in her blessing. There was an insult made regarding Moira's apparent barren state; there was an illegal duel fought; there was San'Camiel honor betrayed; and Moira's beloved Tonio died as a result. The House of Cortano and the patriarch of the San'Camiels combined forces and went to war against the offender and his family, and although they succeeded with their now legendary vendetta, the grieving, beloved daughter of Cortano had disappeared in the chaos.
Decades passed, the familia Cortano bled on, and the famous Republic of Venezia gnawed upon its own edges until it ate itself whole when conquered and ended by Napoleon Bonaparte in 1797. When Napoleon in turn gave the city over to Austria, the Cortanos were nearly snuffed out. The once massive and praised ranks had been depleted down to a single male heir, who was only nine years old; and Venice nearly wept as it foresaw the near extinction of one its oldest and most affluent families. But then out of the smoke and grief, with an undeniable physical resemblance to the departed daughter of the house, came a savior. The citizens tittered over how young she appeared, how unusual her claims of blood to the Cortanos, but nobody dared to contest the lady's claims of status and cast the final straw that would make the House of Cortano die out forever. She came in, took over the raising of the heir Phillipo and the care of the family fortune, and vanished the very day Phillipo came of an age to preside over the growing-again family. And so the stories, the myth, began; for every hour that the House of Cortano appeared to be hovering on the brink of disaster thereafter, a mysterious, odd relative would appear out of nowhere and save the family by some means or another. This "messiah" was always a young lady who called herself Tobi, a name rightfully belonging to the mysterious ancestress who had vanished ages ago.
The Cortanos would swear this young stranger a saint for the rest of time, myth holding her to be the ghost of Moira come back to pick up the pieces of ancient Venice.
Little do the Cortanos know how close they are to the truth. Little do they know that in 1734, after the news of her husband's demise, Moira Tobi San'Camiel Cortano slit her wrists and stood before the face of the God she had worshipped since she was a toddler; and God, who above everything knows the pains of love, saw the immense love in the widow and had mercy on her soul. He sent her back to the living heart of crumbling Venice, healed her self-inflicted wounds, and gave her both a pledge and an angelic guardian. As an angel of God, a daughter of the House of Cortano and Venice, Moira, now to be known as Tobi, was to be patron savior of her own family for all time.
In the year of our Lord 1995 a young woman by the name of Tobi Cortano arrived in Venice by train, set up household in the ancient Villa dé Cortano with her relatives, and proceeded to save the family from threatening bankruptcy by means that some might dub as miraculous. Only, contrary to historical tradition, she did not disappear the moment the House of Cortano was steady again on its foundations. She remained within the bosom of the family until when in 1999 Maria Cortano on her death bed requested Tobi to search for a lost Cortano cousin in the States. Tobi agreed, staying only long enough to hold Maria's hand into death, and caught the next plane to New Orleans, Louisiana where, once landed, she traveled on to a small isle where the cousin, said to be named Tonio, was last seen.
