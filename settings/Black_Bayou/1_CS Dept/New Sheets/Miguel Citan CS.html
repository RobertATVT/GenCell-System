Background


Let's begin this with love. After all, that is where he would start. During Spain's civil war, preceding WW II, both sets of his Spanish Gypsy grandparents fled to America, managing, for the sake of friendship, to stick together and settle on the Texas Coast. It was luck that managed to get a good portion of their families there also. His parents, though born here, spoke better Spanish than English, and even Miguel's speech carries a Castilian flavor. Family life for Miguel, or Miko his nickname, was loving and despite the sometimes harsh bigotry of the border states towards 'Mexicans', Miko's education was thorough - up until high school. Now is where we fast forward until that high school venture.

It was until his senior year before he actually understood what his parents meant when they said they only had to look in the other's eyes. It was not until the end of that year that he understood his maternal grandmother's advice that young love often does not survive. Of course, her prediction this time had not a betrayel at its end. It had death. But that is too fast, eh?

He met Amanda in his physics class - and had to wonder how he missed her over the three prior years of high school. It was almost his birthday, months after they had privately agreed to marry, when she told him, smiling nervously, that she was pregnant. She was just two months gone - not even showing yet when the lovely graduation pictures were taken. Miko had been welcomed to one of her clique's graduation party where the alcohol flowed freely. The tale can be guessed from here, no? Miko got into a fight with a jealous jock, breaking a vase over his head and was cooling his heels in the back of a police cruiser while the jock's parents swore at their son - apparently holding more respect for humanity than their child. And Amanda's parents had come to escort her home, thinking time away from both boys would do her good. The small Nissan was unrecognizable after the drunken driver of the suburban missed a red light.

The next five years were a haze to him really. It was blur of playing his guitar in one place or another, for one struggling band or another. It was scraping the bottom of sanity, struggling with the God his parents taught him over her death. It is within the last year that in his travels he has found the terms and peace he sought. The sound of her voice that had haunted him throughout, began promising him a way to feel her touch again - a way to understand, at least partially why she had died. This is where his travels on his paternal grandparents' graduation gift leave him at Isle Voletta's edge, seeking that higher understanding.
