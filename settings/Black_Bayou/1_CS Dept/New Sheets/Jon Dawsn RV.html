Background

Jon Riley Dawson was born to Jonathon Riley Dawson II and Emelia Dawson one cold Nebraska day in early February. The couple had been trying for conception for quite a while and had been told that because of complications from illness as a teenager, Emelia was incapable of childbirth. It was a miracle that she and the baby made it through the birth. However - Jonathon was to be their only biological son, his two brothers both being adopted when Jon was 9 and then 10. Jon's father was the local preacher and the family owned a modest farm not too far outside the limits of Rawlings, Nebraska. It was in this God fearing, hard working atmosphere that Jon was raised. He showed excellent academic ability at an early age, as well as a love for the church. His academic endeavors were always coupled with his learning the Bible and singing with his father. For the most part Jon had a normal childhood and adolescence. He was a good church-going, moderately athletic, excellent academic son who was praised by both of his parents.
Things only started getting odd on his 18th birthday. His mother had been planning a large familiy get together on that day, but Jon had a massive chemistry test the following day. So, the few hours between school and the get-together, he decided to spend in the old hayloft of his father's never-used barn. He spent a great deal of time studying there because it was quiet, isolated and not too cold. But, as he was climbing the ladder, one of the barn's tomcats jumped after a mouse and landed on Jon. The weight of his bookbag threw him off balance and he fell twenty feet to the barn's floor. The floor was old and gave way under him, shards of broken wood ripping through his femoral artery.
He should have died within the span of a minute, but somehow Jon lived through the fall and the bleeding. His mother found him an hour later, still breathing, the wound having been staunched in some way the she, nor the doctors could understand. But that was not the most disconcerting thing. The first...event...happened while Jon was in the garden section of the recouperation clinic he had been sent to. The garden was outside and being in Nebraska...none of the flowers had bloomed. However - after being in the garden for some time, a nurse went to check on Jon..only to find him covered with butterflies and seemingly in the throws of a seizure. The minute he was shaken the butterflies flew from him to die in the cold winds and he snapped out of the violent thrashings...only to mutter the same word over and over again. "Voletta."
His doctor figured that in the fall Jon had damaged some sort of sensory processing section of his mind and that the crossed neuro-wiring was responsible for the visions. In short his mind was damaged and overloading itself. However - extensive testing showed that to be untrue and Jon was sent back to Rawlings as somewhat of a medical mystery. Nonetheless, the visions and experiences did not stop. Jon started having terribly realistic dreams about falling from this Lighthouse into the Gulf and of this sleepy town. He knew the name like his own...and -had- to go there. By the beginning of March, Jon had to be supervised at all times. He spoke in tongues and saw angels. He heard their singing and kept seeing this sad-eyed, black haired man in his dreams. He would wander away from school if no one was watching and would murmur about someone he had to find and how he had to talk to her.
His parents were close to making the difficult decision of hospitalizing him when late on the eve of March 2nd, he appeared in the doorway of their bedroom. A bookbag was thrown over his shoulder and he was fully dressed. His hand hung at his side and as his parents turned on their light they saw the blood trickling from his hands and the growing blotches on the sides of his white sweater. His parents moved to come to him, but he silenced them with a motioning of his hand. His voice was serene and gentle as explained that God was calling him toward Isle Voletta and someone who lived there. The stigmata was only a sign of mounting urgency.
His father -- being intensely religious -- finally understood that the gibberish of the preceding 4 weeks had been leading toward that moment and that if God willed Jon's leaving, then it must come to fruition. So early March 2nd, Jon left his father's house to do this Father's work..with only the clothes on his back, a few possessions and the Will of God on his side.
