Special Abilities


Animal Mimicry - 0 Soul to use. (##<falcon> form##)
System/Duration: NA / Unlimited; takes 2 turns to shape shift
Effect/Description: This spell enables the lamia to shape shift into one type of small
animal. This would include, but is not limited to: dogs, cats, mice, etc. This form must be chosen at the time of character creation. Other animal types may be purchased at a cost of 5 Generation Points or 25 XP later on per form. The animal form you choose must be approved by the Rules Coordinator or Forum Director. No form of spell casting is possible while in an animal form. Please send requests to ANTEmail 2.

(Just a quick note about shape changing (all forms). While a lamia is in another form, if its current Body falls to 0, the lamia will immediately change back to mortal form minus the damage it has already taken, leaving a minimum of 1 Body. It should also be noted that materiel objects do not shapeshift with the character. Any and all clothes and objects the character was holding will need to be transported by some other means.)

Healing - 0 Soul to use. (##Heal##)
System/Duration: 2d6+Will vs. TN 20 / Instant; takes 1 hour to cast
Effect/Description: When this spell is successfully cast, the lamia will heal at an incredible rate. Upon successfully casting this spell, the lamia heals 1 Body for each point over 20 they make on the activation roll, unless the wounds were created by fire or salt water. Wounds created by salt water cannot be healed with this spell. Fire wounds heal at the rate of 1 Body per day and only when the character sleeps. If the character is woken up before the day is over, the healing process is disrupted and they do not regain their point (which means they cannot role-play more than the casting of this spell until the damage is healed). If fire wounds aren't healed with this spell they will take twice as long to heal than normal. The spell takes a full hour to cast and the lamia must be undisturbed for the entirety of the casting. Any distractions will for the lamia to start over a gain. The lamia must role-play the hour long casting of this spell in the main room.

Meditation - 0 Soul to use. (##Meditation##)
System/Duration: NA / NA; takes 30 minutes to activate
Effect/Description: In order to center themselves and replenish their mystical energies after spell casting, a lamia needs to shut themselves off from the world around them and meditate. To accomplish this, they must be sitting alone and relaxing, concentrating on a candle flame, or some sort of talisman - perhaps chanting. The meditation must be role-played for the full 30 minutes in the main room. If they are interrupted (legitimately) for even a moment, the meditation must begin anew. 1 Soul Point may be regained each half-hour, going no higher than the original level.

Night Vision - 0 Soul to use. (##Nightvision##)
System/Duration: 2d6+Will vs. TN 20 / Scene; takes 1 round to cast
Effect/Description: The lamia can see twice as far as a normal human can. In addition, when the lighting is low, or near total darkness, the vision of the lamia takes on the effects of infravision. In total darkness, this mastery is useless. Unable to make out detail, the lamia can discern between animate and inanimate objects, sensing their red or blue glow, respectively.

Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs victim 2d6+Will / Instant; takes 1 round to cast.
Effect/Description: This spell enables the Lamia to flawlessly detect the true nature of a target. The Lamia will instantly know what the individual's breed is. The individual must IM the Lamia with the truth if the Lamia wins the roll. *Demons using Soul Mask, will reflect the breed being masked and not the nature of the demon. Lamia must possess Lore Level 3 for the breed they are attempting to detect, in order to recognize it for what it is. If the lore is not possessed, the Lamia will only know there is something odd about the target.
Charge - 1 Soul to use. (##Charge ##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 22 / Scene; takes 1 round to cast.
Effect/description: Through the use of this spell, the Lamia can electrically charge an item to deliver a shock upon touch. This spell causes the static electricity in the air around the item to spark when another object comes into contact with it. This spark causes -1 Body damage to anyone who touches the item. This can be cast on weapons, causing knives to do 2 damage for instance. Due to their explosive natures, bullets and shells cannot be charged. The item must be touched when the charging is cast.

Fog - 1 Soul to use. (##Fog ##)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 28 / 4 rounds or 2 minutes; takes 1 round to cast.
Effect/Description: This spell enables the Lamia to create a cloud of fog that surrounds the target. The fog created is very thick, making combat nearly impossible. The victim caught in the fog can barely see their hand in front of their face. Attacks are made at random, meaning the victim cannot target any specific body location. These attacks are wild, thus requiring a penalty of -4 to their Attack Modifier. If the attacker in a fog misses their intended target, each person within range must roll to dodge the same attack in the order of who is closest (to be determined by the ST).

Background


Born on Oct 21, 1973 in Ft. Lauderdale, FL. He had a better than average childhood. He excelled in whatever he attempted by always giving 110%. He received a football scholarship to play for FSU for his play as a linebacker. He quit football after his sophomore year when he saw one of his teammates lose the use of both of his legs after a legal hit. He proceeded to get his BS in Criminology & Criminal Justice and had started his way on to receiving a Master's in Psychology. He wanted to become a Forensic Psychologists. His ambitions left him on Jan 6, 1996 when his fiancée, Jen, died inn a car accident. She was six weeks months pregnant at the time. Ever since that night, James has vowed to never let anyone get close to his heart again. He dropped out of graduate school and began to bartend at the local bars. After a year he moved to Biloxi, Miss. to become a blackjack dealer. While learning the trade, he began to tend bar at some of the strip clubs in town. There he befriended a stripper named Raven, who shared the same outlook on life as he did. She used to tell him about the wild & amazing times she had in Isle of Voletta when she lived there. Finally after hearing enough of her tales, James decided to pack up what ever he could into his jeep, along with his Black Akita named Zeus, and take a chance on the Isle.
