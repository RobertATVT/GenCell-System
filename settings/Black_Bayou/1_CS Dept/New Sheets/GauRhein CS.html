Background

Background: The Rheinholts had been a prominent family in Germany for centuries. With a past both famous, and shrouded in mystery, legends were abound throughout. Some said they were vampires, or werewolves. Others said that they were a line of heroes, champions against the dark. The questions were numerous, but all remained the same. Unanswered.
There was another legend, one that some claimed was entertwined with the family Rheinholt. A tale of a dark cult of druids, secretly influencing events throughout history. It was said that they would steal away children when they were very young, taking them to the underground temples, raising them to their own will. They had many names throughout the many villages and cities. The Dark Ones. The Child-Theives. The Blood-Takers. Worshippers of the Night.
This tale, was more then just a story.
It was an acolyte of the Dark Ones that stole into the Rheinholt estate late that night, when Gauren was only one year old. He moved with the stealth of shadows, past trained dogs that didn't hear or smell him, past state of the art security systems without so much as a blip, to the nursery where Gauren slept, and took the sleeping child. Noone even knew he was there until he was gone.
But something else sensed him, and had ever since he had left the temple hours before. And it would find him.
The acolyte had run far through the woods, dodging across snowbanks and frozen rivers, using every trick he knew to escape his pursuers. Something...vicious...terrible... followed him through those woods, moving like the winds. He changed course, and it even when it couldn't see him it still changed directions with him, perfectly...tracing...searching...
Hunting.
The wolf tore into the acolyte before he even had a chance to react. It was a quick, clean kill, his jugular pierced before he even had time to scream. The wolf started to feed...then stopped, as his attention was turned to a small cry, from the bundle the man had been carrying.
Perhaps, had it been any child other then Gauren, the wolf would have killed him, too. All instinct normally would have told him so. But...oddly...something was different about that small boy, different from all humans the wolf had ever before encountered. In a small, almost indistinguishable way...he smelled...different. Not wrong, the way most humans did, but...almost like pack.
The wolf was incredible in size, even for the already large wolves of the mountains, and carried the infant Gauren easily back to the lair. The whole pack felt what the Alpha had, the strange kinship, something that tugged in a deep part of them. They accepted the babe as their own, raising him among them. He grew to be fiercely strong, lean and tough, able to survive in any environment. He learned to hunt, to kill, with his bare hands, as fiercely as any of the pack.
Just this year, at the beginning of March, Gauren was discovered by a team of wildlife researchers. Though it took an entire team, one of which was killed by the pack in the process, they tranquilized Gauren, and brought him in for study. After a week, he was transferred to a high security research center in Louisana.
The guards, majorly underestimated his ability to escape.
He roams Louisana now, lost and confused, trying to find his pack. Until, one night, he happens on Voletta, and may find himself even more lost and confused...
