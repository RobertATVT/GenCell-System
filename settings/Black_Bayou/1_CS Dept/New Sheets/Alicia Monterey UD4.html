Background

An orphan at six months , Megan Brown was the only survivor of a single car accident 20 kilometers outside of Sydney, Australia in the summer of 1971. Having sustained semi-major injuries, she spent the next two months of her young life in a pediatric ward at the County hospital.

At 8 months she was adopted and raised by a wealthy middle aged couple by the name of Jonathan and Beverly Monterey. Upon the signing of the adoption papers, the couple legally changed Megan's name to Alicia Monterey. The three lived in a nice home on the residential side of Sydney.

Her childhood and teenage years were normal, other than private tutoring for her education she was pretty much like any other child her age. Each of her professors did note that she was almost fanatical about solving problems and finding solutions where none were readily available. This personality trait was one that led her to excellent scores in her at home education..

At the age of 16, she became enthralled with the idea of medical research and persuaded her adopted parents to allow her additional studies in the field of medicine. Jonathan, being a private physician himself, was thrilled with the idea that his little girl would follow in his footsteps.

Upon her eighteenth birthday the records of her parents death were released to her along with their personal effects. It dawned upon her that it was strange that after 18 years, no one had ever come forth as knowing either of her biological parents. She learned from the death certificates that both her mother, Anita Brown, and father, Stephen Periot had died of unknown causes. Having been told her entire life that she had been the only survivor of
the fatal car crash, Alicia questioned the cause of death. After all, if one were to die in a fatal car wreck, then cause of death would be readily apparent. Unfortunately, for Alicia, this was not the case.

With the support of the Monterey's, Alicia began to relentlessly pursue information connected with her parents demise. From what she has learned so far, both Anita and Stephen were dead prior to the collision. At the time of their deaths in 1971, forensic medicines could not find a plausible reason for the deaths of either parent. There was no evidence of physical trauma to either body that is normally associated with high speed collisions. Alicia
did however, have the medical reports from the coroner and found that both Anita and Stephen had traces of some unknown substance found in their blood.

Alicia has devoted the last 9 years of her life attempting to piece together the puzzle left unsolved from that dark rainy night so many years ago. The bodies of both her mother and father had been cremated, so no further evidence could be found from exhuming the corpses. The only physical remains of her parents is a single locket containing a picture of the couple.

Having graduated from the University of Sydney in 1998 with a degree in forensic pathology, Alicia now pursues any lead that may help her in solving the strange circumstances in which her biological parents died. Her inquiries have led to America, more specifically the Isle of Voletta. **
