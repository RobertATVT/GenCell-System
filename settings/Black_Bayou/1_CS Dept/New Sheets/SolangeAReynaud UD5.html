Physical Description


Solange is 21 years old, 4'11" tall and weighs 95#. Many mistake her for a child until she opens her mouth and sings, but no child could have such a voice. Her hair is long and wavy, blue-black, and falls to her knees; she wears it braided when it needs to be, but loose and flowing when danger does not threaten. Her eyes are her Irish father's vibrant green, a gift from the Emerald Isle, and her skin is very fair; most people would never guess that her mother was African-American. Her figure is slender and waifish--the best words to describe the way she looks are 'fragile' and 'breakable'--but unmistakably female despite the fact that she doesn't fill the front of her blouses with breasts the size of overfilled basketballs. She typically dresses in off-the-shoulder peasant's/ poet's blouses of loose cotton and long, flowing skirts in wild gypsy patterns and colors. More often than not, she goes barefoot; a lifetime of running through the back alleys of New Orleans have toughened her feet. She wears lots of jewelry when singing--jingling bangles on her wrists and belled chains around her ankles, rings on all ten fingers, pendants with mystic symbols snatched from her mother's jewelry box--but nothing that makes noise when on the run. Her ears are both pierced three times each, her navel once. She has a small tattoo of a black swan on her right hip.


Background


Solange's mother was black, a mambo with one of the voodoo cults in New Orleans; her father was a hard-drinking Irish merchant marine working on one of the many boats that carried crude oil back and forth across the ocean and up and down the Mississippi from Baton Rouge to the Delta. The two made an unlikely pair, but they were smitten, and a child was born--out of wedlock--to the couple 21 years ago. The mambo named her daughter Solange, Creole French for 'Angel of the Sun', and the child showed at an early age that she was a gifted singer. Jazz, blues, rock, her father's native Celtic music--no style was beyond her grasp. She grew up singing in smoky honky-tonks and backstreet blues bars to supplement her family's meager income, and at the age of 16, her beauty flourished. Of a necessity, she learned to dodge, fight like a hellcat, and even shoot to ward off the advances of the less-than-principled patrons of the bars where she sang, who were naturally fascinated by the combination of her beauty and her singing. But there came a time when her abilities could no longer help her: one of New Orleans' gunrunners saw her, heard her sing, and decided she had to be his. He kidnapped her from the bar after it closed. Her parents discovered what had happened from the bartender and her father, still hale and hearty at the age of 46, came after the gunrunner; he was mowed down by the gunrunner's thugs for his efforts. Her mother tried to rescue her daughter her own way, calling together her group and casting a curse on the gunrunner; she and the group were also slaughtered by the gunrunner's gang. At last, after more than a month of enduring as the man's prisoner, withstanding rape every night and knowing the man was guilty of her parents' deaths, Solange snapped one night after the man had raped her and passed out in an alcoholic stupor. She drove a screwdriver into his ear, killing him, then grabbed one of his guns and fled the city forever. The gunrunner's men search for her vehemently, intent on making her pay for their boss' murder. Solange's flight has brought her at last to a small, backwoods town called Isle Voletta, where she hopes at last to be able to hide from those who pursue her, settle down, and find peace.
Solange's religious beliefs are the same as her mother's were; though not a priestess, Solange has been initiated into the ways of voodoo, over her late father's choice to have her baptized Catholic as he was.
