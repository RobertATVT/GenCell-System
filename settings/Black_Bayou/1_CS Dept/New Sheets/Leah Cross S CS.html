Physical Description


She's smaller than you'd think. Dirty blonde hair has been cropped short; hardly long enough to pull back; just brushing the curve of her chin - sharpish, giving her heart-shaped face a 'pixie' quality. Cheekbones are pronounced - even on a wiry 5'1" frame, ninety pounds isn't a lot - making the dusky blue eyes appear even larger than they are; small ears and a longish nose stick out a bit extra given the gauntness. Her figure is something just barely hinted at. Spindly bones extend limbs to delicately worked feet and hands; long toes and fingers taper off neatly. A much-paled tan rests over her, blurring color. The rosebud lips are softer than the rest of her, paralleling the facets of her eyes - though mouth and windows harden as well, to stubborn lines; her moodiness is reflected perfectly in her expression.

Lee Cross was born August 23, 1978, the second daughter of a couple in Baton Rouge. On first acquaintance, Lee is the sweetest kid -- polite, charming, ready to smile and chat about music or books or physics; anything at all to keep the conversation going. She is, however, a person of absolutes; sometimes harsh in her judgment, and basically moral -- except with herself. Here she can be said to be destructive, and masochistic; these things in such a way that exposes an intense fragility, if you look hard enough.

Her parents divorced when she was fifteen (grounds of abuse). She was hardly ever in trouble at school -- however, when she was, it was bad. She didn't get along with her boyfriend well, but they stuck together as a sort of abusive support system; they'd beat each other up (mostly verbally) and hang around for hugs afterwards. Not to mention the fact that drug connections are important. During high school, she also picked up the nickname 'Starling'. This might best be explained through personality conflicts and quick retorts; in the way she's territorial and inviting at the same time.

After graduating high school, they moved to New Orleans together, and took the cheapest apartment they could find. Neither one had money for rent, let alone college. What they did have went to fuel various addictions. Of course, they eventually lost the apartment... For a while, they took to squatting in an abandoned warehouse, but split up within six months of that, after a particularly bad fight during which fists flew and knives appeared. Intermittently, Lee left and came back - ran into someone in London and became a little more than your regular pickpocket; she stole religious artifacts for this man (Lorenzo Maguey). This last time, however, Lee came further south, towards Voletta.
