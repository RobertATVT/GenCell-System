Background




Lying on the floor,
four stories high,
in the corridor between the asphalt and the sky.
I am caught like bottled water.
The light daughter.

[Ani DiFranco, Work Your Way Out.]



This section of town is not one you want to be caught walking alone at night in; not one you want to be caught walking alone in at all. This is their district; the disillusioned (or never illusioned) deviants; those children never quite raised. They raised themselves on their own rules and made up names from books and plays; songs; Bibles, if the spirit so moved any of them to read. A subculture; a counterculture, maybe. A group of chthonians. They made up their own reality; their own system; their own eternity. There is a heirarchy, when the spirit so moves any of them to enforce it. School was unheard of; parents were absent in mind or in body. Scavengers; streetwalkers - minstrels who carry switchblades and have no qualms about drawing them.
At eighteen years of age, Margaret Revelin has been here forever. It's likely she was born into it; her mother was born into it; it dates back and she has no idea who she is. A razor-sharp edge and a poison-laced interior; a case study in a sociology book. They have been sheltered from 'normal' life; never socialized - shadow-walkers with heavy boots and made-up slang; people who have no inkling about how they should work at being perceived. Dangerous, really. A dark mood. Like blasphemy in Cathedrals. With some of the West Side mixed in and bits and pieces of the traditional Cajun society, Andy Warhol-ish with Chopin accompanying.
She has a sharp tongue, often slow to be used; commandeering, sometimes. The 42nd Street princess; a remnant of dusted butterfly wings. 'Broken'; 'cried'; 'weak' - these words are ones not allowed in this girl's vocabulary. Apologies and pleasantries; pleases and thank you's are not things you are likely to get from her. She's a projection of what this place can embody; veiled magic; a street nymph with a blunt tongue and little compassion put forth. Mag Revelin would turn herself into stone if she could, the untouched and tainted angel. She grew up like this, in this place; has never been outside of it. She's a little bit of sorrow and a little bit of that old bleakness; a rock once-upon-a-time cracked. None of this, however, would ever be allowed the luxury of dwelling on her face; in her eyes. Veritable chaos. They all think they know each other; it may be that no one knows her. She doesn't have those memories.
She (sort of stated) was born in Voletta. Small and red-headed; ripples of waves. She has blue eyes; one wouldn't expect someone like her to have eyes remotely crystalline. She's underweight; somewhat stunted in growth; a heart-shaped face much too gentle for this sort of work. She's made of contradictions; of pallor and incense. Over-alls; hooded sweatshirts and bandanas over her hair; dyed feathers. No tattoos; no birthmarks. Scars, of course, are another story - she has those, on her right forearm and just below both kneecaps. She has small feet and long toes; small hands and long fingers; narrow shoulders. Feminine.
