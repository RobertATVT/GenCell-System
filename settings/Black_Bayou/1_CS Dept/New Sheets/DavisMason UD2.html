Background


Sam Davis was born and raised in Los Angeles, or rather a small city to the
Northeast of downtown LA called Pasadena. A basic slum at the time, it
quickly grew up into a bustling and beautiful city, long after Sam reached
adulthood.

Sam spent most of his childhood in trouble. He was a fairly rebellious
child, enjoying causing trouble and harrassing the local police force. He
was often taken, as a juvenile, to the police station. When he got a
computer, at the age of fifteen, he spent most of his time trying to hack
into local computer systems.

When he turned seventeen, he made his mother go with him and sign the
papers so he could enlist in the United States Marine Corps. He was
assigned infantry, hoping eventually for Force Recon, but was transferred to a
Military police unit. He became very involved in the police unit, and, upon
discharge, applied at the LAPD Academy. After graduation, he was hired
directly into the LAPD, Southwestern Division, deep in the heart of South
Central, intending to go back to the Pasadena PD after five years on the
force, when he would be a veteran. After a year on the force, he left his
Boot stage, and became a P-II, a full patrol officer. Due to his hard work
and dedication, two years later he was promoted to a P-III, authorized to
train Boot's out of the Academy on the force. Another year on the force,
and the department sent him to become a DRE, or Drug Recognition Expert. After
four years of dedication, Sam couldn't contemplate going to another
department, so was intending to stay on the LAPD until retirement or death.

In May of 1997, however, his mother moved to Louisana and became very ill.
Davis moved to Louisiana to join and help her, feeling it his responsibility
after what he had put her through as a child. Once there, he applied for
the Isla Voletta PD.

After some time in the Voletta PD, the local department was shut down to
form one Law Enforcement Agency, and Davis was transferred to the Sheriff's
Department, under the supervision of Sheriff Cameron Slygar. He was appointed
Senior Deputy-in-charge and assigned not only to street patrol, but new hire
applicants. Later, promoted to Chief Deputy, he acted as the assistant
Sheriff.

Davis soon thereafter changed his name from Sam Richard Davis to Davis
Mason and, upon the resignation of Sheriff Slygar, gained the rank of Sheriff in the Voletta Parish Sheriff's Department
