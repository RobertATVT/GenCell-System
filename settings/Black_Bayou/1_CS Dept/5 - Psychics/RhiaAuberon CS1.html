Background


Rhiannon was born and raised in Maryland, leaving to go to Penn State when she was 18 on a scholarship for her writing skills. She had an upper middle class upbringing, her parents; Aaron and Caroline both being teachers in a college prep high school, which she also attended. Always the one to get the straight A's, she never had many friends, and even though she didn't look it, was considered a geek among her peers. Not that it mattered much, her nose was always stuck in a book anyway...for the sheer enjoyment of gaining knowledge. Her favorite author from the time she was a teenager was Anne Rice. Her books, The Vampire Chronicles, fascinated Rhiannon to the point of this almost obsessive lust for finding out all there was to be found about vampires. It was one of her papers in high school on the origins of the legend of the vampire myth that won her the scholarship to Penn State. She graduated there with honors and went on the following year to write a novel about vampires that did fairly well in the bookstores. In the year after that, she sold a few short stories that were published in compilation books.
Then something interesting happened. She was visiting a friend from college who now lived in Metarie when she saw an unusual story on a local news channel out of a place called Isle Voletta. Some kind of strange animal maulings. She asked her friend, Karen's, husband about it, as he had lived in the area almost from the time he was a child. And he told her some of the strange folklore surrounding the island and the people that lived there. Rhiannon couldn't believe it. It was almost too good to be true. Of course, Derek thought she was being absolutely silly to even pretend that she believed anything he had told her. But Rhiannon had read enough books and delved into enough history to know that vampires, in some form, did exist. Who knows, maybe she would be lucky enough to find her own Louis, a vampire who would tell their story, share their side with the world. A factual account of probably one of the most understood creatures in both ancient and modern mythology. It was almost too much to ask for.
