The controls for this monitoring is done only at Victor's office and to access this system you must enter ten (10) sixteen (16) digit alpha-numeric passwords only known to Victor.

3) Standard Security. Motion sensors, laser sensors, pressure and glass sensors, door sensors and thermal sensors. The thermal sensors will go off if there is any sudden ten degree difference anywhere in the room. The video feeds report the feed levels to the systems and monitors so that any attempts to patch feeds from them will register as a decrease in feed. This works for all security devices.

4) All features route to Eclipse and the police can be called with the touch of a button reporting the adress direct to dispatch.

Security Wiring Matrix
All Eclipse Response personel have the Security Wiring Matrix with retinal and thumbprint activation. These devices are activated at the beginning of duty and are active until shut down at shift end. There are 2 Stations where these can be activated and there are only two people that can activate them. The first one is at Eclipse and can be activated by Victor Martel. The Second is on Site and an Eclipse Team Leader must be present with an already active Matrix in order to activate any others. These Wiring Matrix's are part of the Quick Response Intrusion Program set up by Eclipse, also known as Quick-RIP. All buildings protected by Eclipse have had new auto-open doors install so that if there is an intrusion the response team can quickly run down the intruder and aprehend them. This also alleviates the response team from having to lower any needed weapons. These Active Wiring Matrix's are detected by the sensors in the door and open when the Matrix enters within 10 feet from the door. Should one of these Matrix's be active for more than 9 hours they automatically turn off and a locator is activated giving it's location to the Eclipse via a Cellular Communication Chip installed in them.
Properties of the Active Matrix
Cellular Communications chip with one way response to Eclipse Computers. The active Matrix is a wiring harness that fits over the shoulders and back of the head of the security officer. The wiring harness monitors the heartbeat and brainwave activity of the guard wearing it. Should the guard have critical spikes or low levels this will be reported to Eclipse CPU's and an alert will be given out for backup to assist the guard in trouble. This signal is given out via the one way communication chip installed into the wiring harness.

These Matrix's are all GPS individualized and will allow their locations to be overlayed onto a blueprint map of all secured buildings for tactical routing. Due to motions sensors anyone not badged and in the builing will have their location shown on the grid as well. These casings are made with a ceramic composite and can endure heat up to 500 degrees without being damaged. The ceramic keeps the delicate inside electronics inside from being injured before the entire badge itself is destroyed. This composite also makes the badge difficult to destroy through most acids. A good hammer, however, will hurt the badges considerably. They only have an effective 2 Body versus blunt trauma dealt to them and an effective 10 Body versus fire or chemical trauma.
