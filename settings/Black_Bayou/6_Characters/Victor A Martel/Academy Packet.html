~ INTRODUCTION ~


Welcome, Cadet. You are now on the payroll of the Voletta Parish Sheriff's Department. From this day forth, you are no longer a normal civilian. You are an officer and a gentleman. You are a cadet, and, if you complete this course, you will become a fully uniformed, fully trained, highly tuned instrument, ready to serve and protect your community. You will be a Sheriff's Deputy, with all the responsibilities, privileges, duties, and rights expected of a defender and protector of the peace and community.

You are taking a strong, sure step toward your course as a better person. No longer can you be expected to sit by and let things happen. You will be proactive and strong, kind and compassionate, fair and judicial in all matters. While on duty and off, you are a symbol of the Sheriff's Department. You are a part of the community. You stand for the order and peace which keeps the city together. You are a strong, supportive part of the community, and will be looked upon by citizens, criminals, and other officers as such. Congratulations, Cadet. You are on your way to a better life.

~ SHERIFF'S CREED ~


A Sheriff is fair and unbiased. A Sheriff protects the peace and does not endanger civilians. A Sheriff will never look on, but will step forward and protect. A Sheriff will not leave officers stranded or helpless, but will drop every and anything to respond for aide. A Sheriff will not do only what is convenient, but will strive to do what is complicated. A Sheriff will put his life on the line to protect the community and it's citizens, and is expected to act in a highly trained, professional, confident, and sure
manner. A Sheriff willingly devotes their time and efforts to help the Department. A Sheriff is an officer and defender of the law, and does not break or bend the rules, but promotes and emphasizes them. A Sheriff is trusted and respected, and are expected to act as such.

~ REQUIREMENTS ~


Sheriff's Deputy candidates must be a minimum of 5'6" in height and no maximum of height, with weight according to height. Candidates must have a high school education, 20/40 vision uncorrected. Corrective lenses require no time limit. Candidates must be physically fit and expect strenuous and harsh physical work, in relation to police performance. Candidates must have no felony arrests, or any other violation that might prohibit them from carrying a firearm. An officer must have a financially secure history. They must not have more than four Black Bayou traffic violations within the last four years.

No initial firearm or shotgun training is required.

You have three (3) chances to pass the following tests: Sidearms and Shotgun. If these tests are not passed within the alloted chances, your character will be dismissed from the Sheriff's Department Academy and will not be allowed to reapply for six months.

~ INITIAL TESTING ~


Candidates must pass a physical fitness test to be accepted into the Plaquemine County Sheriff's Department Academy. This test includes four parts.

A.) Balance: The candidate must stand on a board balanced on a trifuge. The candidate must keep their balance for one minute without the edges of the board dropping below two inches of the floor. The candidate has two chances for this test.
B.) Endurance: Candidate must pedal as fast as possible on a stationary bicycle for two minutes. Candidates have one chance for this test.
C.) Sidestepping: Candidate must step side to side over a line for thirty seconds, as fast as possible and exceed a preset number. Candidate has two tries for this test.
D.) Sit-ups: Candidate must do at least eighty sit ups in two minutes to pass this test. Candidate has two tries.

~ SHOTGUN TRAINING ~


This section of your course begins your in-depth training of shotgun procedures. This includes recognition, assembly, disassembly, cleaning, maintenance, reloading, quick loading, handling, safety procedures, firing, critical placement and targeting. All training will take place on the Academy firing range, and includes silhouette targets (A silhouette made to look like a person with circles in the chest and head for target practice), lifelike targets (targets which look exactly like real people for target practice), and pop-up quick fire targets (Targets which pop up that look like humans, some armed and some not, in order to test for shoot/no shoot scenerios.). Rounds used include 1-ounce slugs for use on raids for doors and vehicles, and 12-guage 00 buckshot for close protection use.

The Voletta Parish Sheriff's Department uses the Benelli Super-90 Semi-automatic 12 gage shotgun, with choice of 1-ounce slug or 12-ought buckshot. Shotgun is equipped with SureFire tactical flashlight, Federal Ammunition hand pistol mounts, and Bo-mar Day-Glo sights.

~ SIDEARM TRAINING ~


This course begins your in-depth training of sidearm, or small firearm, procedures. This includes firearm recognition, revolver use, automatic use, assembly, disassembly, cleaning, maintenance, reloading, quick loading (Speed loaders for revolvers/magazines for automatics), handling, safety procedures, firing, critical placement, accuracy, targeting and aim. Stationary training will take place on the Academy firing range, and includes silhouette targets (see above in Shotgun Training), lifelike targets (See above in Shotgun Training), and pop-up quick fire targets (See above in Shotgun Training). Tactical firearms training takes place on the Academy combat range, and includes the El Presidente drill (Three targets where the cadet is to place one bullet in the first, two in the second, one in the third. Then go backwards, speed-reloading when necessary), and the pop-up innocent/criminal targets. Cadet is expected to respond according to split-second visual affirmation. Rounds used include Federal Ammunition 9 mm Hollowpoint rounds, Federal Ammunition 9 mm Full Metal Jacket rounds, and Federal Ammunition 9 mm Flechet rounds.

The Voletta Parish Sheriff's Department issues and allows only the Baretta 96F 9 mm Semi-automatic pistol, with ammunition of choice by the officer. Sidearm is equipped with trigger touch laser sight, Bo-mar Day-Glo sights, rubber handgrips, extended safety release, and beveled magazine well.

~ WEAPONS TACTICAL TRAINING ~


Weapons tactical training also teaches the basis for unarmed combat, hand to hand combat, martial arts training, and covers such topics as focusing on the suspect's actions, body movements, and possible hidden weaponry, should a situation arise when the officer is without firearm. This is titled Cadet Training.

Candidates are taught the uses of the police baton, including crowd and riot control, suspect apprehension, use of force procedures, and proper techniques.

Candidates are taught use and restraint of firearms and close-combat situations, as well as safety around civilians, when-to-shoot situations, and long range combat shooting battles.

~ POLICE PROCEDURES ~


This final section of the Voletta Parish Police Academy teaches the candidates how to properly complete traffic stops, felony traffic stops, pursuits, raids, arrest techniques, booking procedures, vehicle maneuvering in various environmental situations, gang and drug apprehensions, violent suspect apprehension, and use of force situations.

Traffic Stop Proceedures:
1) Radio in location
2) Radio in license plate and how many suspects are present in the vehicle.
3) If Suspect comes back with violent wants or warrants, request back-up code 3 before approaching suspect and skip down to felony stop arrest proceedures.
4) If suspect comes back without wants or warrants, approach the suspect on the right side of the vehicle, walking around the front and to the driver's side.

******NOTE****** This is the most dangerous time for a peace officer. Be always wary of where your suspects hands are. Many suspects will wait until the officer has approached the window to try and shoot the officer. DON'T BECOME A STATISTIC.

5) Request suspect's driver's license and registration.
6) Return to your vehicle and radio in driver's license and write citation.
7) Confirm that suspect's driver's license matches with the owner of the car. Use your discretion. If the last name and address are identical, assume a relative.
8) Ask suspect to sign the ticket, advising them that it is not an admission of guilt, merely a promise to appear in court.
9) Return license and continue with patrol.

Felony Traffic Stop Proceedures:
1) You must have a good reason to believe a felony has been committed.
2) Call radio dispatch for back-up unit.
3) Maintain radio contact with back-up unit.
4) Bring the suspect's vehicle to a complete stop. Maintain cover until the suspect is under control.
5) Command suspect to "halt" or "stop". Proceed to command suspect to lie face down on the ground.
6) Handcuff and Mirandize the suspect.

Felony Arrest Proceedures
1) Before proceeding with arrest, your back-up unit must be staked out in close proximity.
2) Maintain radio contact with your back-up unit.
3) Observe the crime.
4) Have weapon drawn at the ready.
5) Identify yourself as a Sheriff's Deputy to the suspect.
6) Command suspect to keep his hands over his head. If at any time the suspect drops his hands prepare to take defensive action.
a. If the suspect flees, follow the suspect on foot. Maintain radio contact if possible.
b. If the suspect takes aggressive action, (i.e. reaching for a weapon, charging you) use necessary defensive actions.
7) When it is safe, approach the prisoner. Handcuff the prisoner. Note: Many suspects will wait until close contact with the officer has been established before taking aggressive action. Always be prepared to defend yourself against any hostile behavior.
8) Search the prisoner
9) Advise supect of his Miranda rights
10) Transport suspect to jail.

Handcuffing Proceedures:
1) All suspects in your custody must be handcuffed.
2) Suspects you feel could be a danger, -must- be handcuffed behind the back.
3) Suspects who you feel are less of a threat, may be handcuffed in front at your discretion.

~ Use of Force ~


LEVEL ONE FORCE- FIRM PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT
Situation: A person shows passive (verbal) resistance (usually on routine stops.)
Correct enforcement:
1) Use firm, professional conduct. In most cases, this will prevent the situation from escalating.
2) Refrain from abusive language or threats, as it will only escalate the situation.

LEVEL TWO FORCE- HAND-TO-HAND COMBAT
Situation: A supect's threats indicate imminent physical attack (no weapons visible)
Correct Enforcement:
1) Call back-up unit if possible
2) Use department-approved hand-to-hand combat.
3) If appropriate, use PR-24 nightstick.

LEVEL THREE-DEADLY FORCE
Situation: A suspect attempts physical violence with a deadly weapon.
Correct Enforcement:
1) Use deadly force in self-defense.
2) Use deadly force when in the fear of the life of another.

~ Radio Codes ~


These are the radio codes that will be used now during radio communications while on patrol. They are said instead of the letters. This is used when calling in License Plates. The correct format for Louisiana License Plates is as follows:

NumberNumberNumber hyphen LetterLetter Letter. i.e: 123-ABC

So that would be read, "Louisiana Plates, One Two Three Adam Boy Charlie"

You will make one up and say it over the radio if you ask for someone's ID, unless they already claim to have a number made up, which is doubtful. You -will- use this format.

Louisiana Driver License's (called LDL's) are nine numeric digits, right after another. Make this up as well when getting information.

~ RIDE ALONGS ~


After candidate proves successful knowledge of the subjects in police procedures, they must complete at least two three hour ride-alongs with an experience officer.

After successful completion they are then commissioned to patrol the streets as a uniformed Sheriff's Deputy, and assume the badge, Sam Browne, firearm, and uniform of a Deputy. Rookie Deputies must have a senior officer present before any felony arrest is attempted. Misdemeanor arrests do not apply to this stipulation. ALL Deputies must call for back-up before any major arrest is undertaken.
