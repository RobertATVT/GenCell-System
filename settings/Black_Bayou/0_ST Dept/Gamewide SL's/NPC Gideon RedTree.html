W Special Abilities W


Communion -0 Soul to use. (##Commune##)
System/Duration: NA / Indefinate; Takes 1 round to activate
Range of Effect: Self / LOS
Effect/Description: This mastery is the principle method of a Belam's natural communion. This mastery allows the Belam to befried mundane animals they encounter and certain types of animals will come to seek them out if nearby. While they are communing the Spirit Animal of the Belam (This animal is chosen during the Belams creation) visits them and the other animals watch over him as he rebuilds his spirit. While in this meditation the Belam will regain 1 lost Soul per 15 minutes of time spent, which must be RP'd in the main room. If there are any PC's in animal form while the Belam is in communion (Within 50' feet) they must make a roll of 2d6+Will vs. the Belams 2d6+Will or they begin taking a more friendly attitude towards the Belam.

Gigas -0 Soul to use (## Gigas ##)

System/Duration: NA / Indefinate; takes 1 round to activate
Range of Effect: Self
Effect/Description: The Belam are a race of giants existing in an age of reason. To this effect they tend to remain in a mortal form most the time, however, when the Belam is involved in conflict they grow in size (an additional 50% in size) for the conflict. The head and hands of the Belam glow with a pale blue. Due to the spiritual nature of them, they are not dumb brutes, they are Honorable and Protective beings. This form grants the Belam a bonus of +1 Body, +1 Mind, and +1 Soul.

Spirit Eyes - 0 Soul to use. (no room command needed)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Range of Effect: Self
Effect/Description: The Belam carry with them the spirit of a great animal, and because of this their sight is increased beyond that of a normal mortal. Belam can pick out details in his surroundings at twice the equivalent of his mortal counterparts and they tend to see farther distances. At night the Belam can see as if it was dusk or daybreak that magical time between night and day. Belam are only at half the penalty for any sort of visual obscurity, this comes to a -1 in Slight Obscurity (Just after Dusk (no light); light rain), a -2 in Medium Obscurity (moonlight; heavy rain), and a -3 in Heavy Obscurity (total darkness; thick fog)

Mending -Varies Soul to use. (##Mend ##)
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Soul vs TN 10 / Permanent
Range of Effect: Touch
Effect/Description: Belam cannot tolerate to see another being suffering. When this mastery is used on person (or Object) the Belam transfers his essence (soul) into them which repairs damage done. For every point above the TN above the Belam gets he will "mend" a point of lost body to the person or object. Each point healed costs one point of soul and the amount isn't controllable. The Belam may very well be rendered unconcious as his spirit flows into them, This will occur if the Belam ever drops below zero Soul. The Belam cannot control the amount of points that are healed, and the target gains only those points to his Body stat and no higher. (See Object Body Levels later on)


W Masteries W


Cloak - 1 Soul to use. (##Cloak##)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 20 / Indefinitely; takes 1 round to activate.
Range of Effect: Self
Effect/Description: The Belam are intrinsic watchers and protectors, standing silent vigil over those they guard. With this ability the Belam can diffuse the opacity of their flesh leaving only the color of spirit, nothing. Though solid in this form the Belam is effectively invisible to the eye and all waves of the light spectrum, including infrared, etc. However, even while not visible, the Belam -can- be heard and smelled, should those methods be available to others. The Belam can maintian this form for great peiods of time and it will only drop should they do so by their own will, attack someone, or are in some way attacked. Attacks or Forced Eye Contact made while under the cover of the Cloak are granted a +5 bonus for surprise, though the illusion will immediately drop. While a Belam is cloaked they cannot be targeted for attacks, as they cannot be seen. This mastery takes a great deal of concentration and while it is in effect no mastery may be enacted. Masteries that are previously in effect may be maintaint. No Aiming, Sure Shot, etc. is allowed while under the Cloak. If a character has a Mastery that allows them to recognize hidden things (Heightened Awareness, Enhanced Senses, etc.) and they SAW the Belam cloak themselves, they can roll their Mastery to 'see' where the Belam is, provided the Belam has not yet left the area.

Spirit Armor - 1 + 1 Soul to use. (##Spirit Armor##)
Generation Cost: 25 / Experience Cost: 75
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Body vs TN 16 / 4 rounds or 2 minutes; takes 1 round to activate.
Range of Effect: Self
Effect/Description: Frequently a Belam will be forced into battle, either to protect, or to avenge. When this occurs the Belam may call upon the Spirit World around him and summon forth great armor to protect him. This armor keeps them from harm and deflects all but the mightiest blows. This armor manifests around the Belam as an ornate suit of armor taking the resemblance of his chosen Spirit Animal. This Armor will absorb 1 point of damage from all attacks made against the Belam. (Damian shoots the Belam in the chest as he runs away, pistols normally do 3 Body damage, but while this is in effect the Belam would only take 2 Body damage). This mastery may only be used once per night. After the duration the Belam may choose to keep this active by spending an additional Soul point after Inits are determined.

Falter - 1 Soul to use. (##Falter ##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs 2d6 + Will / 4 rounds or 2 minutes; takes 1 round to activate.
Range of Effect: Self
Effect/Description:In the natural world, animals and most other mammals see larger creatures as a threat. While the Belam uses this ability the victim feels uneasy and disconcerted, the victim isn't "afraid" of the Belam, but they sense an unwavering aura of power around them. This makes most people unsettled and while under the effects of this mastery the Victim (if the roll succeeds) will be at a -2 to all Will and Attack rolls against the Belam.

Physical Description
Victor is 6'-10" tall with a shoulder length white mane and a tribal crest tattoo that goes around his head from widows peak to occipital lobe and bright green eyes. Victor wears mostly Black clothes with high tunnel neck shirts and well made steel toed boots. When in a bar Victor is nice and charming with plenty of good things to say to all, however the callous uncaring look in his cold eyes seems to have softened unless dealing with an enemy to his family.

Wolf Description
Victors wolf form is that of a large Brendel striped White wolf with green eyes. The stripes are a greyish silver color and Victor has his earings large enough that the show when he is in wolf form, his tattoos also show up in his wolf form as black markings on his white skin. Victors Horus form is more lean and lithe than usual and his head has an almost pit-bullish look to it, as does his legs.
