^v^ Step: 4 Combat Skills ^v^

Following is a description of the skills a character might use in just about any stressful or violent situation. The Statistic in parenthesis (after the name of the skill) represents the abilities you use to perform that skill. You may not raise your skill higher than the statistic listed in parenthesis.

Please note, if your character does not possess a skill below, they may still attempt the action. In this case, the player will use the same roll listed with the skill, without the benefit of the skill level called for in the roll. 2d6+Attack instead of 2d6+Attack+Small Arms, for instance.

For rolls based on a statistic (there is a Stat in the roll) or on a modifier affected by a statistic, you must use the current rating in the roll. For example: If your Body was 5 and you take 3 points of damage, you would have 2 Body left. Then, if you attempted a skill and had to add your Body to the roll, you would only add 2 unless you are healed.

You may notice that the offensive skill descriptions are fairly broad in their descriptions. We did this to allow you the most creative freedom possible. The term - Melee - refers to hand-to-hand combat with one or more opponents. We are always accepting new ideas for skills and if you feel you'd like to make any suggestions, please send them to LDRS ANT BB Mail. Please remember that we cannot use every suggestion sent to us.

Non-weapon Combat Skills

Natural Weapons (Body) Damage: 1
Generation Cost: 1 point per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 2
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Natural Weapons Level
Description: Natural Weapons represents a character's tooth and claw attack. Breeds capable of shape change (the breeds that bring about fangs and/or claws) are the only characters allowed to learn this skill. Please note that mortal's scratching or biting do not cause enough serious damage. Natural Weapons cannot be used in conjunction with any other fighting skills.

Street Fighting (Body) Damage: 1 Body
Generation Cost: 1 point per level
Experience Points: Next level x 2
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Street Fighting Level
Description: Scratch, bite, punch, kick, whatever it takes. That's the basic premise of the street fighter. Whether you're a cadet, thug or bartender, when winning means staying alive, style means nothing but trouble. Besides, style usually gets people dead. There are several different types of street fighting and they are listed in the fighting skills section.

Trained Fighting (Body) Damage: 2 Body
Generation Cost: 2 points per level
Experience Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Trained Fighting Skill Level
Description: Discipline, courage, and honor, these are not only words you live by, but are a part of your lifestyle as well. The path of the martial artist is a tough one, but only leads to self-betterment should you choose to walk its lengthy road. There are several different types of trained fighting and they are listed in the fighting skills section.

Fighting Mastery (Body) Damage: 3 Body
Generation Cost: 3 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 4
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Fighting Mastery Skill Level
Description: The different breeds have physical endowments which, when used to their maximum potential, can be completely devastating. Fighting Masteries are only available for your particular breed. Please refer to the section on fighting skills for more information and the different types.

Small Melee (Body) Damage: 2 Body
Generation Cost: 2 point per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Small Blade Level
Description: Sometimes your bare hands just aren't enough fun. Knives, small clubs, kitchen utensils, ice picks, screwdrivers, etc., are occasionally within easy reach and make functional weapons in times of need. These weapons are small enough to be hidden is a pocket or larger type of cover.

Medium Melee (Body) Damage: 3 Body
Generation Cost: 3 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 4
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Medium Melee level
Description: Close combat can be brought to a new level when the weapons include swords, axes, police batons, baseball bats, and a wooden stake, whether used for stabbing or clubbing is always considered to be a Medium melee weapon. This is the ability to use wield these larger weapons more easily. These weapons may only be concealed by a trench coat or other type of large cover.

Large Melee (Body) Damage: 4 Body
Generation Cost: 4 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 5
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Large Melee level
Description: Close combat at its most brutal. The use of great and two handed weapons take personal warfare to it's deadliest crescendo. Any polearm, or weapon that requires two hands to use is considered to be a Large melee weapon. This is the ability to use wield these larger weapons more easily. These large weapons may never be concealed by anything short of a tent or vehicle.

Non-Lethal Weapons (Body) Damage: 3 Mind
Generation Cost: 2 point per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Non-Lethal Level
Description: Combat does not always have to result in death. Many people carry personal self-defense devices such as tasers, pepper spray, mace, etc. These weapons work to incapacitate a foe, with varying effects depending on the weapon used. See the examples below for ideas. The Storyteller has the final say in the exact effects of these weapons. These weapons can be hidden is a pocket or larger type of cover.
Taser: This is a small, handheld device capable of delivering electric pulses that can temporarily disrupt an opponent's central nervous system. Enough damage will shock the victim into unconsciousness.
Pepper Spray and Mace: These are chemicals usually contained in a small canister. When a victim is sprayed in the face with these vile weapons, they will immediately experience severe pain. Both chemicals blind the victim as they burn the skin and eyes. The severity will depend on the total amount of damage done at the time. The victim will usually fall to the ground, screaming, and covering their burning face with their hands. With enough damage, the victim will fall unconscious due to shock. If this weapon is used against a target with any sort of Enhanced Smell, the target will take an additional +2 points of damage as it overloads their senses. Anyone with Enhanced Smell of any sort standing within 20 feet of te attack will automatically take 1 Mind damage. Ranged Weapon Skills

Throwing (Body) Damage: 1 Body
Generation Cost: 1 point per level
Experience Point Cost: Next Level x 2
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Throwing Skill Level
Description: Special weapons designed for throwing can be used to damage an opponent from afar. Knives, throwing spikes or stars, and other small weapons may be used in this manner. These weapons are small enough to be hidden is a pocket or larger type of cover.

Archery (Body) Damage: 2 Body
Generation Cost: 2 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Attack Modifier+Archery Skill Level

Defensive Skills

Evasion (Body)
Generation Cost: 2 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next Level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Quickness Modifier+Evasion Skill Level when attacked
Description: The best way to not get hurt is to not get hit. That is the premise behind this skill. Ducking, sidestepping, diving for cover, and other means of escape are Evasion actions. The Gamemaster has the final say in what means may be used to avoid attacks, depending on the situation. Higher levels of this skill provide your character with certain bonuses in combat situations besides the obvious.
 Duck: Should you purchase Evasion level 8, you will be able to dodge two opponents a round before suffering the -2 penalty to your dodge. With this ability you won't be subject to the -2 penalty until the third attacker comes after you.
Sidestep: Upon reaching Evasion level 12, you will be able to dodge three opponents a round before suffering the -2 penalty to your dodge. With this ability you won't be subject to the -2 penalty until the forth attacker comes after you.
Note: These abilities do not assist when dodging a Mortal with Ambidextous, seeing as they are not an additional opponent.



Reflexes (Body)
Generation Cost: 2 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Quickness Modifier+Reflexes Skill Level
Description: This skill reflects the character's overall reaction speed, determining how quickly they generally react to a given situation (this process is known as the Initiative Phase of combat). Higher levels of this skill provide your character with certain bonuses in combat situations besides the obvious.
 Quickdraw: Should you purchase Reflexes level 8, you will be able to grab and use one item during combat. Normally, you have to grab and then use taking two separate actions. This "quick draw" effect does not require a roll. You must have enough hands free to wield the weapon in order to quickly ready it.
Combat Savvy: Upon reaching Reflexes level 12, you will be able to choose when you wish to go in the combat round. Ths ability can only be used if you score the highest total initiative in the round. If you do, you may ellect to go first or at any point during the round, this is called a delay and surpasses the normal rules for Delaying.
