The Uncovered Mysteries

Other Systems
The number of different types of actions that can be attempted are virtually limitless. Trying to make rules or lists for each and every possible action would be simply impossible. We have, however, devised a list of several TNs which are requested quite often by the playing mass which are listed below for both the Storytellers and players to use. If a particular action does not require, or is not affected by any type of random chance, then the action can be assumed to be automatically successful. As always, the Storyteller determines what action do or do not require a roll. When a character attempts an action that the ST determines requires a die roll to succeed, the Storyteller will assign a die roll and a Target Number. If the TN isn't in the charts below, further down is the process used by Storytellers to produce a TN for these situations.

Jumping (Body + Athletics):
All TNs are for distances are listed as horizontal / vertical.

 Distance TN
6 feet/ 3 feet 12
10 feet/ 5 feet 16
14 feet/ 7 feet 20
18 feet/ 9 feet 24
22 feet/ 11 feet 28
26 feet/ 13 feet 32

Jumps over 26 feet are not clearable (for mortal based breeds) without the aid of an auto success. These TNs are for running broad jumps or standing vertical jumps. If no room to get a small running start is available, increase TN difficulties by +2 for horizontal jumps.
Jumping is also possible onto and off of moving vehicles, albeit very dangerous. Should a character attempt such a feat, a +4 difficulty will be added to the TN. Bear in mind that environmental conditions may add or decrease from the TNs, based upon the STs or GMs decision.

Shooting (Body + Appropriate Firearms skill)

 Target TN
Windows 14
Tires 20
Fuel Tank 26

Keep in mind that even though the character might hit the fuel tank, there no chance that an explosion would ever occur, due to the fact that fuel tank do not keep the contents under pressure.
As with firing at a character, a moving target is always more difficult to hit. Should either the target or attacker be moving, a +4 penalty to the TN will be added. If both attacker and target are in motion, a +8 penalty will be added.
Environmental circumstances might add or decrease the TNs. The Storyteller or Game Master has final say.

Climbing (Body + Athletics)

 Surface Type TN
Tree 8
Rope 14
Rough Wall 22
Smooth Wall 28

If attempting to climb any sort of wall without the proper tools to do so (ropes, hammer, spikes, etc.) a +4 penalty will be added to the final TN.
Failure to hit the desired TN does not automatically assume that the character has fallen, merely that he/she cannot move further up or down the surface without another roll. Should two rolls in succession be failed, the character has slipped and fallen.
Environmental conditions may hinder or aid the character in their climb. GMs and STs have final say over target numbers.

Breaking Things (2d6 + Body vs. Objects Body)

Object Body Level
Handheld items 2 Body
Thin Wooden Door 3 Body
Car Window, Passenger * 4 Body
Solid Wooden Door 5 Body
Thin Metal Door 7 Body
Car Window, Windshield * 8 Body
Solid Metal Door 9 Body
Small Tree 11 Body
Cell Door, Prison 12 Body
Large Tree 13 Body
Small Vehicle 15 Body
Cell Door, High Security 14 Body
Medium Vehicle 17 Body
Large Vehicle/Van 19 Body

This system for breaking objects replaces the previous one to disallow characters from bashing down oak trees without taking as much as a bruise. Each time the character successfully makes a roll to break something the Body of the object is reduced by one (Wrestling does not assist in this damage) regardless (spelling error) of what is used to break it down. For every (spelling error) three Body damage done to the object the character breaking it takes one damage, reguardless of soaking or Masteries. This process continues until the object has no Body levels left and breaks at zero Body. Multiple characters breaking the same object can select who takes the point of damage done to them by the object. Characters who fail this roll by five or more lose two points of Body to show that they hit it wrong and have sprained or fractured something. Simply missing the roll incurs no damage to the character. Weapons can not be broken in this fashion.

The addition of a dead bolt, or similar device, makes the target number slightly more difficult. A +1 will be added to the Objects Body per deadbolt placed onto it.

* Most vehicle windows today are designed to not shatter apart when broken. Rather, they remain intact and allow the object to push through, carrying with it a whole piece of glass that is held together by a clear adhesive. This adhesive coating prevents major cuts that would be prevalent in plate glass windows breaking.

Vehicular Chasing

System:
Escaping Driver Quickness + 2d6* vs.
Chasing Driver Body + Athletics + 2d6

In high speed chases, the person with the highest roll wins the chase.

If it is police related, each subsequent car added to the chase gives the person fleeing a -2 (Cumulative for each additional car) to their roll.

For each block the escaping driver pulls away from ("gains on" would be what the chaser does to the escaper) the chasing driver apply a -1 to the chasing drivers roll.

Generic Target Numbers

There are some TNs that a Storyteller may have to make which aren't provided above. Any number of possible TNs may be necessary actually, and under these circumstances the Storyteller will use the following basic guidelines in deciding the proper TN. When the Storyteller decides the TN for a particular feat, they will use the charts above when deciphering exactly what Easy, Moderate, Difficult and Almost Impossible are. The Storyteller will next decide which of the two charts to use provided below, then the ST will decide exactly how difficult the action would be based on the circumstances surrounding the character. This process will conclude with a precise TN produced by the ST in order for the player to attempt whichever feat they requested.

Statistic Roll Target Numbers (Mind/Body/Soul + 2D6)
Easy = 12 and under
Moderate = 12 - 18
Difficult = 19 - 23
Almost Impossible = 24 and up

Modifier Roll Target Numbers (Mind/Body/Soul + Skill Modifier + 2D6)
Easy = 16 and under
Moderate = 17 - 23
Difficult = 24 - 29
Almost Impossible = 30 and up
