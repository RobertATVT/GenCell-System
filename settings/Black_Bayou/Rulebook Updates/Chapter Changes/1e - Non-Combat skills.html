^v^ Step: 3 Non-Combat Skills ^v^


Following is a description of the skills a character might use in just about any situation. The Statistic in parenthesis (after the name of the skill) represents the abilities you use to perform that skill. You may not raise your skill higher than the statistic listed in parenthesis.

As you purchase your skills, it is important to realize a few things. There is a large difference between your characters CS and how they are RP'd. CS's and the majority of the rules are simply supplements to the RP in Black Bayou. However, the CS's and skills do represent a type of balancing between the characters. It is not necessary for the Player to be a certified MD in order for the character to have a medical skill. Granted, an in-depth knowledge of an area always makes RP more interesting, but it is not mandatory. The skills reflect what abilities the character possesses. On the flip-side, of course, the fact that you know a lot about an area, does not necessarily mean your character does. You could be a multimillion dollar lawyer in real life, but if your character does not have any law skills, when it comes to the courtroom your character does not know how to behave.

Please note, if your character does not possess a skill below, they may still attempt the action. In this case, the player will use the same roll listed with the skill, without the benefit of the skill level called for in the roll. 2d6+Body instead of 2d6+Body+Athletics, for instance.

For rolls based on a statistic (there is a Stat in the roll), you must use the current rating in the roll. For example: If your Body was 5 and you take 3 points of damage, you would have 2 Body left. Then, if you attempted a skill and had to add your Body to the roll, you would only add 2 until you heal.

Your first attempt at any Non-Combat Skill roll is made normally. If you roll again inside of an hour for the same thing, your roll will be at -1. Roll again? -2. And so on and so on until that hour is up when the Skill roll will once again be made normally. Remember that after the first attempt and the negative modifiers are added, automatic success with double sixes is no longer applicable until the hour is up and the Skill roll is once again made normally. The only exception is the Luck Skill.

NOTE: Unless otherwise specified or obvious, all Skill durations are 'scene'. Such Skills include Fast Talk, Music, Theology, Tracking, etc.

Sample Skill Listing:
Skill Name (Stat)
Generation Cost: each point listed costs 1 point at character generation.
Experience Point Cost: each level of the skill costs this number of experience points.
System: This is the dice roll you make when attempting to use a skill and the target number required for the character to succeed with the action.
Description: This, naturally, is what the skill does.

Skills are rated by levels. Each level in a skill represents a little more knowledge or aptitude than the proceeding one. Skill levels 1-4 are considered beginners. 5-8 would symbolize intermediate while 9 and above would be viewed as being quite talented.

Note: The exact effects and their target numbers for both Lore and Faith are listed with their respective breed. For instance, if you would like to know how you can use lore or faith against a demon, you would look in the demon section.

Basic Medical (Mind)
Generation Cost: 2 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Mind+Basic Medical Skill Level vs TN 16 + 1 per Body damage taken by the patient
Description: Many people know the basics of first aide. This includes, but is not limited to CPR, burn care, setting broken limbs, and other basic medical attention. When a successful Basic Medical roll is made the patient receives 2 points of lost body. This form of medical treatment may only be performed on the same patient once and only on the same night that specific injury was received. Multiple uses have no effect, regardless of how many people try over how much time. This skill may be used on any breed in their mortal forms.

Major Medical (Mind) [requires Basic Medical Level 3 to learn]
Generation Cost: 3 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 4
System: roll 2d6+Mind+Major Medical Skill Level vs. TN 16 + 1 per Body damage taken by the patient
Description: A select few have undertaken the specialized training necessary to be a surgeon. These individuals are capable of almost any medical feat. When a successful Major Medical roll is made for a patient, they will heal at x 2 their natural rate, over night. Only one attempt may be made per night on the victim by anyone. The x 2 effect only works for that one day. That means the patient must be cared for every day to gain the full healing bonus until they are better.

In addition, the surgeon may attempt emergency life saving procedures if the victim has fallen to a negative Body total. With a successful roll (same as the above), the surgeon can heal the victim to a Body of 1. This counts as the one Major Medical roll for the patient per day, regardless of the doctor. The victim, after being saved, is considered out of play for the rest of the night as they are hospitalized in that type of condition. If the victim is 2 times their Body in the negative or worse, there is no chance of saving them. For instance: Body of 4. If their Body at the time of the emergency procedures was -6, it would still be possible to save them. The target number would be 26 (16 + 10 damage), but it would still be possible. If the same victim had a body of -9, they would be too far gone and would die. The successful use of this skill does not counteract the killing effects of faith objects.

Success of a writing roll allows the character to write a musical piece that others enjoy and want to listen to. Successfully making a play roll represents the characters ability to perform a musical composition well by utilizing voice or more mechanical instrument, not making mistakes, and generally impressing those who listen.

Experience Point Cost: Next level x 4

Description: An adept con artist can easily persuade one or two people while the practiced politician can fool hundreds. This skills works in much the same as Fast-Talk above, except that it can be used on many people at once. Instead of erupting with a flood of information, the politician tends to twist the order of their words, hiding the true meaning.

Special Effects (Mind)
Generation Cost: 3 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Mind+Special Effects Skill Level vs TN see below
Description: Another form of entertainment, special effects are used in many different mediums, from concerts (stage explosions) to Hollywood movies (make-up, stage explosions). Characters may utilize this ability to create and handle stage flames and explosions as well as create suitable disguises. While the effects of this skill can look harmful, damage is never incurred. The target number varies, depending on the situation. The Storyteller has the final say, but should follow this scale: small, flashy pyrotechnics / slight facial feature changes = 16; medium size explosive effects / drastic appearance change = 18; large explosive pyrotechnics / the most extensive changes to appearance = 20; huge explosive pyrotechnics / mimic the appearance of another = 30 (Note: You will not completely mimic another unless you achieve a TN of 30 with this skill and a TN of 30 with Acting) With this skill no more than +/- 3 inches may be altered nor +/- 40 lbs in weight. The Final TN is left to the Storyteller to determine and may be modified by circumstance.

System: roll 2d6+Writing Skill Level vs TN 14 to write

Description: All people have some form of writing talent, however, there are a select few who have such skill that they are able to affect peoples beliefs and possibly even change some of them. From short stories to novels, writing can be an invaluable skill. When writing, for each roll 3 higher than TN 16 the player gains 1 to their Finance roll that night. Hacking (Mind)
Generation Cost: 3 points per level
Experience Point Cost: Next Level x 3
System: roll 2d6+Mind+Hacking Skill Level vs. TN (see below)
Description: The black sheep of the computer world. Information is out there and these people want it. Money, a clean slate, your cousin's birthday, these people maneuver their way around information the way you maneuver around in a car. Knowledge is power, and they are taking all they can. Those who have knowledge of programming computers gain a certain edge when they try to hack into another program or system. They are capable of using that programming knowledge to assist with their hacking skills. Characters with a hacking skill equal or greater than their programming skill may add those levels of programming to their hacking roll.

Type of System You're Hacking: Target Number
A program to rewrite its code 16
A standard home system 20
A grade-A store bought computer 26
A professional security system 32
A standard government system 38
A top-secret system 44

Hacking may be attempted as many times as the character would like. However, failure does have it's price. If a character ever fails to hack a program or computer system in person (missing the Target Number by 10 points or more), they have either caused a malfunction or some other kind of permanent damage. No further hacking attempts may be made for the rest of the evening on that program or system or until the program or system has been repaired. Should a hacker fail a roll by 10 points or more on a remote system (i.e., hacking over the phone lines or into a network), then they have been caught. Depending on the circumstances, they may be traced, physically discovered or suffer other consequences, which is all Storyteller discretion. No further attempts may be made on that same system or network for the rest of the night.
