^v^ Lycanthrope Masteries ^v^

The Lycanthrope is neither good nor evil as the genetic change to their system doesn't effect their overall psyche. However, the mighty powers of the genetic change transforms a standard mortal into a hunting machine, one of the most devastating creatures Mother Nature has ever invented. The genetic change gives the lycanthrope powers unknown by standard man while cursing the lycanthrope at the same time. To use a mastery, lycanthropes tap into the power of the virus in their blood and use this power to greatly enhance the abilities of their body and mind. Though a lycanthrope may not be evil, while in Horus they do not have any control over their actions and feel nothing but the blinding rage of the viral curse in full effect.

Sample Mastery Listing:
Mastery Name Soul Cost per use. (room command for activation)
Mastery point cost at Generation / Mastery cost with Experience Points
System/Duration: the roll required to activate the mastery and the target number needed for the character to succeed / how long the effects of the masteries last. The role-played effects of certain masteries can last a lot longer than the combat effects. Also listed here is the number of rounds it takes to activate the mastery.
Effect/Description: This is a description of the mastery and its effects.

For most lycanthrope mastery use, you will need to do three things. First, your character must harness the energy within required to activate the mastery. Whether this be concentrating on a target, chanting a ritual, screaming at the top of your lungs, etc. Then you must enter the room command. This command is listed in parenthesis on the top line of the mastery's description. They are used to alert the host and the other players that you are using a mastery. The other characters may not be aware of this, but their players should be. During the duration of the mastery, you burn the fuel in order to perform it. Therefore, once the mastery's duration is concluded you will expend the soul listed. You must decrease your character's soul statistic by each point listed to the right of the mastery's name. The final thing you have to do is follow the rest of the description. This is usually a dice roll and upon success, some sort of effect.

Range of Effect definitions:
Masteries include four different areas of effect. These are: Self, Touch, Forced Eye Contact (FEC), and Line of Sight (LOS). Below are the description of how these work. Some masteries will not apply to these ranges such as Extended EMS.

Self ~ Masteries with a range of Self can only be used on the user, there are no exceptions. Some examples of this are the various shapechanging masteries the breeds have (Minor Cat Form, Bat Form, Horus Form, etc...)

Touch ~ Masteries with a range of touch require you to touch the person through RP or if resisted by using 2d6 + Body vs. the victims 2d6 + Body (Both must be witnessed by a Host or ST). This contact must be maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

(e.g. it takes two rounds to activate a mastery being used on someone then you must hold onto them the full two rounds)

FEC ~ Forced eye Contact Masteries can only be used within conversation distance (roughly 15 feet). In order to catch a persons eye and maintain contact the user must roll 2d6 + Will vs. the targets 2d6 + Inits (This pits the users will against the targets ability to be quick enough to not look or break contact). This contact must be maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

LOS ~ Line of Sight Masteries require just that, a line of sight. The target of the mastery can be any distance as long as they can see the target. This contact must be maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

Note: Lycanthrope Masteries are split in to five sections, General, Wolf, Bear, Gator, and Quttah. A Lycanthrope may only buy from General and their animal type.

Agility - 1 Soul to use. (##Agility##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Body vs TN 16 / Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: The body of the Lycanthrope possesses a natural animal quickness, which allows them to react faster than normal. This mastery can be used in all forms of the lycanthrope, as the animal within them has turned their instincts to animal based, not human. This mastery adds +3 to all defense rolls (i.e. Evasion and Reflexes).

Call The Pack - 1 Soul to use. (>>::pack:: <<)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: NA / Instant; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: Having such an incredible bond between pack mates, lycnathropes have learned to fine tune Mindspeak during times of war. The lycanthrope can spend a turn shouting out a one sentence Mindspeak to each of its pack mates, regardless of distance. Each pack mate must have the mastery Call The Pack to be included in the Mindspeak. The pack must spend an evening attuning their minds together (and this log file must be posted) for the mastery to work. This attunement only needs to take place once, unless a new member is added or removed. At that point, the attunement must be renewed. The pack member must be available (online) in order to receive this call. They may not necessarily be in their pack name SN. It is okay to IM the message normally, but if the pack member is on an unknown SN or not online, you cannot Email the message. Pack members are considered in 'dead zones' during these circumstances and will not receive your message.

Dispas - 1 Soul to use. (##Dispas##)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 20 / Indefinitely; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: Cunning animals can sometimes blend in with its surroundings when danger is near. For all intents and purposes, the lycanthrope is considered to be totally invisible to the eye and all waves of the light spectrum, including infrared, etc. However, even while not visible, the lycanthrope -can- be heard and smelled, should those methods be available to others. The lycanthrope will remain in this state until it drops the cover or attacks its prey. Attacks made while under the cover of Dispas are granted a +5 bonus for surprise. Lycanthropes under dispas cannot be targeted for attacks, as they cannot be seen. Lycanthropes also cannot target others for attack as that would be too strenuous. In this case, 'targeting' is the ability to use skills or masteries to enhance damage or hit a certain spot on someone. No Aiming, Rending, Sure Shot, etc. If a character has a Mastery that allows them to recognize hidden things (Heightened Awareness, Enhanced Senses, etc.) and they SAW the lycanthrope change into Dispas, they can roll their Mastery to 'see' where the lycanthrope is, provided the lycanthrope has not yet left the area.

Extended Mindspeak - 1 Soul to use. (requires mindspeak)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: roll 2d6+Will vs TN 20 / Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: The lycanthrope has learned to seek others out by further expanding the mastery : Mindspeak. This allows the lycanthrope to communicate via Mindspeak to a target within one mile. For every mile after the first, a modifier of +3 is added to the target number. The communication is only one way, unless the target also has the mastery: Extended Mindspeak. No roll is necessary unless the lycanthrope is in combat. If combat is initiated while this mastery is in effect, the lycanthrope must reactivate it due to the stress of combat.

This allows an extra +1 to damage from physical hand-to-hand attacks. This damage bonus only applies while the character is in their mortal form.

System/Duration: NA/NA

In addition, when the lighting is low, or near total darkness, the vision of the lycanthrope takes on the effects of infravision. Unable to make out detail, the lycanthrope can discern between animate and inanimate objects, sensing their red or blue glow, respectively.

Predators Eyes 1 Soul to use. (##Pr Eyes##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25

Lycanthropes that utilize their sense of smell can effectively "see" in complete darkness. It can distinguish between the many different scents around him and can pinpoint their exact location. This allows the lycanthrope to ignore any penalties due to vision. Strong winds or powerful smells can throw the lycanthrope off. The nuances are up to the room host, ST or GM.

Effect/Description: By baring it's claws and maws and issuing forth a frightening roar, the lycanthrope can paralyze its victim with fear. If successful, the victim will be at -4 to Attack and Quickness for the duration of the mastery. The victim snaps out of this stunning fear as the instinct for survival kicks in.

Dulled Pain - 1 Soul to use. (##Dulled Pain##)
Generation Cost: 10 / Experience Cost: 50
System/Duration: NA / Scene; Instant
Effect/Description: Due to the lycanthrope's innate healing system and mind set, its body is able to handle the stress of physical attacks better than any other creature. They are more resilient to the damage they can take before pain begins to affect their abilities. A lycanthrope with Dulled Pain ignores changes to their modifiers that would normally take effect through the loss of Body for the first 3 points of damage. See Below Example:
 Damage Suffered Effective Damage
1-3 0
4 1
5 2
6 3
etc... etc...
With this example A werewolf with 10 Body that just suffered a pistol shot would normally be at 7 body and all body effecting modifiers would drop by 3. With Dulled Pain however they would be affected at all. If that same wolf was hit by another gunshot for 3 body damage, they would now be at a -3 to all Body affecting mods, instead of -6. Reguardless when the character is dropped to 0 natural Body, they pass out all the same.

If this mastery is used on another Player Character in an animal form, the target number is the victim's 2d6+Will.

Scent Recognition - 1 Soul to use. (##Scent ## / ##Track ##)
Generation Cost: 5 / Experience Cost: 25
System/Duration: To memorize a scent, roll 2d6+Body vs target 2d6+Mind (TN 16 for inanimate objects) / Perm; takes 1 round to activate. To track a scent, roll 2d6+Body vs TN 20 / Scene; takes 1 round to activate.
Effect/Description: A werewolf can use the scent of others as a way of identifying them, even if they can't be seen. This can be useful when they enter a large room and are looking for a specific person. The werewolf will automatically recognize any scent he encounters, if it has previously memorized the scent. Whether they are in the same room or out in an open field, the werewolf will always know if the memorized scent is near. By concentrating on a single scent, the werewolf can pick out its direction. There is no way to mask the scent once the werewolf has memorized it. Though people often attempt to used sealed containers or strong odors, the scent is almost more mystical in nature than physical in nature. Only one scent may be tracked at a time and if it leads beyond a mile, a roll is required. For every mile beyond the first, another roll is required, with a cumulative +2 to the TN. Each level of the Tracking Skill will reduce the Scent Recognition TN by one.
