[ Step: 2 Character Creation ]


Step 1: Occupations
 Everyone has 50 points to purchase Occupations, Skills, and Masteries/Cybernetics.

Step 2: Statistics
 Record the Statistics that your Occupation gives you in the appropriate spaces.

Setp 2: Modifiers
Calculate your Modifiers
(Pr) Prowess = Body x 2 (Split between Close and Ranged)
(In) Intellect = Mind x 2 (Split between Knowledge and Technical)
(Di) Discipline = Soul x 2 (Split between Control and Defense)
(Co) Coordination = Body + Mind (Split between Dodge and Initiative)
(Ch) Charisma = Body + Soul (Split between Appearance and Manipulation)
(Wi) Willpower = Mind + Soul (Split between Self and Others)

Step 3: Spend Creation Points
Each Character has 50 Creation points to spend.
These can be spent in the following ways:
Skills - as per Skill CP Cost
Occupations - as per Occupation CP Cost
Statistics - 3 CP's to raise a Statistic by 1 point.
Equipment/Gear - Cost of Item divided by 100.

Step 4: Spend Special Interest Points
Each Character has 5 points to put into Special Interests

Step 5: Finishing Touches
 Select Phenotype (If able too play something other than Mortal) and add Innate Masteries and Stat Changes


[ Character Details ]


When you wrote your character's background, you began to understand how you'd expect your character to turn out as a living being. The next few steps of the character creation process involves detailing the technical aspects of your character. The Character Sheet (CS) is what we use to measure our character's physical, mental and spiritual selves. We use numbers to signify their strengths and weaknesses. The character sheets are then used to aid us in fairly determining whether or not our characters are successful in attempting certain actions: combat, masteries, etc.. Send the completed CS in an Email message addressed to HOST ANT Aegis.

[ Occupations ]


Starting off every character has 50 points to purchase their occupation, skills, masteries, and statistics. The first thing to buy is an occupation, everyone has an occupation in this game as it determines your base statistics.

Everyone on Europa does something. Everyone works, everyone contributes, and everyone serves a purpose. Occupational Training types are general groups of skills with special advantages that reflect what it might be like to have a specific job. Each training type determines your characters base statistics and offers certain advantages bestowed upon your character as they learn how to perform their selected career. Just like real life, there are often opportunities made available in time for your character to change careers. Please keep in mind that just because your character knows how to perform a certain job does not mean they actually have the job in character. You may need to seek out certain individuals to actually obtain the job of your character.

[ Statistics ]

 Body (??xp) ~ 0 Mind (?? xp) ~ 0 Soul (?? xp) ~ 0

Statistics are the way we measure our character's physical, mental and spiritual aspects. These three statistics, together represent the core of our character's being. All other entries on the character sheet have their foundations based on these three statistics. For ease of record, we have provided a space next to your Statistics to keep track of how much the next level of the Statistic costs in XP. The statistics are:

Body is the physical aspect of your character. It represents your character's strength, agility and resilience. Body is used for many different things. Any die roll that requires your character act physically is affected by their body statistic. Running, throwing, attacking, dodging and many other physical actions are included. It also determines how much physical punishment your character can sustain before they are rendered unconscious or dead. Body replenishes at a rate of one every three days, unless caused by burn damage in which it returns at a rate of one a week.

Mind is the mental aspect of your character. It represents your character's speed of thought, general knowledge and their capacity for reason. Mind is used for many different things. Any die roll that requires your character to think, reason or otherwise exercise their mind is affected by their mind statistic. This includes, but is not limited to the use of many Masteries, solving logic problems or using skills that require the character to think. It also determines how much mental punishment your character can sustain before they fall unconscious. Loss of Mind is recovered at the rate of 1 point for every half hour of rest.

Soul is the spiritual aspect of your character. It represents your character's strength of the spirit and mystical energy. Any die roll that requires the use of a character's inner strength is affected by the soul statistic. Examples are: a showing of great faith, resisting an addiction, etc.. Soul also determines how many times per night a character may use their Masteries. Each mastery has a soul cost listed in its description and each night a character can only use a number of masteries who's cost does not exceed their soul. (For example: Tasha is a Heretic Pit Fighter using her Telekinetic Knives, it costs 2 Soul to use it each time. Since she has a six soul, she can only use it three times that night.) Soul that is spent or drained is recovered at a rate of one point for every hour of rest.

NOTE: A characters Statistics are determined by their Occupation.

The next section on the character sheet is where the modifiers are listed. These modifiers are used to alter your die rolls to reflect your individual character's chances to succeed at the action in question. Half of the base modifiers are determined by doubling one Statistic, the last three are done by adding two Statistics together. Each Base Modifier is then split into two Sub Modifiers. These final Modifiers will be what you use to modify your Skills or rolls in most cases, though there will be situations that may change that. (Sub-Mods cannot be more than 6 points in difference.)

Prowess (Pr) is the Modifier group used when your character wishes to attack or otherwise strike at a specific target. Whether it is a slap, punch, kick, knife slash, club bash, you use your character's Prowess Modifier to hit the target. To determine your character's Prowess Modifier, multiply the character's Body by two. This number is recorded next to Prowess on the character sheet. Once that is done, you need to decide if Ranged combat or close combat is more important and split your prowess accordingly.

Intellect (In) is the Modifier group that's used when you your character is wanting to do something technical or involving knowledge. This is used for repairing items, remembering facts, recalling lore or other information. To determine your characters Intellect you multiply your Mind by two. This number is recorded next to Intellect on the Character Sheet. Once this is done you decide which is more important to you, knowledges or technical skills.

Discipline (Di) is the Modifier Group that's used anytime an issue of self control comes up. When you need to control yourself, or fend off a mental attack, you use Discipline. Discipline is determined by multiplying your Soul by two. This number is recorded next to Disipline on the character sheet. Once this is done you decide which is more important to you, controlling yourself or fending off mental attacks and Masteries.

Coordination (Co) is the Modifier Group is used when your character wishes to attempt an action quicker than normal. It represents your character's physical and mental speed and reaction time. It is most often used during combat or during other scenes where time is of the essence. To determine your character's Coordination, add the character's Body and Mind statistics together. This number is recorded next to Coordination on the character sheet. Once you get this done decide Which is more important to you, Initiative or dodge.

Charisma (Ch) is the Modifier Group that you use when you want to interact on a social level with others. This modifier determies how polite, attractive, smooth, etc... the character is. This group is used when you wanna Fast-Talk, use Theology, Public Speaking, or any Social Skill. To determine your Charisma you add your Body and Soul together. This is then recorded next to Charisma on your character sheet. Once you have the base modifier determined you need to decide which is more important, your appearance or ability to manipulate others.

Willpower (Wi) is the modifier used when your character wishes to exert control or manipulate the world around them. It is also used mainly to activate many of the masteries that someone may attempt to use on someone. To determine your character's Willpower Modifier, add the character's Mind and Soul statistics together. This number is recorded next to Willpower on the character sheet. Once this is done determine which is more important, activating masteries on yourself or using them against others.

[ Skills ]


Throughout the lives of our characters, they have undoubtedly learned how to do this thing and that. The next step is to determine exactly what you character knows how to do at the time they join the story. When you create your character, you are allowed to spend 50 points (Generation Points) to purchase your character's starting Occupation, what you have left can be used to raise your statistics or to purchase Skills and Masteries. Beside the description of individual skills and masteries you'll find two separate costs. The first is listed as a Generation Cost and the second is Experience Point Cost. While spending your 50 Generation Points, you use the cost listed as the Generation Cost. Later on, you will be introduced to Experience Points and Experience Point Costs.

w Skills represent the talents and traits you character has either learned through a teacher or picked up as a necessity of life. There are many types of skills your character already
knows or could learn: weapon skills, hand-to-hand fighting skills, technical skills, social skills and many others.

Sample Skill Listing:
Skill Name (Stat) Damage: How much damage the skill causes, if any.
Generation Cost: each point listed costs 1 point at character generation.
Experience Point Cost: each level of the skill costs this number of experience points.
Requirement: Any skill levels that must be obtained in another skill before this one may be pruchased. (Not listed for every skill)
System: This is the dice roll you make when attempting to use a skill and the target number required for the character to succeed with the action.
Description: This, naturally, is what the skill does.
Level/Effect: Certain abilities do not become usable until the skill reaches a specific level, these are listed here in detail.

Skills are rated by levels. Each level in a sill represents a little more knowledge or aptitude than the proceeding one. Skill levels of 1-4 are considered beginners. 5-8 would symbolize intermediate while 9 and above would be viewed as quite talented.

Each skill requires the use of one specific statistic. This statistic is listed to the right of the skill's name in parenthesis. The skill level may never be a higher number than the statistic listed in parenthesis.

[ Masteries & Cybernetics ]


w Masteries are the "supernatural" abilities unique to each phenotype that the characters may already know or may learn. These abilites are generally fueled byt the supernatural elements of spirit that we all posess, though some breeds are cut completely from that ebb of flux, while others are completely emerged into it.

w Qualities are the special abilities that every human can display at some point in their life, some however learn to hone that quality into a nearly supernatural ability. These abilities are free from the normal stigma that those that wield the flux have, since they are not powered by any odd supernatural origin.

w Cybernetics are the "technical" equipment that some phenotypes may have added to them. This equipment is listed here because of the unique advantages they bestow but it is just equipment. These items cost a minimal amount of XP to purchase and cost mainly Credits, though each and every person can only hold so much before the pull of energy becomes to great for their mind to hold, and insanity eventually takes over.

w Innate Qualities are the Special Abilities that are completely unique to each phenotype. No other phenotype may learn them, though some powers may closely duplicate them. Some Innate Qualities may be raised with CP's or Experience.

Sample Mastery Listing:
[ Mastery Name ] Soul Cost per use (room command for activation)
Mastery point cost at Generation/Mastery cost with Experience Points
Level: The level of the mastery.
Requirement: If there is a prerequisite for a mastery, it is listed here.
System/Duration: The roll required to activate the mastery and the target number needed for the character to succeed/how long the effects of the masteries last. The role-played effects of certain masteries can last a lot longer than the combat effects. Also listed here is the number of rounds it takes to activate the mastery.
Range/effect: This is the way the mastery will be conducted in order to be cast. This will include Self, Touch, Forced Eye Contact (FEC), and Line of Sight (LOS)
Durability: The durability for Cybernetics
Body Location: Where the cybernetic device is located in the body.
Effect/Description: This is a description of the mastery and its effects.

Most of the time your character tries to use a mastery, you will need to do three things. First, your character must harness the energy within required to activate the mastery. Whether this be concentrating on a target, focusing their energy, screaming at the top of your lungs, etc. Then you must enter the room command. This command is listed in parenthesis on the top line of the mastery's description. They are used to alert the host and the other players that you are using a mastery. The other characters may not be aware of this, but their players should be. During the duration of the mastery, you burn the fuel in order to perform it. Therefore, once the mastery's duration is concluded you will expend the soul listed. You must decrease your character's soul statistic by each point listed to the right of the mastery's name. The final thing you have to do is follow the rest of the description. This is usually a dice roll and upon success, some sort of effect.

Range of Effect definitions
Masteries include four different areas of effect. These are: Self, Touch, Forced Eye Contact (FEC), and Line of Sight (LOS). Below are the description of how these work. Some masteries will not apply to these ranges such as Extended EMS.

w Self: Masteries with a range of Self can only be used on the user, there are no exceptions.

w Touch: Masteries with a range of touch require you to touch the person through RP or if resisted by using 2d6 + Close Combat+Skill vs. the victims 2d6 + Dodge (Both must be witnessed by a Host or ST). This contact must be maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

[e.g. it takes two rounds to activate a mastery being used on someone then you must hold onto them the full two rounds]

w FEC: Forced eye Contact Masteries can only be used within conversation distance (roughly 15 feet). In order to catch a persons eye and maintain contact the user must roll 2d6 + Willpower (external) vs. the targets 2d6 + Discipline (control) (This pits the users will against the targets ability to control themselves). This contact must be maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

w LOS: Line of Sight Masteries require just that, a line of sight. The target of the mastery can be any distance as long as they can see the target. This contact must be
maintained for the entire activation time of the mastery and if the contact is broken before it is successfully activated the mastery fails.

[ Target Numbers ]


When your character attempts an action that requires a die roll, there must be a means of knowing whether or not they succeed. This is the purpose of a target number. Many skills, masteries and other rolls will have a target number listed in the description of the die roll. When you make the die roll, if the total result (a combination of the die roll and any other additions to it either from statistics, modifiers or skills) is equal to or exceeds
the target number, your character succeeds in the action. If this total falls short of the target number, your character fails in the attempt.

Note: Should you roll double six's on any roll, regardless of the target number, you automatically succeed. This allows for those rare instances where we occasionally accomplish something we normally wouldn't be able to. This applies to combat as well. As always, ties go to the defender.

[ Updates ]


Every Character Sheet must be updated every 2 months (Jan 3rd - March 3rd - May 3rd - etc..), if this isn't done the character becomes inactive and any unsaved XP will be lost. There are five kinds of Character sheet updates:

 Normal Update: This is where you simply spend what experience points you have earned and update your CS. Once you update a CS you cannot do so again for 30 days (Cs was Updated or Created on January 3rd, you can't update it again until at least February 3rd, but if you wait longer than March 3rd you lose your XP)
 Non-Update: This is where you take all the XP you have earned and bank them for later expenditure. What this does is keep all your XP's on record so that youcan save them for something large or just until you decide on what to do with them. After an approved Non-Update (NU) you can update at any time.
 Reactivation: When your character has passed the 2 month limit for Character Sheet updates they will need to be Reactivated. When this happens all XP that were not previously banked are lost.
 Freezing: Anytime you will have to leave the game for any justified period of time (Moving, losing AOL, medical issues, etc...) You can freeze your character up to six months. When you do this, all special benefits are held until you can return to the game. If you donot return and Reactivate the CS within that time, all special status (Breed Leader, etc..) is revoked and you will become a normal member of that Breed.
