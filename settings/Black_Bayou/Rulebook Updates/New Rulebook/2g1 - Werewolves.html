When you think of a wolf, what comes to mind? The nasty beast dressed up like a sweet little grandmother to fool Little Red Riding Hood? The big bad wolf that blew the Three Little Pigs house down? In many fairy tales and cartoons wolves are portrayed as sly and sneaky, or just plain mean. Like most wild animals, though, they seldom seek out confrontations with humans or other wolf packs, unless the latter has crossed into their territory.

Wolves are beautiful, intelligent animals who are loyal to each other. They like company and live in packs - the pack always has a leader, the Alpha, chosen for his strength, wisdom, and cunning. Wolf families are affectionate and cooperative. They play and hunt together. They protect each other, too. If one wolf finds itself in some sort of trouble, the others will try to help it. A wolf does live alone, but it is a hard life without the safety of the pack. Lone wolves often die.

A werewolf is a mortal with a genetic virus. This virus, transmitted by the bite of another werewolf, merges itself with the cells of the mortal. There is no known cure. The werewolf appears to be a normal mortal in all respects. It is now half-man half-beast, however. A monstrous force dwells deep within the werewolf's soul, constantly searching for an opportunity to escape. This, coupled with the raw instinct that occasionally overcomes a werewolf, makes the newly created among them rather dangerous, that is, until they learn to control their newfound animal natures.

The Becoming: To become a werewolf, you must first be chosen by the creator. This werewolf must spend 6 hours in a ritual (must be posted) in which they focus on their target. Dancing around a bonfire, occasionally tossing in something personal of the werewolf-to-be. At the conclusion of the ritual, the werewolf goes immediately Horus and then tracks down the target. The tracking is automatic and the Horus will take whatever means necessary to reach the target. Once the hunt has ended, the werewolf will attack the target, attempting to bite them. Unfortunately for most, the Horus is a maddened beast nearly impossible to control. In order for the werewolf to control itself and not kill the target, they must attempt to control the insane beast they have become. To do so, they must successfully roll 2d6+Will vs. TN: 20. If the Werewolf fails this roll, they tear the victim to pieces in a blind rage. Should this roll be successful, as well as a successful attack roll for the bite itself, the green froth around its mouth enters the targets bloodstream? One bite is enough and the Horus will immediately flee, having bitten its target.

Over the next few days or weeks, the virus will work its way through the target's body. On the night of the next full moon, the target will unexpectedly fly into Horus for the first time and will immediately begin a hunt. The player must then roll 2d6+Will Vs a TN of 24. Success means the werewolf will find the scent of a Mortal (Psychic or Lamia included) and track down their intended target. If the roll is failed, the creature will follow the scents of people, wherever they lead and attack at random. (The host on duty will roll a die to determine the wolf's target.) Its not unheard of for a werewolf to attack a city or town during its first transformation. In fact, that is where some of the rumors about werewolf attacks occurring during the full moon come form. Once it kills its first victim, (for gaming purposes, once it has reduced the CS to 0 Body or below) the newly created werewolf will change back to mortal form, the curse complete (this must be witnessed by a GM or ST and posted). If the werewolf is killed in its attack (Body reduced to negative its Body total as per the Mortal breed) they will die as they haven't completely reverted to werewolf until AFTER successfully accomplishing this attack. If there is some reason that the werewolf-to-be missed the full moon (captured underground, etc) they will turn Horus each evening upon return/release (once they can see the sky again) during the hours of darkness until they either kill a victim or are killed themselves.

Please note: only one werewolf may be created by any single werewolf per week.

Mortality: Mortal with regenerative abilities (Werewolf lore level 3 is required to kill a member of this breed).

Means of Death: There is only one thing that can truly kill a werewolf (see Lore Level 3 below for details). However, there is one other way to seriously injure one. Regular fire will cause 2 points of damage per round the werewolf is exposed to it. These wounds, while not deadly to a werewolf, can cause serious damage since they cannot be healed normally. See Healing below for more details. Werewolves age at a very reduced rate and could theoretically live almost forever.

Please note: If you have the following level of lore, this is what your character thinks they know about Werewolves. Some are fact and some are myth. If it is not listed in the information above or specifically stated below, it is myth.

Lore Level 1: You have watched many horror movies and that is where you get most of your knowledge from. Of course, most of what you might believe is untrue. You believe simply shooting a werewolf with a silver bullet will kill them. You believe them to be creatures of the night who change into a horrific beast by the light of a full moon. You believe there are physical signs of the werewolf (unibrow, fingers of the same length, etc.) You believe they cannot touch silver objects. You are wary of the dreaded bite of the werewolf, as you still believe a random bite will curse you as well.

Wolfsbane: Wolfsbane is a plant of varying size originally found in Europe. How the plant managed to make its way to the United States is anyone's guess. The spores of this plant that grow just beneath the petals of the yellowish flowers are believed to have a particularly distasteful odor that can only be noticed by canines. Whether this is true or not is irrelevant. It's the faith in the horrible stench of the herb that causes werewolves to be repulsed by it. Wolfsbane can be found in most herbal shops around the country. Holding the wolfsbane aloft, the character must concentrate, focusing their faith on the herb and the werewolf they are attempting to ward off. This requires a roll of 2d6+Soul+Faith vs. the werewolf's 2d6+Soul+Faith. If the character's roll beats the werewolf's roll, they are successful. The werewolf must flee the scene immediately for the next 4 rounds / 2 minutes. Nothing will dissuade the werewolf from fleeing until the duration wears off and it even works on a werewolf in Horus form. This is a blind panic and the only actions the werewolf may take are those that enable it to get as far away as possible. Should there be no place to run, the werewolf will collapse to the ground, cowering in fear. If the werewolf is successful, they may ignore the affects and will be immune to any further attempts of warding by the same character for 4 rounds / 2 minutes. The failed use of wolfsbane will only serve to enrage the werewolf, causing it to attack the wielder as their next action however they are able. The werewolf will also change into their Horus form (if not already there) unless they attempt to resist the change with a standard roll. It is possible for one werewolf to use this warding method on another of its kind, but it requires that the one warding have Steel Will 8 and Faith 8. NOTE: The target must be able to see the person warding and the wolfsbane for this warding to work.

Lore Level 2: You have done an incredible amount of reading on the subject of werewolves. Having read everything from the local booksellers', to the more arcane legends, you believe to know the truth. Most of this information is, of course, false. You believe that any type of silver can kill a werewolf, once it enters the bloodstream. You also know that werewolves can shape shift into the form of a wolf. You still believe, however, that the werewolves' change into the monstrous form is related only to the full moon. You also realize merely touching silver has no effect on them. Having studied werewolves for so long, you've begun to pick up on their more subtle abilities. When a werewolf uses a mental mastery with you as the target within line of sight from you, roll 2d6+Mind+2 vs. a TN: 20. It matters not if you know the characters breed - your mind will still attempt to defend itself from attack, even though your physical self had no realization of the process. If you succeed, you recognize the ability and its effect, giving you a one time +3 to the resistance roll (if any) for that mastery. (masteries requiring the werewolf use Will, Mind or Soul rolls only; cannot be used while in direct combat).

Bane Water: Bane water is little more than water used to boil the wolfsbane herb that has been empowered with the character's faith. It takes 10 minutes to empower enough water for 1 attack. The water must have been used to boil wolfsbane and posted in the ST's Lair message board area each day it is used. The empowering with faith must be role-played in the main room for the entire 10 minutes for it to work. The character must put the bane water in whatever item they intend to attack the werewolf with prior to the empowering. These objects can be any small item that can hold water. Balloons, vials, squirt guns, etc. Only 3 attacks worth of bane water may be empowered at one time. If the character attempts to make more than 3 attacks worth, the first amount looses its ability, etc. The empowered water may only be used by the individual that created it. The roll to empower the water is 2d6+Soul+Faith vs TN 22. This roll must be witnessed and approved my either a room host, ST or GM. Attacks made against the werewolf are made as normal, depending on the method being used to hold the water. For instance, balloons and vials are thrown while squirt guns use the small arms skill. If an attack is successful, the werewolf suffers 1 point of damage that cannot be healed supernaturally. The werewolf must heal the damage as any normal mortal would. That would mean each point of damage would take 3 days. Major Medical is the only medical skill that will help the werewolf heal faster at the maximum rate of x2.

Lore Level 3: You have studied werewolves most of you life, and perhaps you've even known a few. The rituals required to pass the curse of lycanthropy are known to you. Having studied werewolves for so long, you've begun to pick up on their more subtle abilities. When a werewolf uses a mental mastery with you as the target within line of sight from you, roll 2d6+Mind+3 vs. a TN: 20. It matters not if you know the characters breed - your mind will still attempt to defend itself from attack, even though your physical self had no realization of the process. If you succeed, you recognize the ability and its effect, giving you a one time +5 to the resistance roll (if any) for that mastery. (masteries requiring the werewolf use Will, Mind or Soul rolls only; cannot be used while in direct combat).

Sight Recognition: There are two methods of identifying a Werewolf by sight. Not the identity of the person, but the identity of the breed. Only through long hours of study (and the prerequisite Lore level 3) are these methods possible. The first is by physically seeing the true form of the Werewolf, which happens to be Horus. If you wish to identify the Werewolf as a breed, roll 2d6 + Mind vs. TN 14. If the roll is successful, you have gathered enough knowledge to understand that you are actually looking at a Werewolf, and not some illusion of the eyes. The second method is by observing the Werewolf transform either to or from Horus. If this is physically witnessed and there is a Room Host, ST or GM, the observer automatically recognizes what others may think of as 'swamp gas' or 'light flashes' as a Werewolf.

Silver Bullet: To kill a werewolf, they must first be brought down to the negative total of their body statistic. A body of 8 would have to be brought down to -8, for instance. Once this is accomplished, it becomes possible to get around the werewolf's natural healing abilities. Werewolves happen to be susceptible to a silver bullet that penetrates their brain. The bullet used must be made of silver. The bullet has to be a solid slug, like that from a handgun. Unlike a shotgun loaded with silver pellets that spray over an area. Once the appropriate body number is achieved, the attacker must fire the silver bullet directly into the fallen werewolf's head. A full round of uninterrupted concentration is necessary as they summon their faith, focusing it on the bullet as it is fired. This is when a roll of 2d6+Soul+Faith vs TN 26 must be rolled successfully - no roll is required for hitting the prone werewolf so long as the range is point blank. A ST or GM must be present. If the roll is successful, the silver will kill the werewolf instantly. The empowerment does not last any longer than the round following the empowerment and if it misses (at long range) it will not kill the werewolf. No character may attempt to empower a killing weapon more than once an hour. The werewolf will appear to be mortal after it has died.

Mandatory Role-play Requirements

1) When a mortal becomes a Werewolf, it must learn to control the bestial nature that will soon overcome them. Anytime even the slightest thing might upset a werewolf during the 3 days that make up a full moon (the day before, day of and day after), they must roll 2d6+Will vs TN: 24. If this roll is failed, the werewolf will change to Horus, the target being whatever caused the irritation. After the first three months, the werewolf has learned to control their inner beast enough so they can control the random full moon changes.

2) Werwolves tend to gain a pack mentality as time goes by and as the wolf within them saturates their spirit. After the three month time that a werewolf gets used to this new rage within them, they begin to interact in a pack like fashion. This has it's drawbacks as well as it's merits. If at anytime the wolf is cornered by itself and threatened it will panic. The wolf will suffer a -1 penalty to all combat actions (All Modifiers suffer a -1 for the duration of the confrontation) because they aren't accustomed to being on their own, wolves hunt in packs and panic and strike out when forced into a solitary stance. Foremost this fear causes a fight or flight response in them. As soon as the first blow is thrown, the wolf must roll 2d6 + Will vs TN 32. If they succeed they are fine and resist the urges and can act normally (without the -1 penalty in combat). If they fail the roll they will immediately change to wolf form and flee the area as quickly as possible. The wolf will attempt to use any methods possible in order to escape the situation but they will not fight. If the agressors grapple or trap the wolf, thus providing no means of escape, the wolf will immediately fly into the Horus rage as mentioned below. If they fail the roll by 5 or more or if they botch (Rolling double ones), the cornered wolf will fly into a Horus rage and begin lashing out at the nearest agressor. They are restricted to Mastery resistance and usage as per the description of Horus

3) The mood of the werewolf depends on the phase of the moon (to find out what phase the moon is in, either look outside or ask a Room Host.) Below is a description of how the phases of the moon affect a Werewolf.

New Moon - The werewolf if at its most calm and friendly. Some wolves become blissful or even giddy as this phase comes around. A werewolf is nearly impossible to anger in this time period and can remain calm under the most stressful situations. If you have a mastery that assists going in Horus or forces someone to go horus the difficulty is raised by +6 or the defender gets a +6 bonus to resist. (Horus Mastery, Summon the Beast, or Goad)

Crescent Moon - In this phase the Werewolf is generally calm and contemplative. Any attempts to force Horus get a +3 difficulty (Horus Mastery, Summon the Beast) or a bonus +3 to resist (Goad).

Half Moon - During the half moon phase Werewolves are just like anyone else. They are normal everyday people.

Gibbous Moon - The Werewolf will begin to get a little more agitated during this time period and prone to being moody and dark. When the moon is in this phase the werewolf is more susceptable to Horus and so any difficulties to change to Horus are reduced by -3 (Horus Mastery, Summon the Beast) or the defender gets a -3 penalty to resist (Goad).

Full Moon - The Werewolf's attitude has worsened to an extent that they are difficult to be around. During the full moon all social skills (Fast Talk, Theology, etc...) are reduced to half their levels because of the tense aggravation of the Werewolf. Any attempts to change to Horus are reduced by -6 (Horus Mastery, Summon the Beast) and any time they are defending against a forced change they suffer a -3 penalty to their roll to resist (Goad).

Lunar Eclipse - The Eclipse is the most dreaded times for wolves. When a Lunar Eclipse occurs all Werewolves lose their connection to the Wolf inside them. They lose the ability to use any of their Masteries and cannot shift forms. The only Masteries that they have acces to during this time is Mindspeak (Not Extended Mindspeak) and Healing. They still need Level Three Lore to be killed however.

Werewolf Special Abilities

Healing - 0 Soul to use. (no room command needed)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Effect/Description: Werewolves have an incredible natural healing ability. Any wound caused to the werewolf will heal at the rate of 1 Body per hour the character rests. If the character takes any strenuous action, the healing is disrupted and the hour starts over. A Room Host must be notified when you claim your Body point back. Wounds created by bane water cannot be healed with this mastery. Wounds from fire heal at the rate of 1 Body per day and only when the character sleeps. If the character is woken up before the day is over, the healing process is disrupted and they do not regain their point (which means they cannot role-play until the damage is healed). The damage from an attack that removes a limb heals at the normal rate, but the player must role-play the limb slowly growing back. (Length of time for the limb to regenerate is determined by the Forum Director.)

Horus Form - 0 Soul to use. (##HorusForm ##)
System/Duration: NA / Until target(s) is destroyed; takes 1 round to shape shift.
Effect/Description: Tall, muscular, and extremely strong, this raging beast is terror incarnate. The massive head of a deranged wolf sits upon a massive set of shoulders. Thick matted fur covers its body as four inch claws tip the fingers on their enormous paws. Their eyes are slits like animals, but still retain their human color. Their reasoning is that of a maddened beast; pure instinct. Impervious to logic, they seek only to destroy the target of their fury. It will go to whatever means necessary to kill the target of its rage, whether it is loved one or foe. Once the target has been killed, reason begins to fight its way back. How the Horus reacts depends on the individual it is. Some flee while others fall to their knees sobbing. While in Horus form, the werewolf gains +3 Body. Due to its enraged state, the Horus is immune to the following masteries: Terror, Hypnotic Suggestion, Mind Scrye, Illusions, and Twisted Visions. Since the Horus is unable to think rationally, it may only use the following masteries: Dulled Pain, Rending, Agile Wolf, and Terror.

Mindspeak - 0 Soul to use. (>>::target of Mindspeak:: message<<)
System/Duration: NA/NA
Effect/Description: By concentrating on a chosen target, the werewolf is capable of speaking directly to the creature's mind. Mortals are unable to return the thoughts. The target must be within view of the werewolf attempting to communicate in this fashion. Mindspeak can only be used to speak to one individual at a time.

Wolf Form - 0 Soul to use. (##WolfForm##)
System/Duration: NA / Unlimited; takes 1 round to shape shift.
Effect/Description: A werewolf who is attuned to its own inner nature is able to assume its sister form at will. They appear in many different shapes and sizes, their wolf forms often taking an appearance characteristic of their personality. They still retain their normal eye color, however. While in wolf form, they gain +1 Body.

(Just a quick note about shape changing (all forms). While a werewolf is in another form, if its current Body falls to 0, the werewolf will immediately change back to mortal form minus the damage it has already taken, leaving a minimum of 1 Body. It should also be noted that materiel objects do not shapeshift with the character. Any and all clothes and objects the character was holding will need to be transported by some other means.)
